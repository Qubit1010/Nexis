# Cold Email & Outreach Playbook

## Cold Email Fundamentals

- **Volume targets (Rule of 100):** Send 100 cold outreach messages/emails every single day. Pick one channel, hit the Rule of 100 daily for 100 days. The math dictates you will generate engaged leads.
- **Deliverability basics:**
  - Warm up new domains for 2-4 weeks before sending at volume
  - Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC authentication
  - Sending limits: Stay under 50 emails/day per mailbox; use multiple mailboxes to scale
  - Keep spam words out of subject lines (free, guaranteed, act now)
  - Clean your list: Remove bounces immediately
- **Metrics benchmarks:**
  - Open rate: 50%+ (if below, fix subject lines and deliverability)
  - Reply rate: 5-10% (if below, fix body copy and CTA)
  - Meeting rate: 1-3% of total sends
  - Close rate from meeting: 20-30%
- **Core principle:** You do not have a sales problem. You have a lead problem. Cold outreach is highly controllable -- volume is the lever.

## Email Sequence Structure

### Email 1: Initial Outreach

- **Subject line:** Short, curiosity-based, personalized. Reference their company or a specific detail.
- **Opening line:** Personalized. Never "I hope this finds you well." Reference something specific about them.
- **Value proposition:** Dream outcome, not deliverable. Sell the result they want, not the tool you use.
- **CTA:** Micro-commitment. Not "book a call." Ask permission to send something free.

**Template:**

```
Subject: Quick question about [COMPANY]'s workflows / [X] hours saved

Hey [FIRST_NAME],

I run NexusPoint, an automation agency. I'm putting together [NUMBER]
custom workflow teardowns this week for a few [INDUSTRY] founders,
showing them exactly how to automate their most annoying operational
bottleneck using tools like n8n and Make.com.

I map out the exact architecture for free in a 5-minute video. No
sales pitch, just the blueprint.

Are you open to me doing one for [COMPANY]? If so, mind if I send
over a quick 3-question link so I know what bottleneck to look at?

Best,
Aleem
```

**Why this works:**
1. Subject line is casual and references their company (not a mass blast)
2. "No sales pitch, just the blueprint" lowers their guard
3. CTA is a micro-commitment ("mind if I send a link?") -- much easier than "book a call"

### Email 2: Follow-up (Day 3-4)

- New angle, not "just following up"
- Add social proof or a specific result
- Keep it shorter than Email 1

**Template:**

```
Subject: Re: Quick question about [COMPANY]'s workflows

Hey [FIRST_NAME],

Wanted to share a quick example. Last week I mapped out an automation
for a [SIMILAR_INDUSTRY] agency that was spending [X] hours/week
manually [SPECIFIC_TASK]. The teardown showed them how to cut that
to near-zero with a simple n8n workflow.

Still happy to do the same for [COMPANY] if you're open to it.
Worth a look?

Aleem
```

### Email 3: Value Add (Day 7-8)

- Lead with free value: a mini insight, observation, or resource
- Show you have actually looked at their business

**Template:**

```
Subject: Noticed something on [COMPANY]'s site

Hey [FIRST_NAME],

I took a quick look at [COMPANY]'s [website/process/tool stack] and
noticed [SPECIFIC_OBSERVATION -- e.g., "your lead capture form
doesn't connect to your CRM automatically" or "your onboarding
flow has 3 manual handoffs that could be automated"].

Not trying to sell you anything. Just thought it was worth flagging
since it's probably costing your team [X] hours a week.

If you want, I can record a quick Loom showing the fix. Takes me
5 minutes.

Aleem
```

### Email 4: Breakup (Day 14)

- Permission-based close
- Triggers loss aversion (Never Split the Difference "Have you given up?" technique)

**Template:**

```
Subject: Should I close your file?

Hey [FIRST_NAME],

I haven't heard back, so I'm guessing the timing isn't right or
this isn't a priority for [COMPANY] right now.

No hard feelings either way. If you ever want a fresh set of eyes
on your workflows, the offer stands.

Should I close your file, or is this something you'd want to
revisit down the line?

Aleem
```

## LinkedIn DM Playbook

### Connection Request

Keep it short. No pitch in the connection note.

**Template:**

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME], saw your work with [COMPANY/POST_TOPIC].
Would love to connect.
```

### DM Sequence

**Message 1** (after connection accepted -- same day or next day):

```
Hey [FIRST_NAME], quick question for you.
```

Wait for them to reply "Sure" or "What's up?" -- this gets permission to pitch.

**Message 2** (after they reply):

```
I run an AI and automation agency called NexusPoint. I'm doing
free workflow teardowns this week where I find a bottleneck
costing your team time and record a Loom video showing the exact
logic to automate it.

You can hand the video to your devs to build, or just keep it
for later.

Want me to send over the link to drop your bottleneck in?
```

**Why this works:** Breaking into two messages acts as a pattern interrupt. "Quick question" gets a reply first. "You can hand the video to your devs" removes the fear of a hard sell.

**Message 3** (if no reply after 3-5 days):

```
No worries if the timing's off. Just figured it might save your
team a few hours. The offer's open whenever.
```

## Subject Line Swipe File

2. `[X] hours saved`
3. `Noticed something on [COMPANY]'s site`
4. `Should I close your file?`
5. `[FIRST_NAME], quick question`
6. `Idea for [COMPANY]`
7. `[MUTUAL_CONNECTION] suggested I reach out`
8. `[INDUSTRY] automation question`
9. `Saw [COMPANY]'s growth -- had a thought`
10. `This might not be for you`
11. `[COMPETITOR] is automating this already`
12. `The [PAIN_POINT] problem`

**Rules:** Keep under 6 words when possible. Lowercase looks more personal. Never use ALL CAPS or exclamation marks.

## Personalization at Scale

### Variables to Research Per Lead (2-3 min max)
- `[FIRST_NAME]` -- first name
- `[COMPANY]` -- company name
- `[INDUSTRY]` -- their niche/vertical
- `[PAIN_POINT]` -- specific manual process or bottleneck (check their job postings, LinkedIn posts, or website for clues)
- `[SPECIFIC_OBSERVATION]` -- something you noticed about their site, stack, or process
- `[SIMILAR_INDUSTRY]` -- comparable company for social proof
- `[COMPETITOR]` -- a known competitor (use carefully)

### Tools for Enrichment
- Apollo, Hunter.io, or Snov.io for email finding
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator for targeting
- BuiltWith or Wappalyzer to check their tech stack
- Their job postings (reveals what they are struggling with)

### Merge Tag Strategy
- Minimum 2 personalized variables per email (name + company is table stakes)
- Best practice: 3 variables (name + company + specific observation)
- Never send an email where removing the personalization makes it read identically

## Tactical Empathy in Outreach

### Using Labels in Email Copy
- "It seems like [COMPANY] is scaling fast right now" (opener)
- "It sounds like your team is spending a lot of time on [MANUAL_TASK]" (problem identification)
- "It looks like you've already tried [THING] but it didn't fully solve the problem" (shows you've done research)

### Accusation Audit in Cold Openers
- "I know you probably get dozens of emails like this..."
- "You're probably thinking this is just another agency pitch..."
- "I'm guessing your inbox is already overflowing, so I'll keep this short..."
- Pre-empts their objection and makes them more receptive

### Mirroring in Follow-ups
- Reference their exact words from a previous reply
- "You mentioned [EXACT_PHRASE] -- I wanted to follow up on that"
- If they said "we're not ready," follow up with: "You mentioned you're not ready yet. It sounds like timing is the main factor. When would make sense to revisit?"

### No-Oriented Questions in CTAs
- "Would it be a terrible idea if I sent over a quick example?"
- "Is this completely off base for [COMPANY]?"
- "Have you given up on automating [PROCESS]?"

## The Lead Magnet That Drives This System

**The "100-Hour Reclaim" Audit** -- a free custom Loom video reviewing one of their workflows.

- Prospect fills out a 3-question form (biggest bottleneck, current tools, email)
- You record a 5-min Loom: map their current messy process, then show the automated architecture
- Give away the strategy completely for free
- Close the video with: "You can hand this to your devs, or if you want it done right the first time, NexusPoint can build, test, and deploy it by [DATE]. Let me know if you want to chat about implementation."

This turns free intellectual capital into high-ticket clients. The strategy is free; they pay for the build.
