# Copywriting Principles — NexusPoint Outreach

Distilled from the cold outreach copywriting course (10+ years, $15M+ in outbound sales).
These rules apply to all outreach: Instagram DMs, LinkedIn notes, cold emails.

---

## Core Philosophy

**Be genuinely curious, not a pushy salesman.** Start conversations, don't pitch.
Give value before asking for anything. The goal of Touch 1 is a reply — not a sale.

---

## The 4-Step Framework (every message)

1. **Personalization** — Hook attention. 1-2 sentences, informal, rooted in something observable.
2. **Who am I?** — Brief credibility. 1 sentence. Only when relevant (Touch 2+).
3. **Offer** — Specific, quantified, low-friction. Never in Touch 1.
4. **CTA** — One specific ask. Never vague. Use no-oriented questions (Voss).

---

## Personalization Rules (Touch 1)

**Cold reading** — Make observations that feel personal but are rooted in observable facts:
role, company size, industry, growth stage, website tech, pain signals from their content.

**DO:**
- "It looks like you're running a lean [N]-person team at [Company] — that usually means..."
- "It sounds like [Company] is at that stage where the ops overhead starts compounding..."
- "It seems like you're betting on the [specific model] approach — most [industry] founders I know..."

**DON'T:**
- "I love what you're doing!" (fake compliment)
- "Your content is amazing!" (generic praise)
- "I came across your profile and was impressed..." (AI-sounding opener)
- Elaborate flattery before you've earned it

**Mirror their tone.** If their bio/posts are casual — be casual. If they're formal — be professional.
Match their message length style. Never write an essay for someone who tweets in fragments.

---

## Psychological Principles (apply naturally)

| Principle | How to Use |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Give First** | Insights, free teardowns, useful observations — before any ask |
| **Micro Commitments** | Small yes first (reply to a question), then escalate gradually over touches |
| **Social Proof** | Specific, not vague. "12-person [industry] team cut reporting by 80%" not "a client" |
| **Authority** | Brief credentials relevant to their niche. Don't overstate. |
| **Shared Identity** | Reference the founder/operator journey they're on — shared struggles build rapport |
| **Scarcity** | Only use when genuine (limited slots, real deadlines) — never fake urgency |

---

## Offer Formula: CVR = (ROI × Trust) / Friction

- **ROI**: Quantified, time-bound. "20 meetings in 60 days" not "more pipeline"
- **Trust**: Social proof + shared identity + give-first track record
- **Friction**: Minimize — free trials, no upfront cost, Loom instead of a call

---

## CTAs (Touch 3+)

- **Specific**: "Would it be completely off base to do a quick 20-min call?" (Voss no-oriented)
- **Never vague**: "Let me know your thoughts" / "Would love to chat" = no response
- **Propose times** when asking for meetings — reduces back-and-forth friction

---

## Platform Rules

### Instagram
- Casual, peer-to-peer. Like a DM from someone you'd actually follow.
- Under 300 characters. Long openers get ignored.
- Touch 1: Voss label + one genuine question. Zero pitch. Zero NexusPoint.
- Build pre-engagement first (like posts, comment, story reply) so they recognize your name.

### LinkedIn
- Professional but personal. Credible, not corporate.
- Connection note: 300-char hard limit. Reference something specific. End naturally — no CTA.
- Sound like someone who actually read their profile for 30 seconds, not a template.

### Cold Email
- Personal but professional. Informal greeting ("Hi [Name]"), short paragraphs.
- Subject line: creates curiosity or plausible deniability — does NOT sell.
- Give-first offer leads (free Loom teardown) — the ask comes after value.
- Follow-up 2 only if the sequence is strong. Don't spam.

---

## Sequence Structure (all channels)

| Touch | When | Goal | Pitch? | CTA? |
|-------|------|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Start conversation | Never | Genuine question only |
| 2 | Day 3-4 | Build trust | No | "No reply needed" |
| 3 | Day 8-10 | Soft ask | Brief intro | No-oriented question |
| 4 | Day 15-16 | Close loop | Light reference | "Should I close your file?" |

---

## What to Avoid (always)

- Fake AI compliments ("Your work is truly inspiring!")
- Generic openers ("I came across your profile...")
- Pitching in Touch 1 on any platform
- Vague CTAs ("Let me know!")
- Long messages when the platform calls for short
- Claiming results you don't have
- Fake urgency or scarcity
