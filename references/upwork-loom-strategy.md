# Upwork Loom Video Strategy

## The Core Answer: Hybrid, Not One-or-the-Other

Don't record a personalized Loom for every job — it burns 2-4 hours daily at scale. Don't send a fully generic Loom — it wastes the one thing that makes video powerful on Upwork: the perception you watched their post carefully enough to make something just for them.

**Tier by job score:**

| Score | Loom Type | Time Cost |
|---|---|---|
| 70+ (Green) | Personalized, 60-90 sec — record fresh per job | 20-30 min |
| 40-69 (Yellow) | Semi-generic base + spoken personalized intro line at top | 3-5 min |
| Below 40 (Red) | No Loom — proposal text carries it | 0 min |

**Operational setup:** Record all 3 base Looms in one 90-minute session. Store the URLs in a snippet manager. Base Looms are a one-time investment that pays forward indefinitely.

---

## Where the Loom Goes in the Proposal

Embed in **paragraph 2**, not at the bottom as a CTA. Clients skim proposals in 8-12 seconds. If the link is buried after 4 paragraphs, most never see it.

**Revised proposal structure:**

> **Para 1** — Voss label opener (unchanged)
>
> **Para 2** — What you'd build (2 sentences), then: *"I put together a 60-second screen share walking through exactly how I'd approach this — [quick walkthrough here] — so you can see the thinking before we talk."*
>
> **Para 3** — Proof stat (unchanged)
>
> **Para 4** — Remove the "Mind if I send a Loom..." CTA since you already sent it. Replace with: *"If the walkthrough resonates, I'm ready to move quickly. Happy to answer any questions before we get on a call — or we can skip straight to scoping."*

**Link text:** Use "quick walkthrough here" or "I sketched it out here" — not "watch my Loom." This frames the video as made specifically for them, not a marketing asset you blast to everyone.

**For Green jobs:** Set the Loom thumbnail to show the client's company/project name. Title the Loom file: "[Client Name] — NexusPoint Walkthrough"

## First Impression Rules (apply to ALL Looms)

1. **Never open with your name first.** "Hey I'm Aleem" is how every other freelancer starts. Open with their problem or a pattern interrupt — then introduce yourself.
2. **First 5 seconds = hook, not hello.** The viewer decides to keep watching in the first sentence.
3. **Outcome language, not feature language.** "I eliminate manual handoffs" beats "I build automations."
4. **Specificity creates trust.** One specific reference to THEIR post > three generic claims.
5. **After the hook, introduce in one sentence — make it adaptive, not generic.** Tie the credential to their specific problem or stack. Examples below.

### Opener Variants (pick by situation)

**Variant A — Problem-First** (strongest for specific job posts)
> "[CLIENT NAME], most proposals you're seeing right now are going to describe what they'd build. I recorded this to show you."

**Variant B — Outcome-First** (when the result is the most compelling thing)
> "In the next 60 seconds I'm going to show you exactly how I'd approach this — not a pitch, an actual walkthrough of the build."

**Variant C — Shared-Pain** (best for automation/AI jobs)
> "The reason this kind of project usually fails isn't the tech — it's that no one maps the edge cases before they start building. Here's how I handle that."

**Variant D — Direct Specificity** (Green/personalized jobs)
> "[CLIENT NAME] — your post mentioned [SPECIFIC DETAIL]. That told me exactly where the friction is. Let me show you what I'd do."

## Script: Personalized Loom (Green Jobs — 70+ Score)

**Runtime: 60-90 seconds**
**Structure: Hook / Brief intro / Label / Approach (screen share) / Proof / Soft CTA**

---

**[0:00-0:08 — Face on webcam — pick one opener above]**

Variant A: "[CLIENT NAME], most proposals you're seeing right now are going to describe what they'd build. I recorded this to show you."
Variant D: "[CLIENT NAME] — your post mentioned [SPECIFIC DETAIL]. That told me exactly where the friction is. Let me show you what I'd do."

**[0:08-0:12 — Still face]**

Adaptive intro — pick the form that fits the job:
- Web Dev: "I'm Aleem — I've shipped 90+ sites, including [Framer/Webflow/Next.js] builds like this."
- Automation: "I'm Aleem — I've eliminated this exact bottleneck for [SaaS ops teams / e-commerce brands / agencies] before."
- AI: "I'm Aleem — I've built production AI systems handling [their use case] for real businesses."
- Fallback: "I'm Aleem — founder of NexusPoint. I've solved this exact problem before."

---

**[0:10-0:25 — Face, then switch to screen share]**

"It sounds like the real challenge here isn't just [SURFACE-LEVEL PROBLEM] — it's that [DEEPER FRICTION, e.g., 'every new client creates a manual process someone has to babysit, and that's eating time you don't have']. That's exactly the kind of problem I've been solving."

---

**[0:25-0:55 — Screen share: show relevant prior work, diagram, or sketched approach]**

"Here's how I'd structure this. [NARRATE WHAT'S ON SCREEN — 15-20 seconds]. For your situation specifically, I'd [STEP 1, e.g., 'start by mapping where data is getting lost between your form and your CRM']. Then [STEP 2]. The stack I'd use is [TOOL 1] and [TOOL 2] because [ONE REASON TIED TO THEIR CONTEXT]."

---

**[0:55-1:10 — Screen or return to face]**

"I did something similar for [VAGUE CLIENT TYPE, e.g., 'a consulting firm in the US'] — [RESULT STAT, e.g., 'cut their manual onboarding work by 70%, 4 hours per client down to 20 minutes']. I can show you exactly how that was built if it's relevant."

---

**[1:10-1:20 — Face on webcam]**

"I don't want to assume this is the right fit — but if what I walked through sounds directionally right, I'm happy to send a more detailed breakdown before we ever get on a call. No pressure either way."

---

**Production notes:**
- Record in one or two takes. Imperfect delivery reads as real.
- Show something on screen during Approach — a Make.com template, Framer editor, Notion doc with their process sketched, etc. Never talk to camera for the full video.
- Set custom Loom thumbnail with their company name visible if possible.

You get documentation, a test log, and a handoff session."

## Discovery Calls & Interviews — First Impression Toolkit

### "Tell Me About Yourself" — Three Versions

Most people answer chronologically. Wrong move. Answer with value: **what you solve → who for → proof → why now.**

**Version 1 — Client-facing (discovery call)**
> "I help startups and growing businesses stop losing hours to manual operations — by building AI automation and web systems that run without someone babysitting them. I've done it for 90+ clients, mostly through Upwork where I'm Top Rated. Now I'm building NexusPoint as an independent agency to do the same thing at a higher level."

**Version 2 — General / interview**
> "I'm a founder and AI builder. I run NexusPoint — we take businesses that are growing faster than their operations can handle, and build the systems that let them scale without hiring more people. Full-stack web, AI automation, the whole stack. I'm also finishing my BS in AI at Iqra University, which means I'm building this in real-time — not just theorizing."

**Version 3 — Short / conversational**
> "I build AI systems and web infrastructure for businesses that are scaling. Think: replacing the manual stuff that's eating your team's time. Done it 90+ times on Upwork, now doing it direct."

---

### The "What Do You Do?" One-Liner

**Default:** "I help businesses automate the manual work that's slowing them down — AI systems, web builds, the whole stack. Founder of NexusPoint."

**AI-forward:** "I build AI automation systems — the kind that replace 4 hours of manual work per day. Also build the web infrastructure around it."

**Web-forward:** "I build websites and web apps that actually convert — and automate the backend operations so they run themselves."

---

### What NOT to Say (ever)

- "I'd love to help you with this" — sounds desperate
- "I have X years of experience" — credentials without context
- "I'm a full-stack developer" — commodity framing
- "I'm passionate about..." — meaningless filler
- "So basically what I do is..." — weak opener
- Listing your tech stack before stating what problem you solve
