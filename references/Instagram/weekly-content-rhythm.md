# Weekly Content Rhythm — NexusPoint Instagram

The goal: make posting feel like maintenance, not creation.
Everything batched, nothing improvised on the day.

---

## Core Principle: Separate Creation from Publishing

Creation happens once a week in focused blocks.
Publishing happens daily in under 5 minutes.

Most people fail at consistency because they try to create and publish on the same day. Wrong model.

---

## Monday — Writing Day (2 hours)

Everything downstream depends on what gets written here.

### Hour 1 — Ideation + selection (30 min)

Run the content engine. Pick 3 carousel ideas and 1 Reel idea. Filters:
- Does it speak directly to ops leads or SaaS founders?
- Does it fit Proof, Education, or BTS pillar?
- Can it be said in 8 slides or 45 seconds?

Standing fallback list if engine doesn't produce:
```
Proof:     Pick a result from the results bank → write the case study
Education: Pick a pain point from last 3 prospect conversations → break it down
BTS:       What did you build or learn this week? Write that.
```

### Hour 1 (remaining 30 min) — Write all 3 carousel captions

Write the caption first, not the slides. Caption = the argument. Slides = visual expression of it.

For each carousel write:
1. Hook (first line — becomes slide 1 text)
2. Body (points 2-6 — one sentence each, become slide headlines)
3. Payoff (the principle or takeaway)
4. CTA

~150-200 words per carousel. Three carousels = 30-45 min if you don't stop to design.

### Hour 2 — Reel script + caption

Pick the format (screen recording / talking head / before-after). Write:
- Hook line (said or displayed in first 1.5 sec)
- 3-4 points (bullet form — spoken or shown as text overlays)
- Closing line

Also write the Reel caption: 80-100 words, one hook, one CTA.

**End of Monday:** 3 carousel scripts + 1 Reel script. Nothing designed yet.

---

## Tuesday — Design Day (1 hour)

Pass everything to Areeba. Don't design it yourself.

**Handoff message format:**
```
Areeba — 3 carousels for this week:

Carousel 1: [Title]
Slide 1: [Hook text]
Slide 2: [Point 1]
...
Slide 8: [CTA]
Notes: [design direction — color accent, diagram of X, etc.]

Carousel 2: [Title]
...

Carousel 3: [Title]
...

Same NexusPoint template. Slide numbers in corner. Need by Thursday.
```

If doing a screen recording Reel — record it Tuesday after the handoff (20 min):
- Open n8n / Claude Code / whatever you're showcasing
- Record with OBS
- Save raw file — CapCut edit happens Wednesday

---

## Wednesday — Reel Production (30 min)

**Screen recording Reel:**
- Import raw footage into CapCut
- Auto-captions (one click)
- Add text overlays for hook and key points
- Export 1080x1920

**Talking head Reel:**
- Film in one take. Literally one take.
- CapCut → auto-captions → trim dead air
- Export

**Before/after Reel:**
- Capture "before" (screenshot or recording of manual process)
- Capture "after" (automation running)
- Stitch in CapCut with transition
- Text overlays

**Also Wednesday:** Write this week's 5 Story poll ideas (10 min). Keep in a notes doc to build a backlog.

---

## Thursday — Review + Schedule (30 min)

Areeba delivers the 3 carousels. Review each:
- Does slide 1 stop the scroll?
- Is text readable on mobile?
- Slide numbers present?
- Last slide has CTA?

**Schedule everything for the week:**

| Post | Day | Time |
|------|-----|------|
| Carousel 1 | Monday | 7pm PKT |
| Carousel 2 | Wednesday | 7pm PKT |
| Carousel 3 or Reel | Friday | 7pm PKT |

Use Instagram's native scheduling (clock icon before posting). Set and forget.

Also Thursday: flag topic ideas for next Monday's content engine run so ideation is faster.

---

## Friday — Reel Live + Weekly Story Audit (15 min)

Post Reel if not scheduled.

**Weekly Story audit (10 min):**
- Which poll this week got the most responses?
- DM everyone who voted the high-pain option — warmest leads of the week
- What Story type got replies? Double down on that format next week

---

## Daily — Stories (10 min/day)

Can't be batched — feel best when timely.

| Day | Story type |
|-----|-----------|
| Monday | Poll — ties to the carousel dropping that day |
| Tuesday | BTS clip — something you built or worked on |
| Wednesday | Teaser for Wednesday carousel |
| Thursday | Question box — "what's your biggest ops headache right now?" |
| Friday | Repost of the week's best-performing post |
| Saturday | Casual — tool you're using, thought you had, something real |
| Sunday | "This week I'm working on..." — sets up Monday's content |

No design needed for Stories — native Instagram tools (polls, questions, text) outperform
over-designed graphics anyway.

---

## Daily Engagement Block (15 min/day — separate from production)

Not content creation — distribution. Do it every day before posting anything.

- Comment on 10 posts from ICP (SaaS founders, ops leads, COOs) — substantive, not "great post"
- Reply to every comment on your own posts within 24 hours
- Reply to every Story response immediately — these are the warmest signals

Commenting in the morning. Replies whenever notifications come in.

## Full Week at a Glance

| Day | Time | What gets done |
|-----|------|----------------|
| Monday | 2 hrs | Content engine run, write 3 carousel scripts + 1 Reel script |
| Tuesday | 1 hr | Handoff to Areeba, record Reel footage |
| Wednesday | 30 min | Edit Reel in CapCut, write Story poll backlog |
| Thursday | 30 min | Review carousels, schedule week's posts |
| Friday | 15 min | Reel live, Story audit, DM poll respondents |
| Daily | 25 min | 10 min Stories + 15 min engagement |

**Total: ~5-6 hours/week** including daily engagement.
Creation work is 4 hours, batched Monday-Thursday. Friday and weekend are maintenance.

---

## The Compounding Timeline

| Weeks | What's happening |
|-------|-----------------|
| 1-4 | Building the habit and backlog. Engagement is low. Normal. |
| 5-8 | Algorithm recognizes consistent posting. Reel reach climbs. Story poll responses pick up. |
| 9-12 | Inbound DMs start. Followers who've been watching for 8 weeks warm up and reach out. Polls convert to conversations. Conversations convert to discovery calls. |

The system pays off in week 9, not week 1.
Most people quit in week 3.
That's the only difference between accounts that grow and accounts that stall.
