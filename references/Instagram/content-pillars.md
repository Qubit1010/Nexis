# Instagram Content Pillars — NexusPoint

Three pillars, each doing a different job in the conversion funnel.
Ratio: Proof 40% / Education 40% / Behind the Scenes 20%.

---

## Pillar 1 — Proof (40%)

**Job:** Converts skeptics. Answers "has this person actually done this?" before they have to ask.

**Formula:** Situation → Problem → What we built → The result → What it unlocked

### Carousel slide structure
- Slide 1: Bold hook (the result) — stop the scroll
- Slide 2: The situation before — make the reader feel the pain
- Slide 3: What the problem actually was (root cause, not symptom)
- Slide 4: What we built — simple diagram or tool stack
- Slide 5: The result — number-forward, specific
- Slide 6: What this unlocked for the team + CTA

### Post ideas

| Post idea | Format | Hook |
|-----------|--------|------|
| 70% onboarding reduction case study | Carousel (6 slides) | "A SaaS founder was spending 15 hrs/week manually onboarding clients. Here's the system we built to eliminate it." |
| Lead pipeline with 80% less follow-up | Carousel | "3 people were manually chasing leads every day. We removed all of them from the process." |
| Hours-to-minutes CRM response | Before/after Reel | "Their team took 4 hours to respond to inbound leads. We got it to under 3 minutes." |
| Client DM screenshot + context | Single image | Screenshot of client saying "this saved us X hours" + 2-sentence context |
| 90 projects delivered milestone | Graphic + caption | "90 projects. Here's the 3 things I learned that most automation agencies get wrong." |

### The one rule
Never generalize. "We helped a SaaS team" is weaker than "A 28-person HR tech SaaS in London."
Specificity is proof. Vague is sus.

## Pillar 2 — Education (40%)

**Job:** Builds authority and gets saves. Saves = highest-signal engagement on Instagram.
Algorithm treats saves as "worth returning to." Education gets saved. Opinions get liked. Proof gets DMs.

**Formula:** Name the pain → explain why it happens → show the pattern → give the insight → CTA

### Sub-pillar A — Ops pain posts (speaks directly to ICP)

| Post idea | Hook |
|-----------|------|
| 5 workflows killing your ops team's week | "If your team is doing any of these 5 things manually, you're bleeding 10+ hours a week." |
| What breaks first at 25 employees | "Every company hits a wall around 25 people. It's not a hiring problem. It's a systems problem." |
| The hidden cost of manual follow-up | "Most founders count hours. Nobody counts the deals lost while someone was manually following up." |
| Why your CRM is always out of date | "Your CRM isn't dirty because your team is lazy. It's dirty because updating it requires 3 steps nobody has time for." |
| Signs your ops are costing you clients | "You're losing clients before they even sign. Here's how to tell." |

### Sub-pillar B — AI automation demystified (positions NexusPoint as expert)

| Post idea | Hook |
|-----------|------|
| What "AI automation" actually means | "Everyone's selling AI. Here's what it actually looks like inside a real ops workflow." |
| n8n vs Make vs Zapier — when to use what | "3 tools, 3 different use cases. Most people pick the wrong one." |
| What you can automate in a week vs a month | "Not everything takes 3 months. Here's what we can build in a week." |
| The 3 automations with the fastest ROI | "If you only automate 3 things this quarter, make it these." |
| What AI agents actually do vs chatbots | "A chatbot answers questions. An agent makes decisions. Here's the difference." |

### Sub-pillar C — Founder perspective (builds personal brand)

| Post idea | Hook |
|-----------|------|
| What I learned from 90 projects | "90 projects in. The thing that kills most automation projects isn't the tech." |
| Why we say no to some clients | "We turned down 3 projects last month. Here's why." |
| The mistake every first automation makes | "The first automation I ever built was technically correct and completely useless." |

### Education carousel format
- Slide 1: Hook — bold claim or number
- Slides 2-6: One point per slide, short headline + 1-2 sentences of context
- Slide 7: Summary or key takeaway
- Slide 8: CTA — "Save this" or "DM me AUDIT"

## Pillar 3 — Behind the Scenes (20%)

**Job:** Humanizes the brand. People hire people, not agencies. BTS makes Aleem the character
in the story — not just NexusPoint the brand.

**Formula:** Show the real thing → give one honest observation → make it relevant to the ICP

### Post ideas

| Post idea | Format | Hook |
|-----------|--------|------|
| Screen recording of building a workflow | Reel (30-45 sec) | "Built this in 2 hours. Here's what it does." |
| The LinkedIn DM dashboard built internally | Carousel or Reel | "I automated my own outreach. Here's the tool I built for it." |
| Actual desk/workspace | Single image + caption | "Where $X of client work gets built every week." |
| n8n workflow screenshot | Single image | "This workflow runs 24/7. Nobody on the team touches it." |
| The tools used every day | Carousel | "My actual stack. No sponsors, no affiliation." |
| A mistake made on a client project | Caption-only text post | "We broke a client's workflow last Tuesday. Here's what happened and what we rebuilt." |
| The briefing process before a project | Short Reel | "Before we write a single line, here's what we map out." |
| A day in the life — student + founder | Reel | "6th semester BSAI student by day. Agency founder by night. Here's what that actually looks like." |

### The student + founder angle is an asset

Most people in AI automation are either US/EU senior consultants at $500/hr or faceless freelancers.
Aleem is a 25-year-old BSAI student in Pakistan building real systems for US, UK, AU clients.
That's a genuinely interesting story. The constraint (resource pressure, time pressure, timezone gap)
is the differentiator. Own it in BTS content — it makes NexusPoint memorable.

## How the 3 pillars work together

The conversion sequence:

1. **Reel** reaches a new person (Education or BTS)
2. **Profile visit** — they see proof carousels in Featured and grid
3. **Follow** — now they see regular content
4. **Education post saved** — algorithm shows them more
5. **Story poll** triggers a warm DM
6. **DM converts** to discovery call — proof posts did the selling before the call started

Each pillar feeds the next:
- Proof without education = sales pitch
- Education without proof = theory account
- BTS without both = vlog

Together they build the trust stack that makes cold DMs warm and discovery calls short.
