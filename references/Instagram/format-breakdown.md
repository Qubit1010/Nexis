# Instagram Format Breakdown — NexusPoint

Three formats, each doing a different job. Carousels build authority, Reels grow audience, Stories convert followers into conversations.

---

## Carousels (3x/week — the workhorse)

**Why carousels win:**
Swipe-through time = watch time. Each swipe is a separate interaction. Instagram reshows carousels to people who didn't swipe the first time — they get a second chance automatically.

### Slide structure

**Slide 1 — Hook (most important)**
Competes for attention in the feed. If it doesn't stop the scroll, the other 7 don't exist.
- Bold claim, specific number, or provocative question
- Large text, minimal words (under 10)
- NexusPoint branding in corner — not center
- Color contrast that pops on a dark feed

Hook formulas:
```
"[Number] [thing] every [ICP] should [action] before [trigger]"
→ "5 workflows every ops lead should automate before scaling past 30"

"[Specific result]. Here's the exact system."
→ "Saved a SaaS team 15 hours/week. Here's the exact system."

"Most [people] [wrong belief]. [Contrarian truth]."
→ "Most agencies will sell you Zapier. That's not automation."

"Your [thing] is [costing you something]. Here's why."
→ "Your CRM is lying to you. Here's why."
```

**Slides 2-6 — Body**
One point per slide. No paragraphs.
- Headline: 5-8 words max
- Supporting text: 1-2 short sentences
- Optional: simple diagram or icon reinforcing the point
- Consistent layout across slides — same font, same positioning

**Slide 7 — Payoff**
The summary or the principle. What they screenshot and share.
- "The real lesson here is..."
- "Bottom line:"
- A single powerful statement that stands alone out of context

**Slide 8 — CTA**
One action only.
- "Save this if your ops team does any of this manually"
- "DM me 'AUDIT' — I'll tell you what's worth automating in your stack"
- "Follow for more on building ops that run without you"

### Design rules
- Consistent color palette — NexusPoint dark background + brand blue accent
- Same font across all slides
- Leave breathing room — don't fill every pixel
- Slide numbers in corner (1/8, 2/8) — increases completion rate
- First and last slide should feel like a set — same energy bookending the piece

## Reels (1x/week — the reach engine)

**Why Reels matter:**
Only format shown to non-followers. Every other format mostly reaches people who already follow you. Reels are how you grow past 3K.

### Format A — Screen recording walkthrough (easiest to make)
Show a workflow being built or running. No face required.

Structure:
- 0-3 sec: Hook text overlay — "This workflow runs 24/7 without anyone touching it"
- 3-25 sec: Screen recording — n8n canvas, Claude Code, a dashboard — with text callouts explaining each step
- 25-35 sec: The result — "Here's what it replaced" or "Here's the output"
- 35-45 sec: CTA overlay — "Want this built for your team? DM me"

Tools: OBS or built-in Windows recorder → CapCut for editing + auto-captions.

### Format B — Talking head (builds personal brand fastest)
Aleem on camera, direct to lens, 30-45 seconds.

Structure:
- 0-3 sec: Hook — say it out loud, don't just text overlay
- 3-30 sec: One insight, conversational — not scripted
- 30-45 sec: The flip — "here's what to do instead"

Good topics:
- "The first thing I automate for every client" (30 sec)
- "Why I turned down a $5K project last week" (45 sec)
- "What most founders get wrong about automation" (30 sec)

Tips:
- Film in one take, don't over-edit
- Slight imperfection builds trust
- Subtitles mandatory (85% watched muted)

### Format C — Before/after process reveal
- 0-5 sec: "Before" — messy spreadsheet, manual steps, Slack chaos
- 5-30 sec: "What we built" — the automation running
- 30-45 sec: "After" — result, time saved, person freed up

### Reel rules
- Vertical 9:16 always
- Captions/subtitles on every Reel
- Hook within first 1.5 seconds
- 30-45 seconds is the sweet spot
- Post between 6-9pm PKT (US/UK audience midday)

## Stories (daily — the conversation layer)

**Why Stories are different:**
Stories don't build audience — they convert existing followers into conversations. 3K people already follow but haven't messaged. Stories nurture them.

### Type 1 — Poll (highest DM trigger rate)
Weekly. When they vote, Instagram tells them you saw it — that's the permission to DM.

Examples:
```
"How much of your team's week goes to manual data tasks?"
[ Under 5 hrs ] [ 5-15 hrs ] [ Way too much ]

"What's the biggest ops bottleneck right now?"
[ Onboarding ] [ Follow-ups ] [ Reporting ]

"How long does it take your team to respond to inbound leads?"
[ Under 1 hour ] [ Same day ] [ Honestly... days ]
```

After vote — DM the "Way too much" / "Honestly... days" respondents within 24 hours:
> "It sounds like the [X] side of your ops is eating more than it should — what's the process that's costing the most time right now?"

### Type 2 — Teaser (drives carousel saves)
Before posting a carousel:
> "Dropping a breakdown on [topic] today. Save it before it gets buried."

Story viewers who saw the teaser will save the carousel.

### Type 3 — Behind the scenes clip (30-60 sec casual)
Something you're working on, a tool discovered, a thought while building. Raw, phone footage. Most human format.

### Type 4 — Question box
> "What's the most repetitive thing your team does every day?"

Each answer is a warm conversation. Respond to every one personally.

### Type 5 — Repost your own post
When a carousel or Reel goes live, immediately add to Story:
> "New post — save this one."

Story viewers and feed viewers are different people. Reach both.

### Story posting times
- Morning 8-10am PKT — Pakistani/South Asian audience
- Evening 7-9pm PKT — US/UK audience midday
- Post twice daily if possible

### Story design rules
- Minimal text — big, readable
- Consistent brand colors, less formal than feed
- Polls and questions look better native (Instagram's built-in tools) than over-designed
- Face in Stories builds trust faster than graphics

## Weekly production rhythm

| Block | Time | What gets done |
|-------|------|----------------|
| Monday 2hrs | Writing | Draft captions for 3 carousels + Reel script |
| Tuesday 1hr | Design | Areeba turns carousel copy into slides |
| Wednesday 30min | Reel | Film or record screen, export |
| Daily 10min | Stories | Poll, BTS clip, or teaser |

Total: 3-4 hours/week batched. The content engine generates the raw ideas — the gap is turning those ideas into the right format.
