# LinkedIn Content Strategy — NexusPoint

LinkedIn is the primary revenue channel, not just a publishing platform.
Every post warms up a prospect, builds credibility, or reaches someone in your ICP's network.
Instagram builds audience. LinkedIn builds pipeline.

---

## How LinkedIn Content Fits the Bigger Picture

The flywheel:
```
You comment on ICP's post → they check your profile
→ they see your content → they follow
→ your posts appear in their feed → they engage
→ they accept your DM → they already know who you are
→ discovery call is shorter because trust is pre-built
```

Content doesn't close deals. It makes the closes faster.

## The 4 LinkedIn Content Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Proof and Case Studies (30%)

**Job:** Converts profile visitors and DM recipients into serious prospects.
**Format:** Text post or document carousel | **Length:** 200-400 words

**Structure:**
```
Line 1: The result (hook)
[blank]
Lines 2-3: The situation before — what was broken
[blank]
Lines 4-6: What we built — specific, no jargon
[blank]
Lines 7-8: The result — number-forward
[blank]
Line 9: The lesson or principle that generalizes
[blank]
CTA: "If your team is dealing with [X], DM me"
```

**Example opening lines:**
- "A SaaS team was spending 15 hours a week onboarding clients manually. They now spend zero."
- "We cut a marketing agency's lead follow-up time by 80%. Here's the exact system."
- "90 projects in. The single most common ops problem I've seen across all of them."

**Rule:** Never name the client without permission. "A 30-person HR tech SaaS in London" is specific enough to be credible without being identifiable.

### Pillar 2 — Sharp Opinions and Hot Takes (30%)

**Job:** Gets shared. LinkedIn rewards saves and shares more than likes.
**Format:** Text post — no image needed | **Length:** 150-300 words

**Structure:**
```
Line 1: The opinion, stated boldly (the hook)
[blank]
Lines 2-4: Why the common belief is wrong
[blank]
Lines 5-7: The better mental model
[blank]
Lines 8-9: The implication for their business
[blank]
Optional CTA or open question
```

**Specific post ideas:**
- "Most agencies will sell you a Zapier workflow and call it AI. It isn't."
- "Hiring an ops manager before automating your ops is backwards."
- "Your CRM isn't dirty because your team is lazy. It's dirty because updating it requires 3 steps nobody has time for."
- "The cheapest hire you'll ever make is the automation that replaces the task you hired for."
- "Stop measuring automation ROI in hours saved. Measure it in decisions your team can now make."
- "Nobody talks about the ops wall every company hits at 25 employees. I'll talk about it."
- "AI won't replace your ops team. It'll replace ops teams that aren't using AI."

**Hot take formula:**
```
"[Common advice] is [wrong/backwards/incomplete]."
Explain why. Give the better version.
```

**Job:** Gets saved. Positions you as someone who understands the problem structurally.
**Format:** Text post with structure, or document carousel | **Length:** 300-500 words

**Structure:**
```

[blank]
```

### Pillar 4 — Building in Public (15%)

**Job:** Humanizes NexusPoint, builds personal brand, earns algorithm favor.
**Format:** Text post — conversational, no polish needed | **Length:** 100-250 words

**Specific post ideas:**

## Format Breakdown

### Text Posts (primary format)

Text posts consistently outperform image posts for reach and engagement. Algorithm reads time-on-post.

**Rules:**
- First line is everything — all they see before "see more"
- Short paragraphs — 1-3 lines max, then a blank line
- No bullet points in the opener — signals "list post," people skip
- Write like you talk, not like a press release
- End with open question or soft CTA

**First line formulas:**
```
"[Specific result]. Here's how."
"Most [people] [wrong belief]. [Contrarian truth]."
"I [did X]. Here's what I learned."
"[Number] [ICP] [problem]. Most never fix it."
"[Question your ICP asks themselves]. Here's the answer."
```

### Document Carousels

High saves because people download to refer back to. Best for frameworks and step-by-step content.

**Rules:**
- Slide 1 = the hook (same as text post first line)
- One idea per slide
- Last slide = CTA with name and contact
- NexusPoint branding consistent
- 6-10 slides maximum

### Text-Only vs Image Posts

Skip stock images — LinkedIn's algorithm has deprioritized them. If adding a visual, use a document carousel or a real screenshot (workflow, dashboard, result). Otherwise pure text performs better.

## Posting Frequency and Timing

**Minimum:** 3x per week
**Target:** 5x per week (weekdays)
**Ideal breakdown:** Mon/Wed/Fri text posts + Tue/Thu document carousels

**Best posting times (PKT):**
- 8-9am — catches early UK morning
- 12-1pm — UK lunch, US East Coast morning
- 6-7pm — US afternoon

Post at consistent times — LinkedIn rewards predictable posting patterns.

**Batch creation:** Write all 5 posts on Monday using the content engine. Schedule via LinkedIn native scheduler or Buffer. 90 minutes once a week.

## Comment Strategy — Integrated

Runs parallel to content publishing. Equally important.

**Daily: 15 minutes before posting anything**

**3 tiers:**
- **Tier 1 (5 comments):** ICP — SaaS founders, ops leads, COOs posting about ops pain, scaling, manual work
- **Tier 2 (3 comments):** Peers and thought leaders in AI/automation
- **Tier 3 (2 comments):** High-engagement connectors your ICP follows

**Comment formula — every comment does one of three things:**
1. Add a data point from your own experience
2. Sharpen their point one step further
3. Offer a contrarian nuance with reasoning

Never: "Great post!", "So true!", generic agreement.

**Warm DM trigger:**
When someone likes or replies to your comment — send the connection request or DM that day.
They know your name. Acceptance rate is significantly higher.

## What to Stop Doing

- Posting without a strong first line — if the hook doesn't earn "see more," the post doesn't exist
- Images for the sake of images — stock photos hurt reach
- Engagement bait — "Drop a 🔥 if you agree" — damages ICP credibility
- Posting about AI trends broadly — attracts enthusiasts, not buyers
- Long blocks of text with no line breaks — nobody reads walls on mobile
- Tagging people randomly — only tag if they were directly involved
