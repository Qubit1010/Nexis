## Core Rule: 3-5 Posts Per Week, Not Per Day

**Do not post 2-4x per day.** The 2026 LinkedIn algorithm (360Brew, 150B-param interest-graph AI) runs a Golden Hour test on every post:
- Post shown to **2-5% of your network** in the first 60 minutes
- Needs **5-10% engagement** in that window to advance to wider distribution
- Below 2% engagement: distribution dies — kills ~87% of posts

**Why multi-posts-per-day backfires:**
- Each post splits your audience, making it harder for any single post to clear the Golden Hour bar
- A pattern of low-engagement posts signals low quality to the algorithm, reducing baseline reach on your **entire account**
- You reach fewer people posting 4x/day with weak posts than 3x/week with strong ones

## Optimal Cadence

- **Frequency:** 3-5 posts per week (one per day max)
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Avoid:** Monday AM, Friday PM, weekends (-30-60% reach vs. Tue-Thu)

## The Lever That Matters More Than Timing

**Dwell time is the #1 ranking signal** [s186]:
- 0-3 second dwell = 1.2% engagement rate
- 61+ second dwell = 15.6% engagement rate (13x)

**Engage before you post:** Comment on 5-10 niche posts in the 15 minutes before publishing. This primes your distribution by warming the algorithm to your account's activity.

**Reply to every comment in the first 60 minutes.** A 10+ word comment carries 5-7x the weight of a like; threaded back-and-forth = 5.2x amplification.

## What Will Kill Your Reach

- External link in post body: **-60-68% reach** [s185]
- Link in first comment: also penalized in 2026 (use "comment X and I'll DM it")
- 3+ hashtags: **-29% reach** (use 1-2 max, or none)
- Editing the post in the first 10-15 minutes: resets the algorithm evaluation
- Generic AI/"bro-etry" copy: 360Brew detects and throttles it
- Engagement pods: 97% detection rate, permanent reach cut
- Mass tagging (5+ people)
