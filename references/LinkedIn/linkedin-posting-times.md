# LinkedIn Posting Times — Best Schedule for Maximum Reach

*Source: Buffer (4.8M posts), Sprout Social, Hootsuite, Hopper HQ — compiled April 2026*

---

## Best Days Ranked

1. **Wednesday** — Highest engagement, peak professional focus
2. **Tuesday / Thursday** — Excellent, close second
3. **Friday** — Good, drop-off after lunch
4. **Monday** — Lower, people are catching up on emails
5. **Saturday / Sunday** — Lowest, but usable with the right timing

## Why This Works

### 1. LinkedIn is a Professional Network
People open LinkedIn when they're in "work mode" — before meetings, during lunch, end of day. Weekends = low professional intent. Your audience is on Instagram/YouTube, not LinkedIn.

### 2. Tue–Thu Sweet Spot
- **Monday** — Catching up on emails, planning the week. LinkedIn is low priority.
- **Tue–Thu** — Peak professional focus. People are settled in, have bandwidth to read and engage.
- **Friday** — Mental checkout starts after lunch.
- **Weekends** — Lowest professional intent on the platform.

### 3. Morning + Afternoon Peaks Map to Real Routines
- **8–10 AM** — Commute or pre-work scroll before the day gets busy
- **12–1 PM** — Lunch break quick scroll
- **3–5 PM** — End-of-day wind-down, less task-focused, more browsing
- **After 7 PM** — Traffic drops sharply as people switch to entertainment platforms

### 4. The First 90 Minutes Are Critical (Algorithm)
LinkedIn doesn't show your post to everyone at once. It first shows it to a small sample (~1–5% of your network). If that sample engages (likes, comments, shares), LinkedIn amplifies it to a broader audience. If nobody engages in the first 90 minutes, it gets buried.

**Posting when your audience is actively online = more early engagement = algorithm amplification.**

### 5. Why Two Posts Too Close Together Hurts You
LinkedIn's algorithm treats each post as competing for the same audience pool. Two posts within a few hours splits attention and often suppresses the second post entirely to avoid spamming followers' feeds. The 8–12 hour gap gives each post its own independent algorithm cycle.

---

## TL;DR

Post when professionals are awake, in work mode, and not slammed with tasks. Give the algorithm enough active viewers in the first 90 minutes to do its job. Space posts far enough apart so they don't compete with each other.
