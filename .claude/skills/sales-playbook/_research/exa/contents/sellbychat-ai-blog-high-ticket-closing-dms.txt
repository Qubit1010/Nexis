## What is SPIN selling and how does it work in DMs?

SPIN selling was created by Neil Rackham for complex B2B sales. It works even better in DMs because the format rewards concise, question-driven conversations.

SPIN stands for:

| Stage | What You Ask | Purpose | |-------|-------------|---------| | Situation | "What does your coaching business look like right now?" | Understand their current state | | Problem | "What is the biggest thing holding you back from [goal]?" | Surface the pain | | Implication | "What happens if that does not change in the next 6 months?" | Amplify the urgency | | Need-Payoff | "If we could solve that, what would your business look like?" | Let them sell themselves |

The magic of SPIN in DMs is pacing. You ask one question. Wait for their answer. Then ask the next. No walls of text. No pressure. Just a conversation that reveals whether your program is the right fit.

## How do you qualify high-ticket prospects in DMs?

Qualification saves you from wasting 30-minute calls with people who cannot buy. Ask these questions naturally in the conversation:

1. Investment readiness. "Have you invested in coaching or programs before? What was the biggest one?"
2. Problem severity. "On a scale of 1-10, how urgent is solving [problem] for you right now?"
3. Decision authority. "Is this a decision you make on your own or do you need to talk to anyone else?"
4. Timeline. "When do you want to start seeing results?"
5. Budget signal. "What have you been investing in your business growth recently?"

Never ask "can you afford it?" Let their answers reveal budget naturally. Learn more about qualifying leads in Instagram DMs and effective lead qualification frameworks.

## What objections come up in high-ticket DM sales?

High-ticket objections are deeper than "it's too expensive." They are about risk, trust, and fear.

### "I need to talk to my partner"

This is real sometimes. Ask: "What do you think they will want to know? I can help you answer their questions."

### "I've invested in coaching before and it didn't work"

Acknowledge the pain. Ask what was different. Explain how your approach addresses the specific failure they experienced.

### "Can I see some results first?"

Share a specific case study. Not a generic testimonial. A result that mirrors their situation. "We worked with a [similar niche] coach who went from [X] to [Y] in [timeframe]."

### "I want to wait until next month"

Apply loss aversion: "What would change between now and next month? Every week you wait, you are losing [specific amount or outcome]."

### "That's a lot of money"

Reframe against the cost of inaction. "What is it costing you every month to stay where you are? Over a year, that adds up to [larger number]."
