# Worked Example — LinkedIn Cold DM to Closed Client

> A full end-to-end walkthrough of one prospect: cold connection note → live conversation → booked call → 30-min Ops Teardown discovery call → signed contract. Uses real opener archetypes (`frameworks/opener-archetypes.md`), the live conversation flow (`scripts/live-conversation-playbook.md`), the discovery script (`scripts/discovery-call-script.md`), and real proof-bank entries (`offer/proof-bank.md` — Steve/Andrey/Mikey).
>
> This is a teaching reference, not a template to copy verbatim. The point is to see how the pieces connect.

---

## The Prospect

**Sarah Lin** — Founder of **Loop & Co**, a 9-person social media agency (Toronto). Trigger event: posted last week about landing 3 new retainer clients and "drowning in content ops." Stack visible from her content: ChatGPT + Later + Notion + a VA team. Textbook NexusPoint fit — maps directly to the Steve/Andrey/Mikey proof bank.

---

## STAGE 1 — The Cold DM (LinkedIn connection note)

**Archetype 2: Specific Signal + Named Peer.** She has a trigger (3 new retainers) AND a matching peer (Steve — same niche). Under 280 chars.

> Sarah, saw the 3 new retainers post, congrats. Teams hitting that point in social usually start drowning in content + posting ops fast. Built an AI content + posting workflow for Steve (agency owner) that took 10+ hrs/week off his team. Worth comparing notes?

*Why this and not the burned skeleton:* names a real trigger, ties it to a specific pain, drops a named peer with a number, asks "worth comparing notes?" instead of "worth a quick chat?". No pitch, no calendar link, no em-dash.

She accepts and replies 2 days later:

> Ha, "drowning" is right. What did you build for Steve exactly?

---

## STAGE 2 — The Middle Conversation (live playbook, 6 phases)

She's asking a real question. **Do not dump the pitch.** Run the phases.

**Phase 1 — Qualify** (fire or curiosity?)

> For Steve it was an AI pipeline that drafts the social copy and auto-schedules across channels, so his team stopped doing it by hand. Before I tell you if it'd even port to your setup though, what's the part of your week right now that's eating the most time, the content creation or the posting/scheduling side?

> Honestly both, but the worst is my team manually rewriting each post for every platform. Eats their whole week.

**Phase 2 — Label, then silence**

> Sounds like that manual rewrite step is quietly becoming the ceiling on how many clients you can actually take on.

*(Send. Say nothing else.)*

> Exactly that. We just turned away a client because we literally couldn't staff the content load.

*(Floodgate. She just revealed growth is capped — bigger pain than time.)*

**Phase 3 — Deepen (quantify the cost in her words)**

> Rough math, how many hours a week does the team sink into that rewrite + scheduling cycle?

> Across 3 people? Probably 25-30 hours a week combined.

> And the client you turned away, what was that retainer worth monthly?

> Would've been $4k/month.

*(She's named both numbers. ~30 hrs/week AND $4k/mo in turned-away revenue. You haven't pitched once.)*

**Phase 4 — Proof (only now, because she's pulling)**

> Yeah, this is the exact pattern. Built it for Steve (marketing agency, same content + posting bottleneck). Cut 10+ hrs/week off his team, he's a repeat client now. I've done the same wedge for two other agency owners this year. Want me to send a 60-sec Loom of the actual build so you can see if it'd fit your stack?

> Yes please.

**Phase 6 — Warm Ask → Call** (anchored to a deliverable, not "a chat")

> Sending it over. While I build it out for your setup, want a 20-min Ops Teardown? I screen-share, look at your Later + Notion + ChatGPT flow, and tell you the exact first thing I'd automate. You decide if it's worth building. Tuesday 2pm or Thursday 11am work on my end, what about you?

> Thursday 11am works.

*Booked.* No objection branch was needed (she was warm). If she'd asked "what's the price?" early: *"Depends what you'd actually need, which is why the Teardown is free, I show you the build then we talk numbers if it makes sense."*

**Then send the pre-call form** with the calendar confirmation (5 diagnostic questions). You walk in with hypothesis-mode authority.

## STAGE 3 — The Discovery Call (30-min Ops Teardown)

### Act I — Up-Front Contract (0–3 min)

> Thanks for jumping on, Sarah. Let's stick to the 30 min you blocked. Is now a bad time to focus on this?

> No, good now.

> Cool. Here's how this works. I went through your form, got a picture of where the manual stuff is hitting you. First 15 min I'll ask sharp questions to pin down THE actual bottleneck, not just symptoms. Then I'll show you the exact first automation I'd build, how long it takes, what it costs. Engagements run $2k to $6k depending on scope. If by the end we both feel it's a fit, we talk next steps. If not, let's say so cleanly. Does that seem like a bad idea?

> No, that sounds great.

*(Price anchored in Act I — no shock later. Two no-oriented questions already asked.)*

### Act II — Sandler Pain Funnel (3–18 min)

Walk the 8 questions in order, labeling between each.

> Your form said the platform-rewrite step is the worst. Tell me more about that.

> Every post has to be reformatted for IG, LinkedIn, TikTok, different lengths, hooks, hashtags...

> Can you give me a specific example, like last week?

> We had a client campaign, 12 posts, times 4 platforms. That's 48 manual variations my team wrote by hand.

> *Label:* Sounds like the work scales with every client you add, so growth actually makes the problem worse, not better.

> ...yeah. I never thought about it like that but yeah.

> How long has it been this way?

> Since we crossed ~6 clients. About a year.

> What have you tried?

> We tried templates in Notion, hired a VA.

> Did that work?

> Templates still need heavy editing. The VA quit, too repetitive.

> *Label:* Seems like every fix so far has added friction instead of removing it.

> Pretty much.

> You said ~30 hours a week across the team. What do you think that's actually costing you when you add the turned-away revenue?

> Honestly? Between wages on busywork and the $4k client we couldn't take, probably $7-8k a month in real terms.

> *Label:* So every month this runs, it's basically costing you a headcount's worth of money. How do you feel about that?

> Frustrated. It's the thing stopping us from scaling.

> Have you given up trying to fix it?

> No. I just don't have time to figure out the tech side.

*(She has now quantified $7-8k/mo herself, admitted past fixes failed, confirmed emotional weight, confirmed she hasn't given up. The close is half-built.)*

### Act III — Pivot + Trust-in-Method (18–23 min)

**Need-Payoff (let her sell herself):**

> If we took that whole rewrite-and-schedule cycle off your team's plate completely, what would that change for the business?

> We could take on the clients we've been turning down. Probably 2-3 more retainers without hiring.

> And if that was live in 14 days instead of the 6-month tech project this usually feels like, what's that worth?

> That's a lot. That's like another $10k/month in capacity.

**Mistake ABC (pre-empts "we tried this before"):**

> Before I show you what I'd build, let me name the mistake I see agency owners make here, because it's why the templates and the VA didn't stick. Most people pick a tool first and automate the workflow they already have. Problem is they end up automating steps that shouldn't exist, six weeks in they've spent money making a bad process faster. What we do instead: first 3 days we map every step, then we KILL 30-40% of them before automating anything. The build's faster because there's less of it, and it sticks because it matches how your team actually works. That's how Steve's setup has held for months without slipping back, he's a repeat client now.

### Act IV — Close (23–30 min)

**Integration plan (3 steps):**

> Here's how it'd run. Week 1: I map your Later + Notion + ChatGPT flow on a Loom screen-share, send the full audit + build plan in 48 hrs. Weeks 2-3: we build it, you don't touch it, we integrate and test. Week 4: live and saving your team time, I monitor 30 days and hand off docs.

**Double tie-down:**

> That aside, how do you feel about the process? Feel like this is what you need?

> Yeah, that's exactly what we need.

**Price drop (tie to her number, two tiers, then silence):**

> Before investment, let's be on the same page. You said this is costing you $7-8k a month in wages and turned-away revenue. So we measure the cost against that, not against zero. Two ways to do this. Tier 1, Done-With-You: I send the full build playbook plus a 2-hour Loom walkthrough, your team implements. $2k. Tier 2, Done-For-You: we build, deploy, monitor 30 days, live in 14. $5,500. Tier 2 against the $7-8k you're losing every month pays for itself in under 3 weeks. After that it's just compounding.

*(Shut up. Do not speak first.)*

> ...the $5,500 one. I don't have the bandwidth to implement it myself.

**Risk reversal (reinforce even though she barely hesitated):**

> Smart. And to make it a no-brainer: built and live in 14 days or you don't pay until it is. And if 30 days post-launch we haven't taken at least 5 hrs/week off your team, full refund and you keep the build. Your downside is basically 45 minutes of onboarding.

**No-oriented close:**

> Would it be a bad idea to kick off Tier 2 next Monday?

> No, Monday works.

**Logistics:**

> Would it offend you if I grabbed a few quick things to line up the timeline? Who else needs to be looped in, how you want to handle payment, and where I should send the contract?

## The mechanics that did the work

| Stage | The move that mattered |
|---|---|
| Cold DM | Named peer (Steve) + real trigger, not the burned skeleton |
| Conversation | Labeled and went silent → she revealed growth was capped (bigger than time) |
| Proof | Held it until she asked, then offered a Loom (she pulls, you don't push) |
| Call ask | "Ops Teardown" deliverable, two specific times, never "quick chat" |
| Discovery | She quantified $7-8k/mo herself in Act II, so price felt small in Act IV |
| Mistake ABC | Pre-killed "we tried this before" before she could raise it |
| Close | No-oriented ("bad idea to start Monday?") + same-day contract |
