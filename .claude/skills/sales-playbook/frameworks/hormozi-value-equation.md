# Hormozi Value Equation — Applied to a Sales Call

> **Source basis:** All formulas, tactics, and quoted moves below are sourced from `references/research-synthesis.md` (Q4 section 5) which pulls from quantumbyte.ai's Hormozi explainer, Hormozi's own $100M Offers / $100M Leads material, and the synthesized "Advanced Conversational Architectures in High-Ticket Closing" report.

## The Formula

> **Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

Every action on a sales call should either **raise the numerator** or **lower the denominator**. If a move does neither, it's filler — cut it.

| Variable | Question it answers |
|---|---|
| **Dream Outcome** | What does the prospect actually want? (Not what you sell — what they want.) |
| **Perceived Likelihood** | Do they believe this will work for THEM? |
| **Time Delay** | How long until they see results? |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** | How much do they have to do? |

## How to apply it on a discovery call (move-by-move)

The discovery call structure in `scripts/discovery-call-script.md` is built around this. Every act either raises numerator or lowers denominator. Here's how each variable maps to actual moves:

### Move 1 — Diagnose, don't sell

> **Sourced principle:** "Do not sell deliverables (like SEO or ads); sell the diagnosis."

Before the call, send a pre-call diagnostic — a Typeform or short questionnaire — so you walk in with hypothesis-mode authority. The opener becomes:

> "We already analyzed your situation; here is what we are seeing — does that feel right?"

This transforms you from "vendor pitching" to "doctor presenting findings." Massive shift in Perceived Likelihood and authority.

**Aleem's adaptation:** A 5-question pre-call form before every Discovery Call:
1. What's the most manual part of your week?
2. What stack is that running through?
3. Roughly how much time per week does it eat?
4. What have you tried to fix it?
5. What would solving it unlock?

By the time you hit the call, you have the diagnosis. You're not asking — you're confirming.

---

### Move 2 — Maximize the Dream Outcome (Numerator ↑)

> **Sourced principle:** "Solve the primary bottleneck causing a monetary leak rather than just pitching a feature."

Don't pitch the feature. Pitch the outcome attached to a number.

| ❌ Don't say | ✅ Say |
|---|---|
| "We optimize your routes" | "Reduce your delivery costs by 30%" |
| "We build AI workflows" | "We take 12+ hrs/week of manual ops off your plate, usually inside 14 days" |
| "We do CRM automation" | "We make sure no qualified lead waits more than 60 seconds for a response — 21x conversion lift" |
| "We integrate Shopify with Klaviyo" | "We turn your Sunday reconciliation panic into a 5-min dashboard check" |

**Rule:** Every line you say about your offer should end in a number, a time saved, or a dollar reclaimed.

---

### Move 3 — Maximize Perceived Likelihood (Numerator ↑)

> **Sourced principle:** "Do not feature-dump. Use a Grand Slam Offer stacked with risk-reversals and social proof to eliminate fear of failure."

This is the "they need to believe it'll work for THEM specifically" lever. Three documented moves:

**3a — Named peer proof (not generic stats):**
- ❌ "We've helped lots of clients"
- ✅ "We built this exact workflow for [Peer 1] in your industry. Same stack, same problem. Cut 14 hrs/week. Want to see the build?"

**3b — Stack a risk reversal:**
- Frank Kern's verbatim: *"If you feel I wasted your time, I will send you a check for $1,500."*
- Aleem-version: *"If we don't take at least 5 hrs/week off your plate inside 21 days, full refund. You keep what we built."*

**3c — "Same/same" framing:**
- "Same stage as you. Same niche. Same toolchain. Same outcome they wanted. Here's what we did."

The more "same" you stack, the higher Perceived Likelihood.

---

### Move 4 — Minimize Time Delay (Denominator ↓)

> **Sourced principle:** "Offer a Fast-Start Bonus or create a quick win so they get results in minutes rather than months."

The prospect's biggest fear isn't price — it's "will I be embarrassed when nothing happens for 3 months?"

Documented moves:

**4a — Specific timeline anchor:**
- "First automation is live and saving you time inside 7 days. No 'discovery sprint,' no 'kickoff phase' — we build."

**4b — The Fast-Start Bonus:**
- "Bonus: I'll set up your first automation for free in the first call so you see the leak fixed before we even contract."
- This anchors Time Delay = 0 in the prospect's brain.

**4c — Sequencing the wins:**
- "Week 1: [specific small win]. Week 2: [specific medium win]. Week 4: [the full thing]."
- Multiple small Time Delays beat one big one.

---

### Move 5 — Minimize Effort & Sacrifice (Denominator ↓)

> **Sourced principle:** "Provide Done-For-You (DFY) execution so they do minimal work."

Founders DO NOT have time. Every "you'll need to..." is friction.

Documented moves:

**5a — Lead with DFY:**
- ❌ "We'll teach your team how to..."
- ✅ "Your team doesn't touch it. We build, we monitor, we report. You see the outcome in your dashboard."

**5b — Pre-empt the onboarding tax:**
- "I know onboarding new vendors usually takes 6+ hours of your time. With us, it's 45 minutes — one call to map your stack, then we go. You'll spend more time reading this email than onboarding us."

**5c — Take work off, don't add it:**
- "We're not giving you a new tool to learn. We're removing 3 of the ones eating your week."

## The Close (Hormozi-style)

> **Sourced principle:** "Present a choice between two yeses. Price based on value, not cost: 'We save you $3,000/month; our fee is $297.'"

The "choice between two yeses" — never single-option close.

| Tier | Frame |
|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — DIY playbook** | "I send you the exact workflow + Loom walkthrough for $X. You implement. No ongoing." |
| **Tier 2 — DFY execution** | "We build, deploy, monitor. Done in 14 days. $Y/month. White-glove." |

Then the math:
> "Tier 2 is $Y/month. You said the manual [thing] is costing your team ~$Z/month in lost hours. It pays for itself in [N] weeks. After that, every month is found money."

Then **silence**. Do not speak until they do.

## The full Hormozi sequence on the call

Stitched together:

```
0. Pre-call diagnostic Typeform → walk in with hypothesis

1. Frame-set as diagnostician, not vendor
   "We already analyzed your situation; here's what we're seeing — does that feel right?"

2. Use Sandler Pain Funnel + Voss labels to amplify Dream Outcome
   → Surface specific $/time leak, get prospect to quantify it themselves.

3. Pitch with Same/Same + Named Peer (Perceived Likelihood)
   "We built this exact thing for [Peer]. Same stack, same problem. Want to see the build?"

4. Compress Time Delay
   "First automation live in 7 days. Fast-Start bonus: I set it up for free on this call so you see it work."

5. Eliminate Effort & Sacrifice
   "Your team doesn't touch it. 45 min onboarding, then we go."

6. Two-yes price drop with math
   "Tier 1: DIY at $X. Tier 2: DFY at $Y. You said the manual costs you $Z/month. Pays for itself in 6 weeks."

7. Risk reversal stack
   "If we don't take 5 hrs/week off your plate inside 21 days, full refund. You keep what we built."

8. No-oriented close + silence
   "Would it be a bad idea to start with Tier 2 next Monday?"
```
