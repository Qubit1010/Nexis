# Agency-to-Agency Positioning — The White-Label Capacity Wedge (primary ICP)

> **This is the primary ICP track.** NexusPoint sells to other agencies — **marketing, AI, design, and branding agencies** — as a white-label AI-automation + build partner. The founder/SMB track (`offer/ai-automation-positioning.md`) is the secondary lane; when the prospect is an end-business, use that file instead.
>
> Grounded in the 2026 research pass in `references/research-synthesis.md` **Q10** (white-label economics, the real objection, decision-makers). Positioning specific to NexusPoint; the Value Equation math is in `frameworks/hormozi-value-equation.md`.

## Why sell to agencies (and why it's a better lane)

- **They already have the clients.** You're not fighting for end-buyers; you're renting capacity to people who've already closed. Shorter path to revenue.
- **They can't build what you build.** Marketing/design/branding agencies are drowning in AI-automation demand from their own clients but have no one in-house to fulfill it [Q10]. You are the fulfillment they're missing.
- **Recurring by nature.** White-label fulfillment becomes an ongoing overflow relationship, not a one-off.
- **Logic-first buyers.** Agency owners buy on ROI, margin, and delivery risk — which is exactly the posture in `frameworks/hormozi-selling-principles.md §1` [Q10].

## What white-label actually means here

You ship the work under **their** brand; their client never knows NexusPoint exists [Q10]. That means: their logo, their client comms, their markup. Your job is invisible, reliable fulfillment.

## The one-liner (verbatim in cold openers)

> **"We're the white-label AI-automation and build team behind agencies — you keep the client and the brand, we deliver the workflows and builds your clients are asking for, usually inside 14 days."**

Value-equation check: dream outcome (fulfill client demand you can't staff for), likelihood (named agency proof), delay (14 days), effort (fully white-labeled, off your plate).

## The real objection — and it isn't price

The dominant hesitation from agencies is **not** cost. It's: *"will delivery be consistent, will quality hold up in front of my client, and will you try to steal my client?"* [Q10]. This is a **fit + trust (self)** objection, not a money one (`frameworks/objection-psychology.md §3–4`). Preempt it in the first real conversation with three things:

1. **A written MSA/SOW/SLA** — delivery standards and turnaround in writing [Q10].
2. **A non-solicit / non-compete** on their clients — table stakes; say it before they ask [Q10].
3. **A small paid pilot** before any retainer — lets them de-risk in front of one client, not bet the relationship.

If you lead with these, you've dissolved the objection before it's spoken. This is "support not permission" applied to an agency's internal trust gate.

## The 3 sub-offers (productized, margin-protecting)

Price every tier so **their markup survives** — agencies buy capacity *and margin* [Q10]. Quote your wholesale; they mark it up to their client.

### Offer 1: The Partner Teardown (free → wedge)
A 20-min call where you look at their current client fulfillment gaps and name the first AI workflow (or build) you'd white-label for them. Free, even if they don't sign. The A2A version of the Ops Teardown — anchor per `scripts/discovery-call-script.md`.
> *"I'll spend 20 min on where your delivery is bottlenecked, and tell you the first thing we'd take off your team's plate under your brand."*

### Offer 2: The White-Label Sprint (per-project — wholesale $1,000-2,500)
One specific AI workflow or build, delivered white-label in 14 days, priced so they mark it up 2-3x to their client.
**Examples:** client-reporting automation, lead-routing/enrichment workflow, an AI content or ops workflow their client requested, a Next.js/Webflow build their team is over capacity for.
**Deliverable:** the built work under their brand + a handoff doc *they* can forward + 30-day fixes.
> *"Send us the brief, we build it under your name in 14 days, you hand your client a finished thing and keep the margin."*

### Offer 3: The Overflow Retainer (recurring — wholesale $2,500-6,000/mo)
Ongoing white-label capacity: a set number of builds/workflows per month, monitoring of everything shipped, and a private Slack for their team to drop briefs into. The "your invisible delivery team" play.
> *"We become the delivery team behind your agency — a set number of builds a month under your brand, monitored, on a Slack your team already lives in."*

## Who decides (multi-thread it)

Even inside a small agency the buy usually touches **the owner** (economics + the client-poaching fear) and a **delivery/ops lead** (will this actually make their life easier or create rework) — and B2B buying groups run 6-10 people at the larger end [Q10]. Sell past both: owner gets margin + non-solicit; delivery lead gets reliability + SLA. Ask in discovery who else needs to be comfortable (this is the authority move in `objection-psychology.md §4` — build *support*, isolate the real concern).

## Objections unique to A2A (map to the taxonomy)

| They say | Real driver | Reach for |
|---|---|---|
| "How do I know delivery's reliable?" | Fit/self — the #1 fear [Q10] | SLA + paid pilot + named agency reference |
| "Will you poach my client?" | Others/trust | Written non-solicit, stated up front |
| "What's my margin?" | Circumstances/money | Wholesale pricing that protects their markup |
| "White-label isn't really us" | Fit | pride-proof with agency-specific cases (`objection-psychology.md §3`) |
| "We handle that in-house" | Fit | reframe as overflow/capacity for when they're slammed (not replacement) |
