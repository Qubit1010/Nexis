# Discovery Call Prep — Latenode
*Cold email reply (AI workflow automation interest) · Call in 1 hour*

## Company Snapshot
- **What they do:** Cloud-based workflow automation platform — visual drag-and-drop builder with access to 1,200+ AI models and 5,500+ app integrations. No-code + full JavaScript support.
- **Scale:** 10,000+ active teams, 10M+ workflows/month. Real traction, not an early-stage experiment.
- **Pricing:** $0–$299/month. Positioned as "10x cheaper than Zapier" — cost-efficiency is a core message.
- **Target users:** Marketing, sales ops, customer support, and operations teams. Also offers a white-label iPaaS for SaaS companies.
- **Ratings:** 4.9 Capterra, 4.7 G2 — customers are happy.
- **Key differentiator:** All 1,200+ AI models accessible under one subscription, no per-model credentials needed.

## Likely Pain Points
- They're an automation company — they understand the value of AI workflows deeply. The question is what they want *built*, not whether they "get it."
- They likely want a **custom integration or feature** that their platform doesn't handle natively — maybe a proprietary AI pipeline, a specialized connector, or an internal tool.
- At $19–$299/month SaaS pricing, they're probably generating enough revenue to invest in internal tooling or expansion.
- Their white-label iPaaS offering suggests they're selling to agencies/SaaS builders — they may want **a showcase demo or client-facing tool** built in a compelling way.
- Website conversion is a growth lever for any SaaS — their homepage is functional but improving CRO could be valuable.

## NexusPoint Positioning

**Best angle:** "We build the custom AI-powered tools and systems that platforms like Latenode power under the hood — but tailored to your specific workflow."

- **AI Automation & Custom Development** — They already live in the AI automation space. The pitch isn't "let me explain automation to you" — it's "what custom system do you need built that you haven't had time to build yourself?" A bespoke AI agent, custom Latenode integration, or proprietary pipeline.
- **Custom SaaS / Web Apps** — If they're building client-facing tools or internal admin systems, NexusPoint builds those. MERN + AI integration is directly relevant.
- **Web Design** — Their SaaS marketing site could benefit from conversion optimization or a redesign to match their growth stage. Softer angle but worth probing.

## Questions to Ask
1. "What was it about the cold email that made you want to reply — is there a specific workflow or project you're trying to get off the ground?"
2. "When you look at your team's biggest operational bottleneck right now, where does the most time get lost?"
3. "Are you building something internally that you've been putting off because it's outside the core product — like an internal dashboard, reporting tool, or custom AI pipeline?"
4. "You have 10,000+ teams on the platform — are there specific automation use cases your customers keep asking for that you haven't built yet?"
5. "What does your current process look like for building custom features or integrations — is that handled in-house?"

## Watch-outs
- They're an automation platform company — don't pitch basic automation. Pitch *bespoke, complex builds* that sit on top of or alongside their platform.
- They're technically sophisticated. Come with specifics (LangGraph, RAG, agentic AI) not just buzzwords.
