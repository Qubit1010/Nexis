# Discovery Call Prep — Buffer
*Source unknown · Call in 15 minutes*

## Company Snapshot
- **What they do:** Social media management platform — schedule, publish, analyze, and engage across 11 social networks (Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, X, YouTube, and more).
- **Products:** Publish (scheduling), Create (AI-assisted content ideas), Community (comment management), Analyze (performance metrics), Collaborate (team approvals).
- **Scale:** 73,845 customers, 223,783 MAU, $24.6M ARR. 73 people across 15 countries — small, distributed team.
- **Founded:** 2010 by Joel Gascoigne. Well-established, profitable SaaS company. Known for radical transparency (they've published salaries and revenue publicly).
- **Target users:** Creators, small businesses, agencies, nonprofits. Skews toward SMBs and solo operators.
- **AI angle:** They have an AI Assistant and a "Create" module — actively investing in AI-powered content tools.

## Likely Pain Points
- **73 people, $24.6M ARR** — they run lean. Internal bandwidth is tight. They likely outsource specific builds rather than staffing up.
- **Content creation at scale** — their users need more AI-powered content tools. There may be internal tooling or feature development they want to move faster on.
- **Web presence** — their marketing site is clean but conversion optimization is always a lever for a SaaS growing at this stage.
- **Automation gaps** — as a product, they help others automate. Internally they probably have manual processes around onboarding, customer success, or marketing ops.

## NexusPoint Positioning

**Best angle:** "We build the custom AI tools and automation systems that fast-moving SaaS teams like yours need but don't have the bandwidth to build in-house."

- **AI Automation & Workflows** — Buffer invests in AI for their users. There may be internal AI tooling, automation, or integrations they want built.
- **Custom SaaS / Web Apps** — At 73 people, they likely outsource specific builds. Custom integrations, admin tools, or new feature prototypes.
- **Web Design** — Marketing site improvements, landing pages, or campaign pages for product launches.

## Questions to Ask
1. "What brought this on — is there a specific project or problem you're trying to solve, or are you exploring what we do?"
2. "What does your internal engineering team focus on — is there a type of work that typically gets deprioritized or outsourced?"
3. "You're investing in AI features for your users — are there any AI tools or automations you want to build for your own operations?"
4. "What's the timeline and decision-making process on your end?"

## Watch-outs
- No context on who you're talking to or why they reached out — open with a calibrated question to uncover the actual need before pitching anything.
- Buffer is known for being thoughtful and values-driven. Don't be aggressive or use hard-sell tactics — they'll disengage immediately.
