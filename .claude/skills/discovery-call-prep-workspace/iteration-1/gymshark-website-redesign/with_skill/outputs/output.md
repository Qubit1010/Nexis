# Discovery Call Prep — Gymshark
*Inbound (saw our work, interested in website improvements)*

## Company Snapshot
- **What they do:** Global fitness apparel and accessories brand. Founded 2012. One of the biggest names in gym/workout clothing — women's and men's activewear, accessories.
- **Scale:** Multi-currency, 25+ international markets, physical retail locations, hundreds of SKUs, mature loyalty infrastructure. This is a large enterprise brand.
- **Current tech stack:** Next.js (React), Contentful CMS, Shopify backend, mParticle analytics, Dynamic Yield personalization. They're already running a sophisticated, well-resourced stack.
- **Target audience:** Fitness enthusiasts across weightlifting, running, HIIT, pilates — broad but community-driven.
- **Notable:** They have a dedicated support hub, blog/editorial ("Gymshark Central"), and a loyalty/referral program.

## Likely Pain Points
- At their scale, website "improvements" are likely **specific experiments or feature additions** — not a full redesign. They probably want to test a specific page, campaign microsite, or new feature.
- **Performance optimization** — large catalog + international traffic means speed is always a concern.
- **AI-powered personalization** — they use Dynamic Yield but could benefit from more customized product recommendation logic.
- **Campaign landing pages** — for product launches, influencer campaigns, or seasonal drops that need to move fast outside their main sprint cycle.

## NexusPoint Positioning

**Best angle:** "We move fast on scoped, specific builds — campaign pages, performance fixes, and AI-powered features you want to ship without tying up your internal team."

- **Web Design & Development** — For campaign microsites, landing pages, or specific feature additions that need quick turnaround.
- **AI Automation** — If they're looking to automate content workflows, product copy generation, or customer support flows on the site.

## Questions to Ask
1. "What specifically prompted the reach-out — is there a project or launch coming up that you need to move fast on?"
2. "What does your internal dev team look like — are you looking for capacity extension or something your team doesn't specialize in?"
3. "What's the timeline you're working with and who else is involved in the decision?"
4. "Have you worked with external agencies before, and if so, what worked or didn't work about that?"

## Watch-outs
- **Size mismatch:** Gymshark is an enterprise brand with a full internal engineering team. They're almost certainly not looking for a full-service agency relationship — they want a specialist for a defined scope. Make sure the conversation is about a *specific project*, not ongoing work.
- **Don't over-pitch.** They've heard every agency pitch. Ask questions and let them tell you what they need before positioning anything.
- **Budget expectations:** Enterprise brands often have enterprise procurement processes (RFPs, legal, net-60 payment terms). Probe early whether this is a direct hire or goes through a vendor process.
