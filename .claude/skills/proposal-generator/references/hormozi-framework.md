## Value Equation

The perceived value of any offer is determined by:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

To maximize value:
- **Increase** the Dream Outcome (make the result bigger, more tangible)
- **Increase** Perceived Likelihood (proof, guarantees, case studies)
- **Decrease** Time Delay (faster results, quick wins, phased delivery)
- **Decrease** Effort & Sacrifice (done-for-you, turnkey, minimal client involvement)

Every proposal section should reinforce at least one of these levers.

## The "So That" Method

Chain deliverables through "so that..." until you reach the real business outcome. Never stop at the deliverable itself.

**Example:**
> We redesign your website **so that** visitors convert at 3x the current rate **so that** you generate 40+ qualified leads per month **so that** your sales team closes $200K in new revenue this quarter.

The final "so that" is the Dream Outcome. Use it as the headline promise of the proposal.

Rules:
- Chain at least 3 levels deep
- End with a measurable business result (revenue, time saved, cost reduced)
- Include a timeframe ("in 90 days", "by Q2", "within 8 weeks")

## Four Friction Categories

Every client faces friction that prevents them from achieving their goal. Map friction across these four categories:

| Category | What They Fear | Example Friction |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Time** | "How long will this take?" | Slow delivery, delayed ROI, waiting for results |
| **Money** | "What if I waste my investment?" | Budget risk, unclear ROI, hidden costs |
| **Effort** | "How much work do I have to do?" | Complex onboarding, constant feedback loops, learning new tools |
| **Fear** | "What if it doesn't work?" | No guarantee, unproven approach, past bad experiences |

For each friction, identify the specific client pain and flip it into a named solution.

## Problem-Solution Mapping

For each friction point, create a named solution that eliminates it:

| Friction | Solution Name | What It Does |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| "Last agency took 6 months" | The 8-Week Sprint Framework | Fixed timeline with weekly milestones |
| "What if the site doesn't convert?" | The Conversion Guarantee | Performance guarantee with metrics |
| "I don't have time for constant meetings" | The Hands-Off Build Process | Async updates, 2 check-ins total |
| "I've been burned before" | The Safety Net Clause | 14-day unconditional refund window |

Rules for naming solutions:
- Use distinctive, branded names (not "website redesign" but "Revenue-Optimized Conversion Platform")
- Each name should imply the outcome, not the process
- Make them sound like proprietary systems/frameworks

## Value Stack Construction

Build a value stack that makes the price feel small relative to total value:

1. **List all deliverables** from the Problem-Solution mapping
2. **Assign standalone market value** to each (what it would cost to buy separately)
3. **Name each deliverable** distinctively
4. **Add one-line descriptions** focused on outcomes
5. **Calculate total value** (sum of all individual values)
6. **Split into Core Offer + Bonuses:**
   - Core: 2-4 high-value items that solve the main problem
   - Bonuses: 2-3 items that are low cost to deliver but high perceived value

**Bonus structure (4 elements each):**
- Name (branded, outcome-focused)
- Retail value (what it would cost standalone)
- Benefit (one sentence on what it does for them)
- Why it's free (brief reason you're including it)

## MAGIC Offer Naming

Use the MAGIC formula to create compelling offer names:

| Letter | Element | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **M** | Magnetic Reason Why | "The Q1..." (timely) |
| **A** | Avatar | "...for SaaS Founders..." |
| **G** | Goal | "...Revenue Acceleration..." |
| **I** | Interval | "...90-Day..." |
| **C** | Container | "...System" / "...Blueprint" / "...Framework" |

**Full example:** "The Q1 Revenue Acceleration 90-Day System for SaaS Founders"

The name should communicate: who it's for, what they get, how fast, and that it's a system (not a one-off project).

## Pricing Psychology

- **Price = 1/10th of total value stack.** If total value is $50K, price at $5K.
- **Never lead with price.** Build value first, present price after the stack.
- **Frame as investment, not cost.** "Your investment" not "the price" or "the cost."
- **Include ROI math.** Show exactly how the investment pays for itself. Example: "10 new leads/month x $5K average deal = $50K/month. Investment pays for itself in the first 2 weeks."
- **Offer 2-3 tiers when appropriate:** Essential (core only), Growth (core + some bonuses), Scale (everything). Anchor with the highest tier first.

## Guarantee Types

Stack two guarantees when possible:

| Type | How It Works | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Unconditional** | Full refund, no questions, within X days | Building trust with new clients |
| **Conditional** | If we don't hit [metric] by [date], we [remedy] | Performance-based projects |
| **Service** | We work for free until [result] is achieved | High-confidence engagements |
| **Anti-guarantee** | "This isn't for everyone" (exclusivity play) | Premium positioning |

Rules:
- Make the guarantee specific and measurable
- Include a timeframe
- Name the guarantee ("The Safety Net Clause", "The Results-or-Free Promise")

## Urgency and Scarcity

Close with genuine urgency. Never fabricate scarcity.

**Legitimate urgency levers:**
- **Capacity:** "We take on 3 new clients per month. 1 slot available for [month]."
- **Bonus expiration:** "Bonuses included if we start by [date]."
- **Pricing window:** "This proposal is valid for 7 days."
- **Seasonal relevance:** "Starting now means results before [event/quarter]."

**CTA rules:**
- Single, clear next step (not multiple options)
- Low friction ("Reply with 'Let's go'" or "Book a 15-minute call")
- Reinforce the dream outcome in the CTA
