# Beginner - Section 6: 10 Everyday Use Cases (With Prompts You Can Use Today)

*The fastest way to learn what Claude is for is to use it for something real right now.*

**Bottom line:** Here are 10 common use cases with prompts you can paste in, adjust, and get value from today. Pick two or three from your own work and try them before the week is out.

---

## 1. Inbox triage

"I am going to paste a block of emails from my inbox. For each one, tell me: what action is needed, who it is from, and what the urgency level is (high, medium, low). Organize the output as a table."

## 2. Summarize a long document

"Here is a document. Give me: (1) a 3-sentence summary, (2) the 5 most important points as bullets, and (3) any action items or open questions. [paste document]"

## 3. Plan a project

"I need to [describe the project]. Help me break this into phases with rough timelines. Ask me any questions you need about scope, team, or deadline before you start."

## 4. Prepare for a meeting

"I have a meeting with [who] about [topic] in [time]. Help me prepare: what questions should I ask, what context should I bring, and what outcome should I be pushing for?"

## 5. Turn rough notes into a polished document

"Here are my rough notes from [context]: [paste notes]. Turn these into a clean, structured document in a professional tone. Keep the same information but make it presentable."

## 6. Research a topic quickly

"Give me a structured briefing on [topic]: what it is, why it matters, the key players or concepts, and the most important thing I should know. Cite anything specific so I know what to verify."

## 7. Analyze a spreadsheet

"I am going to paste data from a spreadsheet. Identify: the key trends, any anomalies or outliers, and what actions these numbers suggest. [paste data]"

## 8. Draft and edit something

"Draft a [type of content] for [audience] about [topic]. Tone: [professional / friendly / authoritative]. Length: under [number] words. I will iterate from your draft." After the draft: "Tighten it, cut the weakest sentence, and make the opening line stronger."

## 9. Learn something fast

"Explain [topic] to me like I have a general business background but no technical knowledge of this area. Use a real-world analogy to anchor it, then give me the 3 things I actually need to understand to use this in my work."

## 10. Make a decision

"I am deciding between [option A] and [option B]. Here is the context: [situation]. Give me a structured comparison: the top 3 pros and cons of each, which you would recommend and why, and what information I would need to be more certain."

---

## How to use this list

- Do not try all ten at once. Pick the two or three that match something you actually have to do this week.
- Paste the prompt, adjust the parts in brackets, and add any relevant files or background above it.
- After the first answer, iterate in the same conversation rather than starting fresh.

> **This week:** Complete at least two of these with real work from your life. Notice how long it takes compared to doing the same task without Claude. That delta is your new baseline.

## Key takeaways

- The fastest way to learn Claude is to use it for something real, not to study prompting theory.
- Adjust and iterate: the first output is a starting point, not a finished product.
- Adding context (a document, your background, the intended audience) is the biggest upgrade to any of these prompts.
- If something does not land right, stay in the conversation and redirect instead of starting over.
