# Intermediate - Section 18: Build Your Personal AI Assistant

*The moment everything in this guide becomes one system.*

**Bottom line:** You have now covered all the pieces: context and memory, tool connections, Skills and routines, and Cowork. This section is about putting them together into a personal AI assistant that knows your work, connects to your tools, has your workflows built in, and shows up automatically. The Four C's framework is your blueprint.

---

## The Four C's

Everything you have learned in the Intermediate level maps to one of four dimensions. Get all four right and you have an assistant that genuinely knows your operation.

| The C | What it means | Where you built it |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Context** | Claude knows who you are, what you do, and how you work | Section 17: Projects, custom instructions, reference docs, the memory feature |
| **Connections** | Claude works inside your actual tools | Section 14: Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Slack, Notion, MCP |
| **Capabilities** | Claude can execute tasks, not just answer questions | Sections 11-12 (Cowork), Section 15 (Skills) |
| **Cadence** | Claude shows up on a schedule, not just when you ask | Section 16: local schedules and Cloud Routines |

A typical chat with Claude delivers Context at best. An assistant built on all four delivers everything.

## What a fully-built day looks like

- **7:30am:** Cloud Routine runs your morning brief. You open it on your phone over coffee. (Cadence)
- **9:00am:** You start a client Project session. Claude already knows the client, the brief, and your voice. (Context)
- **10:30am:** "Draft an update for [client] using yesterday's notes." Claude pulls from the Drive doc automatically. (Connections + Capabilities via Skills)
- **2:00pm:** You brief Cowork to process this week's feedback forms and build the summary sheet. You work on something else. (Capabilities via Cowork)
- **End of week:** The weekly review Routine runs overnight and a summary lands in your inbox Monday morning. (Cadence)

## The 4-step build

1. **Context first.** Set up your core Projects and custom instructions. Upload your two or three most important reference documents. This is the foundation.
2. **Add one connection.** Connect the tool you use most (Gmail, Drive, or Slack). Get value from it for a week before adding another.
3. **Build your first Skill.** Pick the task you do most often and encode it. The weekly client update or the first-draft document are good starting points.
4. **Add one routine.** Identify one task that repeats on a fixed schedule and automate it. The morning brief is the classic first routine.

## Start with one

The temptation is to build everything at once. Resist it. A single well-built Project that you actually use every day is worth more than five Projects that feel incomplete. One Skill you run three times a week beats ten Skills that sit idle.

Build the Four C's incrementally, starting with Context, then adding each layer as you actually feel the gap.

> **The measure of a good assistant:** You stop thinking "I need to explain my business to Claude" and start thinking "Claude already knows my business." That shift is what the Four C's framework produces when it is working.

## Key takeaways

- The Four C's are Context, Connections, Capabilities, and Cadence. All four together produce an assistant that knows your operation.
- Start with Context (Projects, custom instructions, reference docs). Build outward.
- Add one connection, one Skill, and one routine before stacking more.
- The goal is an assistant you stop setting up and just use. Incremental building gets there faster than trying to build everything at once.
