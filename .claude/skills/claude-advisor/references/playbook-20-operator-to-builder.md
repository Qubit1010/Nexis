# Intermediate - Section 20: From Operator to Builder: What's Next

*You have graduated from user to operator. Here is what that means and what comes next.*

**Bottom line:** By the end of the Intermediate level you are not just using Claude, you are operating it. You have context set up, tools connected, Skills built, and some tasks on autopilot. The Advanced level takes you to builder: creating systems that run independently, using Claude Code, and integrating Claude into your actual products and workflows.

---

## What you can do now

If you have worked through the Intermediate level, you can:

- Maintain Projects that give Claude persistent context about your work
- Connect Claude to the tools you use every day (Gmail, Drive, Slack, Notion)
- Encode repeatable workflows into Skills and run them in seconds
- Delegate document-heavy tasks to Cowork
- Schedule recurring tasks as local routines or Cloud Routines
- Choose the right model for each job and manage your usage efficiently

That set of capabilities puts you in the top tier of professional Claude users. Most people stop here and get substantial value.

## Your Intermediate toolkit in one box

- **Projects and custom instructions** for persistent context
- **File uploads and web search** for grounded, current answers
- **MCP connectors** for Gmail, Drive, Slack, Notion, and more
- **Skills** for repeatable workflows encoded once
- **Cowork** for autonomous document and file work
- **Cloud Routines and local schedules** for recurring automation
- **Model routing** (Haiku/Sonnet/Opus) for efficiency

## Readiness check: are you ready for Advanced?

You are ready to move to the Advanced level when:

- You use Claude daily for real work, not just occasionally
- You have at least two Projects and two Skills that you actually use
- You have tried Cowork on at least one real task
- You have a recurring routine running
- You want Claude to build things, not just do things

If your answer to the last point is yes, the Advanced level is yours.

## What Advanced unlocks

- **Claude Code:** The most powerful surface. Build and fix software, automate complex multi-file projects, and create systems that run without you present.
- **Orchestrating agents:** Run multiple Claude sessions in parallel, each working on a different part of a problem.
- **Plugins, CLIs, and MCP at depth:** Extend Claude's capabilities with tools from Anthropic, the community, and your own builds.
- **The Agent SDK:** Build Claude into your own products and services, with custom tools, permissions, and orchestration.
- **Claude as a business OS:** Use the architecture patterns from this guide to run an entire business operation on Claude as the backbone.

> **The shift:** The Intermediate level is about making Claude work better for you. The Advanced level is about making Claude work for your users, your clients, or your business, independently and at scale. If that sounds like what you want, keep going.

## Key takeaways

- The Intermediate level makes you an operator: Claude is set up, connected, skilled, and partially on autopilot.
- Most professional users stop here and get strong value. That is a legitimate place to land.
- The Advanced level is for people who want to build systems, not just use them. Claude Code, agent orchestration, and the API are the main tools.
- The readiness check is honest: do not rush to Advanced. Solid Intermediate habits produce more value than rushed Advanced experiments.
