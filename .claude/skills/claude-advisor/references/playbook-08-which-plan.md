# Beginner - Section 8: Which Plan Do You Actually Need?

*A clear decision framework so you stop over-paying or running out mid-month.*

**Bottom line:** There are four tiers: Free, Pro, Max, and Team/Enterprise. Most individuals who use Claude seriously for work will land on Pro. Max is for heavy daily users who hit usage limits on Pro. Team/Enterprise is for groups. Start lower and upgrade when you actually feel the friction.

---

## Plans at a glance

| Plan | Price (approx.) | Who it is for |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| Free | $0/month | Trying Claude, light occasional use |
| Pro | ~$20/month | Individuals using Claude daily for work |
| Max | ~$100-200/month | Power users who exhaust Pro's limits |
| Team | ~$25/seat/month | Shared access across a small team |
| Enterprise | Custom pricing | Large organizations with compliance needs |

*Note: Anthropic adjusts pricing and plan features. Check claude.ai for the current figures before deciding.*

## What the plans actually differ on

- **Usage allowance:** How much you can do per rolling time window (typically a 5-hour window). Free is the smallest. Pro is generous for most daily users. Max is the highest.
- **Feature access:** Projects and some advanced features require a paid plan.
- **Model access:** Paid plans get priority access to the top models.
- **Team features:** Shared billing, admin controls, and user management at the Team and Enterprise tiers.

## How usage windows work

Claude does not work on a monthly counter. It works on a rolling window, typically around five hours. When you hit the limit, you wait for the window to reset. This means:

- Heavy use in a short burst can exhaust your allowance faster than the same usage spread across a day.
- Max-tier users on the most intensive models still hit limits if they run very large workloads in concentrated bursts.
- Usage-smart habits (covered in Section 19) reduce how often you hit those walls.

## Which plan to pick

- **Free:** Fine for occasional use, one-off questions, and trying Claude for the first time. You will hit the cap quickly if you use Claude seriously for work.
- **Pro (~$20/month):** The right starting point for most readers of this guide. Covers daily writing, analysis, Projects, and coding help. Most people with a genuine work use case should start here.
- **Max (~$100-200/month):** Upgrade here when you are consistently hitting Pro's limits mid-project, not before. If you are building with Claude Code for hours each day, this is where you will end up.
- **Team:** Worth it as soon as you have two or more people using Claude regularly. Shared billing and centralized admin are worth the slightly higher per-seat cost.
- **Enterprise:** When your organization needs SSO, data governance, compliance controls, and SLAs. Not a typical starting point.

> **The rule:** Start on Pro. Use it intensively for 30 days. If you hit the usage ceiling more than once or twice a week, move to Max. If you never hit it, Pro is enough.

## Key takeaways

- Four practical tiers: Free (try it), Pro (daily work), Max (power user), Team/Enterprise (groups).
- Claude usage runs on rolling windows, not monthly counters. Heavy bursts drain allowances faster.
- The right plan for most individual daily users is Pro. Upgrade to Max when you actually feel the limit, not before.
- Prices change. Always check claude.ai for the current numbers before deciding.
