# Beginner - Section 10: Your 7-Day Starter Plan

*Build the habit in one week. Then decide how deep to go.*

**Bottom line:** The fastest way to embed Claude into your work is to commit one week to using it intentionally on real tasks. This is the plan. At the end of the week you will know exactly how valuable it is for your work and whether you want to go deeper.

---

## The 7-day plan

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| Day 1 | Set up | Install the Desktop app. Create your first Project. Set custom instructions. |
| Day 2 | Brief well | Take a real task you have today and prompt it properly using R-C-T-F-E. Compare the output to what you would have done without Claude. |
| Day 3 | Get wins | Use the prompts from Section 6. Pick the two or three that match your real work. Notice the time saved. |
| Day 4 | Files and search | Upload a document or spreadsheet you have been meaning to work through. Turn on web search and ask a question that needs current information. |
| Day 5 | Build a tool | Ask Claude to make you an Artifact: a template, a checklist, a calculator, or a one-page reference document. Keep it. |
| Day 6 | Make it yours | Add a second Project for a different role or client. Refine the custom instructions based on what you noticed this week. |
| Day 7 | Review and decide | Look back at the week. What saved the most time? What did not land? What would you automate next? |

## Your beginner toolkit in one box

By the end of this week you will have:

- The Desktop app installed and a keyboard shortcut to Quick Entry
- At least one Project with real context and custom instructions
- A reliable prompting habit (R-C-T-F-E + ask questions first + iterate)
- A few reusable Artifacts built from your actual work
- A clear sense of where Claude saves you the most time

## Readiness check: are you ready for the Intermediate level?

You are ready to move on when you can honestly say yes to all of these:

- I use Claude at least three or four times a week on real work tasks.
- I have at least one Project set up and I know why it is better than a plain conversation.
- I iterate inside conversations rather than restarting every time.
- I know what to verify and I do it before high-stakes output goes anywhere.

If you are checking all four, move to the Intermediate level. It opens up Cowork (Claude working for you), the right model for each job, connecting Claude to your other tools, and the Skills system that makes Claude truly yours.

> **One last beginner thought:** Most people stop here. They use Claude for writing help and feel a mild improvement. The people who get an order-of-magnitude upgrade from this tool are the ones who go further. The next two levels are where the real leverage is. If you are curious, keep reading.

## Key takeaways

- One focused week builds a habit faster than months of occasional dipping.
- The minimum viable setup: Desktop app, one Project, the R-C-T-F-E habit.
- The readiness check tells you honestly whether to move on or consolidate first.
- The Intermediate level (next) is where Claude stops assisting you and starts working for you.
