# Intermediate - Section 12: Cowork in Action: 6 Real Workflows

*Concrete use cases you can run on your own files today.*

**Bottom line:** Cowork is most valuable when the work is repetitive, document-heavy, or involves many files at once. Here are six workflows that show the range, from competitive research to monthly reporting. Adapt the ones closest to your actual work.

---

## Workflow 1: Competitor content scan

**Goal:** Find what a competitor is publishing and surface strategic gaps.

**How to run it:** Give Cowork a list of competitor websites or RSS links. Ask it to pull recent content titles and summaries, categorize them by topic, and flag any themes you are not covering.

**Output:** A spreadsheet with competitor content mapped by topic, frequency, and angle, with a gap analysis column for your own strategy.

**Why it works:** This would take a human researcher two or three hours of browsing and note-taking. Cowork can work through a structured list in minutes.

---

## Workflow 2: One-asset content repurposer

**Goal:** Turn one piece of long-form content (a blog post, transcript, or report) into multiple formats.

**How to run it:** Give Cowork the source document. Ask it to produce: a LinkedIn post, a Twitter/X thread, a summary email, and three pull-quote candidates. All in your voice.

**Output:** Four ready-to-use pieces from one source document. Keep the ones that land, discard the rest.

---

## Workflow 3: Batch Canva-ready graphics brief

**Goal:** Take a content calendar and produce a structured brief for a designer.

**How to run it:** Give Cowork your content calendar (a spreadsheet or list). Ask it to write a one-paragraph creative brief for each post: subject, hook, key visual direction, and call to action. Output as a table.

**Output:** A table your designer can work from directly, eliminating the back-and-forth of briefing each post individually.

---

## Workflow 4: Morning brief

**Goal:** A daily intelligence document ready when you start work.

**How to run it:** Point Cowork at a folder where you drop news articles, newsletters, or bookmarks. Ask it to summarize each source, identify the two or three ideas most relevant to your work, and format the output as a one-page brief.

**Output:** A one-page reading at the start of every morning that replaces an hour of tab-switching.

---

## Workflow 5: Monthly financial dashboard

**Goal:** Turn a folder of monthly reports or CSV exports into a clear executive summary.

**How to run it:** Export your financial data to a folder (QuickBooks, Stripe, or a bank export). Ask Cowork to read all files, compile key figures (revenue, expenses, margin), spot trends, flag anomalies, and produce a one-page summary table.

**Output:** A clean monthly snapshot. Still verify the numbers yourself, but the compilation and basic trend-spotting are done.

---

## Workflow 6: Automated invoicing

**Goal:** Generate a batch of invoices from a spreadsheet of client work.

**How to run it:** Give Cowork a spreadsheet with client name, project description, hours or deliverables, and rate. Give it a template invoice. Ask it to produce one completed invoice per row, filled in from the spreadsheet data.

**Output:** A folder of completed invoice documents, ready for your review and sending.

---

## Running these well

- **Define the output format upfront.** "A spreadsheet with columns for X, Y, Z" is better than "a summary."
- **Start with Ask Before Acting** for any workflow you have not run before.
- **Give it one task type at a time.** Combining too many steps in one instruction produces messier results than chaining two or three focused instructions.
- **Review before using.** Cowork is fast, but you are the quality check.

## Key takeaways

- Cowork's sweet spot is repetitive, document-heavy, batch work that would take a human researcher hours.
- Define the output format as precisely as the input. A vague output spec produces a vague output.
- Chain focused instructions rather than bundling every step into one huge ask.
- The value compounds: once a workflow is defined and working, you can re-run it on new input in minutes.
