# Beginner - Section 3: Claude.ai Deep Dive: Your First Real Workspace

*Master the main door, the web chat, properly.*

**Bottom line:** Claude.ai is where almost everyone starts and where most everyday value lives. Learn five things, the interface, file upload, web search, Projects, and Artifacts, and you will already be ahead of most users.

---

## The interface, in 30 seconds

- **The conversation thread** in the center: you type at the bottom, Claude replies above. Press the new-chat button to start fresh whenever the topic changes.
- **The model picker** near the top lets you choose which version of Claude answers. The default is fine for beginners.
- **The attach button** (a paperclip or plus) lets you upload files into the conversation.
- **Web search** can be toggled on so Claude can look things up that are newer than its training.
- **The side panel** opens on the right whenever Claude builds something interactive. That is an Artifact.

## Uploading files

You can drop in documents, spreadsheets, PDFs, images, and more, then ask Claude to work with them: summarize a long report, pull the key numbers out of a spreadsheet, rewrite a contract clause in plain English, or describe what is in a screenshot. This is one of the fastest ways to get real value on day one. Instead of describing your document, just give it to Claude.

## Web search: closing the knowledge-cutoff gap

Claude has a training cutoff and does not know today's news by default. Turning on web search lets it look up current information and cite where it came from. Use it for anything time-sensitive: recent events, current prices, a company's latest details. Leave it off when you just want Claude to reason over what you have already given it.

## Projects: stop starting from zero

This is the single most valuable beginner feature, and most people never touch it. A **Project** is a saved workspace that remembers its context. You upload reference files once (up to around 20 files), set custom instructions once, and then every conversation you start inside that Project already knows your world. Think one Project per role or per client.

| A plain conversation | A Project |
|----------------------|-----------|
| Forgets everything once the chat ends | Remembers context across every chat inside it |
| You re-upload your files each time | Files uploaded once, always available |
| You repeat your instructions every time | Instructions set once, applied automatically |
| Best for quick, one-off questions | Best for ongoing work for a role or client |

Custom instructions are where you tell Claude who it is for this Project: your brand voice, your role, the format you want, the things to avoid. Set them well and you stop re-explaining yourself in every chat. *(Projects are available on Claude's paid plans.)*

## Artifacts: when Claude builds you a tool

When you ask Claude to make something more than text, a formatted document, a table, a chart, a simple calculator, even a small working web page, it opens an **Artifact** in the side panel. You can read it, use it, copy it, download it, and ask Claude to change it right there. It is the difference between Claude telling you how to build something and Claude handing you the finished thing.

Try: "Build me a one-page pricing sheet for three packages" or "Make a simple ROI calculator I can fill in."

> **Do this now: build your first workspace**
> 1. Create a Project and name it for a real role or client.
> 2. Add custom instructions: who Claude is, your voice, what good output looks like.
> 3. Upload two or three reference files (a brand guide, past work, a key document).
> 4. Start a chat inside it and ask for something real. Notice that you never had to explain your context. That is the upgrade from search bar to workspace.

## Key takeaways

- Claude.ai is the main door: a chat window that is really a workspace.
- Upload files instead of describing them, and turn on web search for anything time-sensitive.
- Projects remember your context, files, and instructions, so you stop starting from zero. Use one per role or client.
- Artifacts hand you finished, editable things (documents, tables, charts, small apps), not just advice.
