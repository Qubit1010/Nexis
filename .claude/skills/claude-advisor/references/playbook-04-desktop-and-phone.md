# Beginner - Section 4: Claude on Your Desktop and Phone

*Get Claude off the browser tab and onto every screen you use.*

**Bottom line:** The browser is fine for trying Claude. The Desktop app is where you should actually live: lighter, always a keystroke away, and the one place the more powerful modes appear later. The phone app keeps you connected and lets you approve work on the go.

---

## The Desktop app: your daily home

The Desktop app is the same Claude, installed as a native application on your Mac or Windows machine instead of running in a browser tab. Three reasons to switch to it:

- **It is lighter and faster.** A browser tab can eat well over a gigabyte of memory. The app uses a small fraction of that, so it stays snappy even when you leave it open all day.
- **It is always a keystroke away.** A Quick Entry pop-up lets you summon Claude over whatever you are working on, ask your question, and dismiss it, without hunting for the right window.
- **It is the one home for everything.** The app brings chat, Cowork, and Claude Code together in a single place, and it can connect to apps and files on your computer.

Get it from claude.ai (look for the download link), install it, and sign in with the same account. Your conversations and Projects follow you automatically.

## The phone app: pocket assistant and remote control

The mobile app plays two distinct roles.

- **A pocket assistant.** Quick questions, dictating a draft while you walk, snapping a photo and asking about it, or capturing an idea before it disappears.
- **A remote control.** This is the part most people miss. If you have a longer task running on your computer, such as a Cowork job or a Claude Code session, the phone lets you check on it, answer its questions, and approve its next step from anywhere. The feature for passing tasks between phone and desktop is called **Dispatch**.

One practical note: approval requests sent to your phone time out after about 30 minutes. It is built for staying in the loop while you step away briefly, not for leaving something hanging for hours.

## When to use which

| Surface | Reach for it when... |
|---------|----------------------|
| Web (browser) | You are just trying Claude, on a shared or public computer, or you do not want to install anything |
| Desktop app | It is your daily driver, you want Claude a keystroke away, and you plan to use Cowork or Code |
| Mobile app | You are on the go, capturing ideas, or checking and approving a task running on your computer |

> **Do this now:** Install the Desktop app and sign in. Trigger the Quick Entry pop-up once so you know the shortcut. Then install the mobile app so you can capture ideas and approve tasks from anywhere. You now have Claude on every screen you use, all sharing the same account, conversations, and Projects.

## Key takeaways

- The Desktop app is the daily home: lighter than a browser, a keystroke away via Quick Entry, and the single place where chat, Cowork, and Code all live.
- The phone app is both a pocket assistant and a remote control for tasks running on your computer, through Dispatch. Approvals expire in about 30 minutes.
- Your account syncs across all three surfaces, conversations and Projects included.
- Rule of thumb: web to try it, Desktop to live in, mobile to stay connected.
