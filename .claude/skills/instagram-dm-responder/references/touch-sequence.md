Loaded in Scenario A (sequence follow-up). Covers the no-reply follow-up sequence
after Touch 1 has been manually sent on Instagram.

Touch 1 is generated by `instagram-outreach` and sent manually. This file covers
Touches 2, 3, and 4 — which this skill generates.

**Character limits:**
- Touch 2: 300 chars ideal, 400 hard cap
- Touch 3: 400 chars hard cap
- Touch 4: 300 chars ideal, 400 hard cap

Instagram is read on a phone in motion. Long messages get skimmed or ignored. Shorter
is almost always better.

## The sequence at a glance

| Touch | Day | Purpose | NexusPoint named? | Ask? |
|-------|-----|---------|-------------------|------|
| 1 | 0 | Warm opener — Voss label + one genuine question | No | No |
| 2 | 3 | Value drop — observation grounded in their content | No | No |
| 3 | 5 | Soft intro + named-peer proof + anchored Teardown ask | Yes | Yes — anchored Ops Teardown |
| 4 | 7 or 14 | Honest exit | Optional | No |

Touch 3 carries the only ask in the sequence, and it's anchored to the Ops
Teardown deliverable ("I screen-share your stack and tell you the first thing
I'd automate"), never a bare "quick chat". A soft anchored ask still does the
job of generating a reply — the Teardown framing is low-pressure because the
prospect gets value just by showing up. Full sequence + cadence rationale:
`sales-playbook/scripts/instagram-cold-dm-sequence.md`.

## Touch 2 — Day 3 — Value drop

**What it does:** shows you actually read their profile and thought about their
situation. Not a follow-up. Not a pitch. A useful thing a peer would send.

**Structure:**
1. **No-ask opener** — "not looking for a reply, just thought this was relevant"
   or "just wanted to pass this along." Sets the right expectation immediately.
2. **Observation** — one thing you notice about their situation from the outside.
   If they have a recent post or caption with a pain signal — use it. That's
   more specific than anything in their bio.
3. Optional: one concrete thing you've seen at their stage — a pattern, a
   mistake, a lever. Not advice. Not a pitch. Something a peer would notice.

**Where to pull the personalization from (in order of preference):**
1. **Recent post or caption** — highest signal. Someone who posted "spent my whole
   Sunday reconciling Shopify orders" is telling you exactly what to label.
2. **Bio language** — how they self-identify ("bootstrapped", "scaling", "building
   in public", "ops-first") tells you the pain register to use.
3. **Follower count + content frequency** — high follower count + frequent posts =
   they care about brand and audience. Low follower count + ops bio = they're
   building, not performing. Different observations land differently.
4. **Company type + stage:**
   - Solo / 1-3 people: founder is the connective tissue between everything
   - Small team (4-10): coordination overhead just arrived
   - Growing team (10-25): handoffs are breaking, tool sprawl hurts

**Hard rules:**
- No NexusPoint mention
- No case study, no results with percentages
- No call ask
- No "I can help with this"
- No sign-off (Instagram DMs don't sign off)
- No emojis, no em dashes

**Example — bootstrapped e-comm founder, recent post about manual ops:**

They posted: "spent my whole Sunday reconciling orders in Shopify instead of
working on the product. something has to change."

```
Hey Maya - not looking for a reply, just thought this was relevant.

It looks like that Shopify Sunday is the exact thing that compounds - every week
you fix it manually is a week it doesn't get turned into a system.
```

**Example — agency owner, 12-person team, bio says "ops-first":**

```
Hey Carlos - not looking for a reply, just something worth passing along.

It seems like at your stage the handoffs between tools are usually the first
thing that starts quietly eating the team's time - not the tools themselves.
```

## Touch 3 — Day 5 — Soft intro, named-peer proof, and anchored Teardown ask

**What it does:** names who Aleem is and what he does, drops one named-peer
result, and makes the one ask in the sequence — anchored to the Ops Teardown
deliverable. The Teardown framing keeps it low-pressure even though it's a call
ask: the prospect gets value (the first thing Aleem would automate) just by
showing up.

**Structure:**
1. **Honest opener** — "I'll be straight" or "I've been roundabout so here it is."
   Signals you're done being subtle. People respect this.
2. **One-line NexusPoint intro** — what Aleem does, framed around their specific
   problem type.
3. **One named peer from the proof bank** — the single closest match to their
   pain (Steve / Andrey / Mikey from `sales-playbook/offer/proof-bank.md`). Lead
   with the peer + the hours-saved number. No clean match → anonymized fallback
   from the same file.
4. **Anchored Teardown ask** — "Want a 20-min Ops Teardown? I look at your stack
   and tell you the first thing I'd automate, you decide if it's worth building."
   Never "worth a quick DM chat" / "hop on a call".

**Hard rules:**
- Exactly one peer. Never list multiple.
- Lead with the named peer + the number, not adjectives.
- The ask is the anchored Ops Teardown, framed low-pressure. Never a bare
  "quick chat" or calendar-link dump.
- Under 400 chars
- No sign-off

**Example — e-comm founder, continuing with Maya (Shopify pain, no clean named peer → anonymized DTC fallback):**

```
Hey Maya - I'll be straight. I run NexusPoint, we take the manual ops work off
founders' plates.

Built a Shopify reconciliation workflow for a DTC brand that killed 4 hrs of
Sunday recon a week.

Want a 20-min Ops Teardown? I look at your stack and tell you the first thing
I'd automate, you decide if it's worth it.
```

**Example — agency ops lead (Andrey is a clean named-peer match):**

```
Hey Marcus - I've been roundabout so here it is. I run NexusPoint, we take the
manual ops work off agency plates.

Built the lead routing + client reporting for Andrey (agency operator) - 10+
hrs/week back.

Want a 20-min Ops Teardown? I screen-share your stack and show you the first
thing I'd automate.
```

## Touch 4 — Day 7 or 14 — Honest exit

**What it does:** closes the loop cleanly. Signals you're done reaching out. No
guilt, no fake urgency, no last-ditch pitch. Leaves the door open in a way that
feels genuine rather than performative.

**Structure:**
1. **Exit signal** — "will stop reaching out" / "last one from me" / "won't keep
   going" — honest, low-pressure.
2. **Soft door open** — framed around their specific pain or project. Not generic
   ("feel free to reach out anytime") — specific to what they're building.
3. **Optional genuine closer** — something real about what they're doing. Skip it
   entirely if there's nothing genuinely worth saying. Fabricated warmth lands worse
   than nothing.

**Hard rules:**
- No call ask in Touch 4 — this is an exit, not a CTA
- No guilt-framing
- Genuine closer only if it's actually earned — don't force it
- Under 300 chars ideal
- No sign-off

**Example — Maya, final:**

```
Hey Maya - will stop reaching out, no hard feelings.

If automating the Shopify side ever makes it onto the list, I'm easy to find.
What you're building looks like it's heading somewhere real.
```

**Example — Carlos, final:**

```
Hey Carlos - last one from me.

If cutting those handoffs ever becomes a priority, I'm around. What you're
running at Northwind looks like it has legs.
```

## If Aleem asks for all 3 touches at once

Output Touch 2, Touch 3, Touch 4 in order with day-cadence labels at the top of
each. Each at its own char limit. Make them read as a coherent arc:
- Touch 3 should echo the pain space Touch 2 labeled (without repeating exact words)
- Touch 4 should reference the specific context without being repetitive
- Vary the opening line across all three — don't start all three the same way

## Common failure modes

| Mistake | Why it kills the message |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Over 400 chars | Instagram is mobile. Long DMs get skimmed or ignored. |
| "Just following up" / "bumping this" | Instant BDR smell. The goal is to feel like a peer, not a sales sequence. |
| NexusPoint in Touch 2 | Touch 2 is pure value. Naming the company too early triggers sales-mode. |
| Unanchored ask ("worth a quick chat?") | Burned line, instant sales-smell. Anchor the Touch 3 ask to the Ops Teardown deliverable. |
| Multiple peers in Touch 3 | One named peer lands. Three feel like a pitch deck. |
| Emojis or em dashes | Breaks the founder-peer tone. |
| Generic closer in Touch 4 | If you can't say something genuine about what they're building, leave it out. |
| Repeating phrasing from previous touch | Signals copy-paste, not thought. |
| Sign-off like "- Aleem" | Instagram DMs don't sign off. Feels stiff. |
