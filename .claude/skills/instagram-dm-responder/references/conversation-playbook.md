# Conversation Playbook

Loaded in Scenario B (live reply drafter). This is how to read an active Instagram
DM conversation and pick the right next move.

The core principle: **rapport before pitch, rapport before call.** Instagram is a
human channel first. The conversion path is two steps — genuine DM conversation,
then a call — not a straight funnel from "they replied" to "book 20 minutes."

Skipping phases feels pushy. Staying too long in one feels aimless. The goal is to
move one phase forward per message.

**Instagram-specific:** messages are read on a phone, in between other things.
Match their register. If they're casual ("lol yeah that's exactly it"), stay casual
back. Don't corporate-tone someone who's texting you informally. Under 400 chars
per reply.

---

## The 6 phases

### 1. Open

**They look like:** replied with something short, non-committal, or curious.
"Haha yeah." "What do you do?" "Interesting." "lol facts." No disclosed pain yet.

**Your move:** a calibrated question. Open-ended, starts with *how* or *what*,
impossible to answer yes/no. Pulls them into talking about their own situation —
not yours.

Examples:
- "What's the part of the business eating the most of your week right now?"
- "How are you handling [thing their bio suggests] at [company] these days?"
- "What does actually building this look like day-to-day?"

**Do not:** pitch, drop a result, or ask for a call. Nothing is earned yet.

---

### 2. Label

**They look like:** disclosed something. Even small. "Yeah our ops are a mess."
"Honestly we're figuring it out as we go." "It's been rough." "We're still doing
everything manually." That's the crack.

**Your move:** a Voss label. Start with *It sounds like...*, *It looks like...*,
*It seems like...*, *It feels like...* — never "I think" or "I believe" (those
make it about you). Mirror their reality back in slightly sharper language than
they used.

Examples:
- "It sounds like the ops side is the part that hasn't caught up to the pace yet."
- "It looks like you're still the connective tissue between all the moving parts."
- "It seems like the tools are there but the handoffs between them are the expensive part."

**Instagram note:** people here often reply with "lol exactly" or "yeah that's it"
or "facts." That's not disengagement — that's confirmation. You've landed the label.
Move forward.

**Do not:** follow the label with a pitch in the same message. Let it land. End on
the label or a short calibrated follow-up question.

---

### 3. Deepen

**They look like:** confirmed the label and kept talking — shared more detail,
asked a question, showed they're comfortable.

**Your move:** stay curious. More calibrated questions. More labels. Ask about
specifics. Get them to describe the actual problem in their own words.

This is where Instagram diverges from a stricter sales cadence — spend real time
here. The call is two steps away. The goal right now is for them to feel like
you actually get their situation. That's what makes the proof land later.

Examples:
- "What's the piece that takes the most time day-to-day on that?"
- "It sounds like [refined version of their pain] — is that the bit that's costing you the most?"
- "How long has it been running like that?"

**Do not:** pitch. Do not drop proof yet. You're still building the foundation.

---

### 4. Proof

**They look like:** asked what you do, asked how you'd fix it, or asked a specific
question about your work.

**Your move:** one result from the results bank. The single closest match to the
pain they described. One. Not a list.

- Their pain: manual onboarding → "Built a client onboarding pipeline that cut manual work by 70%."
- Their pain: lead follow-up → "Built a lead-to-outreach pipeline that cut follow-up time by 80% and removed 3 handoffs."
- Their pain: CRM/email lag → "Built an AI email + CRM workflow that cut response time from hours to minutes."

Frame it as a story, not a stat dump. "Built X for a team like yours, it did Y" lands
better than "our solution achieves Y."

**Do not:** ask for the call immediately after proof. Let them pull.

---

### 5. Warm Ask

**They look like:** engaged with the result. Asked how long it took. Asked what
tools. Asked if you've done it for their industry. Said "how would that work for us?"

**Your move:** a low-friction next step. Not a calendar link. Not "let's hop on a
call." Something easier:

- "Want me to send you a quick breakdown of how that pipeline was built?"
- "I could walk you through it here in DMs if that's useful."
- "Worth keeping this convo going? Happy to go deeper."

**Why this step exists:** on Instagram, jumping from "they showed interest" to
"book 20 minutes" skips a rapport step the channel requires. The warm ask gets
them more engaged before the ask escalates. Once they say yes to the warm ask,
the call ask has a much higher hit rate.

**Do not ask for a call at this phase.** Even if they seem interested. Let them
take the warm ask first.

---

### 6. Call

**They look like:** engaged with the warm ask OR gave a clear "let's talk" signal
("I'd love to see this," "when could we talk," "are you free this week," "what does
starting look like").

**Your move:** a no-oriented, permission-based call ask. No-oriented questions
start with *Would it be off base...*, *Is it a bad idea...*, *Would it be crazy...*
They give the feeling of control — saying no is easy, which paradoxically makes yes easy.

- "Would it be off base to grab 20 minutes so I can show you what that pipeline looks like end-to-end?"
- "Is it a bad idea to hop on a quick call and map this against your specific setup?"
- "Would it be crazy to do a short 20-min call this week and see if there's a fit?"

**Do not:** apologize, hedge, or give them 3 time options in the first ask. One
sentence, one clean question.

---

## How to read where they are

| What they said | Phase you're entering |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Short positive / "what do you do?" / casual acknowledgment | **Open** — calibrated question |
| Disclosed a pain, constraint, or frustration | **Label** — mirror it sharper |
| Confirmed the label and kept talking | **Deepen** — stay curious, more questions |
| Asked what you do or how you'd fix it | **Proof** — one result |
| Engaged with the result, asked specifics | **Warm Ask** — low-friction next step |
| Clear "let's talk" or engaged with warm ask | **Call** — no-oriented ask |

If unsure between two phases, pick the earlier one. Slower is almost always safer
on Instagram.

## Special cases

### Peer / agency owner / AI consultant / builder

If they do what Aleem does — run an agency, consult on AI, build automations — they
are almost never a direct client. They're a **referral partner or potential
collaborator.** Pivot immediately.

- Don't pitch services
- Treat them as a peer
- Ask about their build-side setup: "Do you handle all the technical builds yourself
  or bring in specialists for that?" / "How are you set up — do you partner out or
  keep it in-house?"

This is still the Open phase — but on the peer track.

### They replied casually ("lol yeah that's it")

Don't stiffen up. Match their register. Stay human. "It sounds like..." works here
too, but keep it conversational. The label doesn't have to be formal to land.

### They replied to your Story

Warmer entry — they already engaged with your content. Acknowledge what they
reacted to before asking anything. "Glad that one landed — what does it look like
on your end?" works better here than jumping straight to a calibrated question.

### They disclosed pain then went quiet

Don't chase with a pitch. Send a label-only message. Labels pull responses far
more reliably than questions do because the person feels compelled to correct or
confirm them. "It sounds like [what they described] is still sitting unresolved"
works better than "hey, still interested?"

### They went cold mid-thread

Don't re-open with "just checking in." Re-enter with something useful — a short
observation or pattern grounded in their situation — and soft-ask if it resonates.
That's a re-entry to whichever phase they last engaged with.

---

## Rules in every message

- **Match their register.** Casual in, casual back. Short in, short back.
- **At least one label per message.** Even in Proof and Warm Ask. The label is what
  keeps it feeling human rather than transactional.
- **Never mention price, rate, or "how much."** That conversation starts on the call.
- **No corporate vocabulary.** No "circle back," "touch base," "reach out," "leverage,"
  "synergies," "bandwidth."
- **No em dashes. No emojis.**
- **Under 400 chars.** Instagram is mobile. Respect the context.
- **The meta-line (Phase + Tactic) is for Aleem only.** Never paste it into Instagram.

## Worked micro-examples (Instagram register)

**Them:** "Ha yeah what do you actually do?"
**You (Open):** "Basically I build automations that take the manual ops work off
founders' plates as they grow. Curious - what's the part of the business eating
the most of your time right now?"

**Them:** "lol honestly onboarding clients is still a mess, everything's manual"
**You (Label):** "It sounds like every new client still costs the same amount of
your time as the first one - like the process hasn't turned into a system yet."

**Them:** "yeah exactly, we're still doing it all on Notion and Slack DMs lol"
**You (Deepen):** "It seems like the tribal knowledge is the part that's keeping
it manual - what's the step that takes the longest each time?"

**Them:** "probably the setup calls, like explaining the same stuff every time"
**You (Proof):** "Built a client onboarding pipeline for a similar team that cut
that manual work by 70% - the core move was turning those setup calls into a
system the tool runs instead."

**Them:** "interesting, how long did something like that take?"
**You (Warm Ask):** "About 3 weeks. Happy to walk you through how it was built here
in DMs if that's useful - easier to show than explain."

**Them:** "yeah that'd be great actually"
**You (Call):** "Would it be off base to grab 20 minutes on a call so I can show
you the whole thing end-to-end and see if the same shape fits your setup?"

Six messages. Real rapport first. Call ask lands after they've already said yes to
a smaller step.
