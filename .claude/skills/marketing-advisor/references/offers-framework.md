# Offers Framework — Marketing Reference

Distilled from Alex Hormozi's $100M Offers. Use this to craft offers, position services, write outreach, ads, and content.

---

## Value Equation

```
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

| Lever | Direction | How to Move It |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Dream Outcome | Maximize | Sell the result, not the deliverable. "40 qualified leads/month" not "a new website" |
| Perceived Likelihood | Maximize | Case studies, guarantees, track record, specificity |
| Time Delay | Minimize | Faster results, quick wins, phased delivery with early ROI |
| Effort & Sacrifice | Minimize | Done-for-you beats DIY. Turnkey, minimal client involvement |

Every piece of marketing should reinforce at least one lever.

## Starving Crowd — Market Selection

Before crafting an offer, validate the market against four indicators:

| Indicator | Test |
|-----------|------|
| **Massive Pain** | They desperately need the solution (bleeding-neck problem, not a nice-to-have) |
| **Purchasing Power** | They can afford premium prices |
| **Easy to Target** | You can find and reach them (specific industries, job titles, platforms) |
| **Growing Market** | The market is expanding, not shrinking |

If your market fails any of these, pivot the niche before optimizing the offer.

## Grand Slam Offer Methodology

A Grand Slam Offer is so distinct, valuable, and risk-free it operates in a "category of one." No comparison shopping possible.

**How to build one:**
1. List every obstacle, hesitation, and problem the client faces before, during, and after working with you
2. Turn each problem into a specific, named solution or deliverable
3. Stack these solutions into a monolithic offer that makes hourly rates and generic retainers look foolish
4. Wrap in psychological triggers (scarcity, urgency, bonuses, guarantees)
5. Name it using the MAGIC formula

## Trim & Stack Process

1. **List all problems** across four friction categories:

| Category | Client Fear | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| Time | "How long will this take?" | Slow delivery, delayed ROI |
| Money | "What if I waste my investment?" | Budget risk, unclear ROI |
| Effort | "How much work do I have to do?" | Complex onboarding, constant meetings |
| Fear | "What if it doesn't work?" | No guarantee, past bad experiences |

2. **Create named solutions** for each friction (branded, outcome-focused names)
3. **Trim** anything that is high cost to deliver but low perceived value
4. **Stack** remaining items into Core Offer + Bonuses
5. **Assign standalone market value** to each item (what it costs separately)
6. **Total value** should be 10x the price

## Premium Pricing Psychology

**The Virtuous Cycle:**
- Higher prices increase client emotional investment and commitment
- Higher prices increase perceived value of your service
- Higher margins let you deliver exceptional, white-glove experiences
- Better experiences generate better case studies
- Better case studies let you charge even more

**Pricing rules:**
- Price at 1/10th of total value stack
- Never lead with price. Build value first.
- Frame as investment, not cost ("Your investment" not "the price")
- Include ROI math: "10 leads/month x $5K deal = $50K/month. Pays for itself in 2 weeks."
- Offer 2-3 tiers when appropriate. Anchor with the highest tier first.

## Scarcity, Urgency, Bonuses, Guarantees

### Scarcity (limit supply)
- "We take on 3 new clients per month to ensure quality"
- Must be genuine. Never fabricate.

### Urgency (limit time)
- "This pricing/bonus is only valid if we start by [date]"
- "Starting now means results before [event/quarter]"

### Bonuses (add value, never discount)
- Instead of lowering price, add high-perceived-value items that cost little to deliver
- Each bonus needs: Name, Retail Value, Benefit (one sentence), Why it's free

### Guarantees (reverse the risk)

| Type | How It Works | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Unconditional | Full refund, no questions, within X days | Building trust with new clients |
| Conditional | If we don't hit [metric] by [date], we [remedy] | Performance-based projects |
| Service | We work for free until [result] achieved | High-confidence engagements |
| Anti-guarantee | "This isn't for everyone" (exclusivity) | Premium positioning |

Stack two guarantees when possible. Name them ("The Results-or-Free Promise").

## MAGIC Naming Formula

| Letter | Element | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **M** | Magnetic Reason Why | "The Q1..." (timely trigger) |
| **A** | Avatar | "...for SaaS Founders..." |
| **G** | Goal | "...Revenue Acceleration..." |
| **I** | Interval | "...90-Day..." |
| **C** | Container | "...System" / "...Blueprint" / "...Framework" |

**Full example:** "The Q1 Revenue Acceleration 90-Day System for SaaS Founders"

## "So That" Method

Chain deliverables through "so that..." until you reach the real business outcome. Never stop at the deliverable.

> We redesign your website **so that** visitors convert at 3x the current rate **so that** you generate 40+ qualified leads/month **so that** your sales team closes $200K in new revenue this quarter.

**Rules:**
- Chain at least 3 levels deep
- End with a measurable business result (revenue, time saved, cost reduced)
- Include a timeframe ("in 90 days", "by Q2")
- Use the final "so that" as your headline promise

## Application to Marketing Contexts

### Outreach (cold email, DMs)
- Lead with the Dream Outcome, not your service name
- Use "So That" chains in subject lines and opening lines
- Reference their specific pain (Starving Crowd criteria)
- Include one proof element (case study, metric) to boost Perceived Likelihood

### Ads
- Hook = Dream Outcome or pain point (Value Equation top half)
- Body = Named solution + proof
- CTA = Low-friction next step + urgency lever

### Content / Social
- Teach using the Value Equation as a framework (positions you as expert)
- Share "So That" transformations as case studies
- Use MAGIC-style naming for lead magnets and free resources
- Bonuses and guarantees work as content hooks ("Why we guarantee results or work free")

### Proposals
- Structure the entire proposal around the Value Stack
- Open with Dream Outcome, not deliverables
- Present price after full stack is revealed
- Close with guarantee + scarcity + single CTA
