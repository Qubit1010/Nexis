# $100M Leads Framework -- Reference Guide

Source: Alex Hormozi, $100M Leads. Distilled for tactical execution.

---

## Core Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Lead | A person you can contact |
| Engaged Lead | A person you can contact who has shown interest in the stuff you sell |
| Advertising | The process of making people aware of your stuff |
| Lead Magnet | A free offer exchanged for contact info that solves a narrow problem |
| Goodwill | The gap between price charged and value received |

---

## The Core Four Framework

Four ways to let people know about your stuff. No others exist.

| Method | Type | Audience | Cost | Speed | Control |
|--------|------|----------|------|-------|---------|
| Warm Outreach | 1-to-1 | Warm (knows you) | Free | Fastest | High |
| Content Posting | 1-to-many | Warm (follows you) | Free | Slow compound | Medium |
| Cold Outreach | 1-to-1 | Cold (strangers) | Low | Medium | Highest |
| Paid Ads | 1-to-many | Cold (strangers) | High | Fast | Medium |

**When to use which:**
- Starting from zero / no budget: Warm Outreach first
- Have audience but no direct sales: Content Posting
- Need predictable, scalable pipeline: Cold Outreach
- Already converting organically and have cash: Paid Ads
- Never start with Paid Ads until your offer converts through cheaper methods

## Rule of 100

Commit to 100 units of lead-gen activity every single day for 100 days. Pick ONE channel.

| Channel | Daily 100 |
|---------|-----------|
| Warm Outreach | 100 personal reach-outs |
| Cold Outreach | 100 cold emails/DMs |
| Content | 100 minutes creating and posting |
| Paid Ads | $100/day ad spend |

The math guarantees engaged leads if you sustain volume. Consistency beats intensity.

## Lead Magnets

**Golden Rule:** Your free stuff must be better than competitors' paid stuff.

### Three Types

| Type | Mechanism | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Reveal a Problem | Diagnosis that exposes pain | Free website speed/SEO audit |
| Give a Sample | Full but brief access to your offer | Free trial, small custom deliverable |
| One Step of Multi-Step | Give strategy free, charge for execution | Free automation blueprint, paid build |

### 7-Step Creation Process

1. **Pick a narrow problem** -- solve it free, reveal bigger problem your core offer solves ("salty pretzel" effect)
2. **Choose solution type** -- Reveal problem, Sample/Trial, or One Step
3. **Pick delivery format** -- Software (calculators, templates), Information (guides, videos), Services (audits, calls), Physical (books, samples)
4. **Test the name** -- Headline is most important element. Test headlines, images, subheadlines
5. **Make it easy to consume** -- Multiple formats (audio, text, video), minimize effort
6. **Make it excellent** -- Give away secrets, sell implementation. No fluff.
7. **Strong CTA** -- Tell them exactly what to do next. Use scarcity (limited supply), urgency (limited time), or a reason ("because it's my birthday")

### Lead Magnet Math
Even if fulfillment costs $25/lead, the drastically lower CAC makes it profitable. More people engage with free value than paid pitches.

## More, Better, New Sequence

**Always follow this order. Never skip ahead.**

| Phase | Action | When |
|-------|--------|------|
| **MORE** | Increase volume of what already works | First always. 10x before optimizing. |
| **BETTER** | Optimize the funnel, test one variable per week per platform | When more volume hits diminishing returns |
| **NEW** | Add new placement, then platform, then Core Four method | Only when current channel is truly maxed |

### "New" Expansion Order (9 out of 10 times)
1. New placements on existing platform (e.g., add Reels to existing Instagram posts)
2. New platforms with existing format (e.g., move short-form to YouTube Shorts)
3. New Core Four activity entirely (e.g., add cold outreach team on top of paid ads)

### Testing Rules
- Test exactly ONE thing per week per platform
- Find the constraint (the step where most leads drop off)
- Fix that single step for biggest ROI
- Log results, try to beat the new "best"

## Lead Getters (Scaling Through Leverage)

When your time becomes the bottleneck, get others to get leads for you.

| Lead Getter | How They Help | When to Deploy |
|-------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Customers** | Referrals, word-of-mouth | When product generates goodwill (25%+ from referrals = healthy) |
| **Employees** | Run your Core Four systems | When you've maxed personal capacity |
| **Agencies** | Manage ads/outreach, teach you new methods | To learn new platforms fast (6-month contracts) |
| **Affiliates** | Promote to their audience for commission | When you have a proven offer and want exponential scale |

## Warm Outreach -- 10-Step Playbook

1. **Get your list** -- Phone contacts, email, social media. You likely have 1,000+ leads already.
2. **Pick a platform** -- Start where you have the most contacts
3. **Personalize** -- Reference something real about the person. Pay social dues.
4. **Reach out 100/day** -- Contact each person up to 3x (once/day for 3 days) or until they respond
5. **Warm them up (ACA Framework):**
   - **A**cknowledge: Restate what they said (active listening)
   - **C**ompliment: Tie their response to a positive character trait
   - **A**sk: Ask a question steering toward your offer topic
6. **Indirect ask** -- Never pitch directly. Ask: "Do you know anyone who struggles with [specific problem]?"
7. **Offer free work first** -- Help first 5 people FREE in exchange for feedback + testimonial
8. **Cycle platforms** -- After exhausting one, move to the next largest contact list
9. **Start charging** -- When free clients refer friends, transition to discounted. Raise price 20% per 5 clients.
10. **Keep list warm** -- 9-word re-engagement email: "Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?"

## Cold Outreach -- Execution Framework

### Three Problems to Solve (in order)

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Can't contact them | Build targeted lists via software, brokers, or manual scraping |
| They ignore you | Personalize (make cold look warm) + give big fast value (lead magnet) |
| They aren't interested | Massive volume + multi-channel follow-up |

### Volume & Follow-Up Rules
- Use automation tools for templated emails, pre-recorded voicemails, auto-dialers
- Follow up multiple times across multiple channels (email, phone, text, DM)
- If no reply to email, call to follow up on the email
- After exhausting the list, wait 3-6 months and restart -- circumstances change

### 7 Benefits of Cold Outreach
1. No content or ads needed -- refine one perfect message
2. Invisible to competitors -- total secrecy
3. Highly reliable -- predictable inputs = predictable outputs
4. Fewer platform algorithm changes
5. Easier compliance -- no ad account bans
6. Sellable business -- doesn't need your face
7. Hard to copy -- scraping, personalization, hiring build a moat

## Content Posting Framework

### Content Unit (every piece must have all three)

| Component | Purpose | Tactics |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Hook** | Grab attention | Recency, relevancy, conflict, unusual. Format for platform. |
| **Retain** | Keep attention | Curiosity via Lists, Steps, Stories |
| **Reward** | Deliver value | High value per second. Satisfy the hook's promise. |

### Give:Ask Ratio
- "Give-give-give until they ask." Give in public, ask in private.
- **Integrated asks:** 58.5 min of value, 1.5 min ask (in every piece)
- **Intermittent asks:** 10 pure value posts, 1 promotional post

### Scaling Content (Width then Depth)
1. Post regularly on one platform
2. Before maxing volume, add next platform while maintaining first
3. Continue until on all relevant platforms
4. Then maximize volume across all simultaneously (repurpose easily)

### 7 Content Rules
1. Switch from "How to" to "How I" -- share experience, not lectures
2. Repeat core messages -- people need reminding more than teaching
3. Puddles > Ponds > Lakes > Oceans -- start hyper-niche, expand later
4. Best content becomes sales tools for your team
5. Free content retains paying customers (they factor it into ROI)
6. No "short attention spans" -- only "boring content." People binge 8-hour Netflix shows.
7. Avoid pre-scheduling -- manual posting forces tighter feedback loops

## Paid Ads Framework

### Ad Structure (3 mandatory parts)
1. **Call Out** -- Grab attention. Use cocktail party effect: call out specific trait/problem/identifier
2. **Value** -- Show benefits massive, costs tiny
3. **CTA** -- Spell out exact next step. Make it frictionless.

### Platform Selection Criteria
- You've used it as a consumer
- You can target your ideal audience
- You understand its ad format
- You have minimum budget to place an ad

### Three Phases of Spending

| Phase | Goal | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| Track Money | Set up measurement | Proper tracking before spending $1 |
| Lose Money | Find winners | Budget 2x first-30-day cash collected per customer. Test ads. |
| Print Money | Scale winners | Spend as much as possible on proven ads. Reverse-engineer daily budget from sales goals. |

### Key Metrics
- **LTGP** (Lifetime Gross Profit) > LTV as the metric that matters
- **3:1 Rule:** Healthy business = LTGP is 3x CAC
- **Client-Financed Acquisition:** Get customers to pay back acquisition cost within 30 days via upsells

### Landing Page Rule
Landing page must match the ad exactly -- design, language, promise. Continuous experience from click to close.

### Three Lessons
1. Don't confuse sales problems with advertising problems (if leads are qualified but not buying, fix sales)
2. Best organic content often makes best paid ads (especially UGC/testimonials)
3. Stop telling yourself you "suck at tech" -- spend $100 to place one ad and learn

## Customer Referrals Framework

### Why Referrals Win
- Worth more (higher LTGP -- buy more, buy frequently, pay upfront)
- Cost less (zero CAC)
- Scale exponentially (1 brings 2, 2 bring 4)

### Two Reasons Businesses Don't Get Referrals
1. Product isn't as good as they think
2. They never actually ask

### 6 Ways to Build Goodwill (Drive Referrals)
1. Sell to perfect-fit customers only
2. Set expectations low, overdeliver
3. Find what top customers did, force all new customers to do the same
4. Stack quick wins early to increase speed perception
5. Use surveys to eliminate friction points
6. Keep selling them more so they stay engaged and keep referring

### 7 Ways to Ask for Referrals
1. **One-sided benefit** -- Pay your CAC to referrer as cash or give friend a discount
2. **Two-sided benefit** -- Split CAC between referrer and friend
3. **Ask at purchase** -- "Who do you want to do this with?" (people get better results with a friend)
4. **Negotiation chip** -- Cut price in exchange for 3-way text intro to 3 friends
5. **Referral events** -- 1-4 week limited promotions with points/prizes for referrals
6. **Ongoing program** -- Promote referral benefits across all marketing
7. **Unlockable bonuses** -- VIP courses, premium support, merch for referral + testimonial

## Employees -- Scaling Lead Gen

### Training Framework: The 3 Ds
1. **Document** -- Step-by-step checklist of how you get leads. Test by following it yourself blindly.
2. **Demonstrate** -- Do the task in front of them following the checklist
3. **Duplicate** -- They do it in front of you following the checklist

### Training Rules
- Praise following directions, not results (fix checklist if directions are right but result is wrong)
- Feedback one step at a time, no punishment during training
- If they're confused, your checklist is broken

### Employee ROI Formula
Total Payroll / Total Engaged Leads = Cost per lead. If 1 in 10 buys, that's your CAC. Profitable if LTGP >= 3x CAC.

## Agencies -- How to Use Them Right

**Purpose:** Learn new methods and platforms, not outsource forever.

### The Approach
- Set a 6-month engagement with explicit learning goal
- Pay extra for them to explain their decision-making
- Train internal team on what you learn
- Transition to consulting arrangement once team is capable

### 10-Point Agency Selection Checklist
Word of mouth, track record, waitlist, clear expectations, no short-term hacks, clear requirements, regular communication, simple tracking, good offer structure, expensive (but expensive alone doesn't mean good)

## Affiliates -- 6-Step Framework

1. **Find them** -- "Who's got my leads?" Target businesses serving your ideal clients
2. **Make an offer** -- Fast, simple commissions + new value for their clients without extra work
3. **Qualify them** -- Make them a customer or charge for certification/training
4. **Pay structure** -- Tiered: 25% CAC (signup), 50% CAC (activated), 100% CAC (sustained performance)
5. **Launch them** -- Provide exact promotional materials. Use whisper-tease-shout to build anticipation.
6. **Integrate them** -- Three levels: give away your lead magnet, sell your lead magnet (keep cash), sell your core offer (split profits)

## The Advertising Roadmap (Zero to $100M)

| Level | Stage | Primary Action |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 1 | Friends know what you sell | Warm outreach, one offer, one avatar, one platform |
| 2 | Everyone you know knows | Max personal capacity on warm outreach + content |
| 3 | Employees help advertise | Hire people to advertise profitably on your behalf |
| 4 | Product drives referrals | Focus on product until 25%+ customers come from word-of-mouth |
| 5 | Multiple channels working | Advertise profitably via 2+ methods on multiple platforms |
| 6 | Hire veteran leaders | Executives run departments for each advertising method |
| 7 | $1B+ | Keep rolling the die |

## One-Page Advertising Checklist

1. Pick your target lead type (Customers, Affiliates, Employees, or Agencies)
2. Choose Rule of 100 or Open to Goal framework
3. Fill out daily advertising checklist
4. Execute daily until you can afford to hire someone to do it
5. Go back to Step 1, but now target Employees as your lead type

**Open to Goal:** Instead of committing to 100 daily actions, commit to working until you hit a specific number of outcomes (e.g., 5 new customers), regardless of how long it takes.

## Key Formulas

| Formula | Calculation |
|---------|------------|
| CAC | Total ad spend / Total customers acquired |
| LTGP | All revenue from a customer - cost to deliver |
| Healthy ratio | LTGP >= 3x CAC |
| Employee ROI | Total payroll / Total engaged leads = Cost per lead |
| Break-even test | First 30-day cash collected >= CAC (client-financed acquisition) |
