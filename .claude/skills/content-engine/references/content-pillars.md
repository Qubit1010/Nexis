# Content Pillars

Aleem's personal brand revolves around 4 pillars. Every piece should map clearly to one.
When scoring opportunity, "strong pillar fit" means the topic sits squarely in the pillar's
core territory, "moderate" means adjacent, "off-brand" means it doesn't serve Aleem's
audience or goals.

**Intent tag:** each pillar is tagged **Conversion** (drives inbound/leads) or **Awareness**
(reach/relatability). Conversion-leaning formats and angles score higher on the new
conversion-potential dimension (see SKILL.md ideate scoring).

**Two high-converting angle types** apply across pillars wherever the topic allows (use them
when relevant - they drive business outcomes, not just reads):
- **Comparison / "X vs Y"** - converts ~3.2x feature/overview content.
- **Customer-voiced story** - a real outcome in the client's own words, ~3:1 over polished case studies.

**Brand rule (all pillars):** content NEVER names the agency or references
university/degree/BSAI/student status. Pillars guide topic selection; the output stays inside
this rule. Keywords below are for blog SEO/internal routing, not phrases that must appear.

## Pillar 1: AI & Automation  — Intent: Conversion

**Core territory:** Claude Code, n8n, LangChain, RAG, agent systems, workflow automation,
OpenAI/Anthropic APIs, LangGraph, agentic AI, Python automation, MCP servers.

**Angle types:**
- Automation teardown: "The exact workflow I use to automate [task]" (high conversion)
- Tool comparison / "X vs Y": "Claude Code vs [alternative] for [use case]" (high conversion)
- Tutorial ("How I"): "How I built X with n8n / LangChain"
- System design: "How I architect AI agents for [outcome]"
- Customer-voiced story: a client's result in their own words

**Platform strength:** Blog (deep teardowns, comparisons), LinkedIn (doc carousels, frameworks),
Instagram (demo Reels, before/after carousels).

**Keywords:** AI automation, Claude Code, n8n workflow, LangChain, agentic AI, AI agent,
workflow automation, Python automation.

## Pillar 2: Founder Journey  — Intent: Conversion + Awareness

**Core territory:** client acquisition, the freelancer-to-agency path, running a team, closing
deals, offer positioning, cold outreach, real business lessons from running a services business young.

**Angle types:**
- Customer-voiced story / client win: "How we delivered [outcome] for [type of client]" (high conversion, keep specific, no agency name)
- Lessons learned: "What I'd do differently scoping projects today"
- Behind the scenes: "The exact process I use to scope and close a project"
- Founder story: "How I went from [A] to [B]"

**Platform strength:** LinkedIn (credibility with potential clients), Instagram (relatable,
behind-the-scenes), Blog (case studies with real detail).

**Keywords:** client acquisition, freelance to agency, AI services, agency operations,
offer positioning, B2B services, cold outreach. (Do not surface the agency name in output.)

## Pillar 3: Tech Insights  — Intent: Awareness

**Core territory:** AI model releases and what they actually mean, industry trends with a
business angle, emerging-tech analysis, market observations, opinion takes on where AI is going.

**Angle types:**
- Opinion / take: "What [new AI development] actually means for [audience]"
- Comparison / "X vs Y": "[Model A] vs [Model B] for [real use case]" (high conversion)
- Data-backed analysis: "The numbers behind [AI trend]"
- News commentary: "[Release] + what I'd actually do with it"
- Prediction/observation: "The AI shift nobody is talking about"

**Platform strength:** LinkedIn (opinion + carousels), Blog (long-form analysis), Instagram
(carousels for "X things about [trend]").

**Keywords:** AI trends, AI for business, emerging technology, AI strategy, model comparison,
AI market, agentic AI.

## Pillar 4: Young Builder / Learning in Public  — Intent: Awareness

**Core territory:** building real products and a business young, without waiting for permission
or credentials. Learning in public. The gap between clean theory and what you actually need to
ship in production. Proof that you can build for real clients early.

**Angle types:**
- Learning in public: "I tried to learn [X] this week, here's what actually happened"
- Theory vs production: "The textbook version of [X] is clean. Shipping it for a real client, [what broke]"
- Relatable insight: "What no course prepares you for when you ship to a paying client"
- Doing it young: "You don't need [credential/permission] to build something real, here's proof"

> Reframed from the old "Student Builder" pillar to comply with the no-academia rule: keep the
> relatable young/self-taught/learning-in-public angle, drop all university / BSAI / degree /
> "student" framing.

**Platform strength:** Instagram (highly relatable to young builders/early founders), LinkedIn
(differentiation - most agency operators aren't doing this young).

**Keywords:** young founder, learning in public, self-taught developer, building in public,
shipping real products, indie builder.
