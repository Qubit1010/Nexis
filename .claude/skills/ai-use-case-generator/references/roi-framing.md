# ROI Framing Reference — by Industry

Use these benchmarks to anchor ROI estimates in use cases. Always prefer ranges over point estimates. When combining AI with automation, the upper end of ranges is more defensible.

---

## E-commerce / Retail

**Common pain points:** customer support volume, inventory tracking, abandoned cart recovery, returns processing, product description writing

| Automation | Typical ROI |
|---|---|
| AI customer support chatbot | Deflects 40-60% of tickets; saves 15-25 hours/week for teams of 5-10 |
| Abandoned cart recovery flows | 5-15% recovery rate on abandoned carts (industry avg is 8%) |
| Inventory reorder alerts | Reduces stockouts by 20-30%; saves 3-5 hours/week of manual monitoring |
| AI product description generation | Cuts copy time from 15 min to 2 min per SKU |
| Returns processing automation | Reduces resolution time from 3-5 days to same-day for standard cases |

**Revenue hook angles:** "Most e-commerce brands leave 8-12% of potential revenue on the table from abandoned carts alone..."

## General framing principles

1. **Lead with time, follow with money** — "saves 10 hours/week" is more visceral than "$2,000/month saved" even if they're equivalent. Use both.
2. **Anchor to their specific numbers** — if you know team size, multiply: "8 hours/week × 3 admin staff = 24 hours reclaimed per week"
3. **Use industry loss framing where strong** — "the average clinic loses $X/year to no-shows" hits harder than "reduces no-shows"
4. **Avoid claiming AI replaces staff** — frame as "frees up your team to focus on X" rather than "eliminates the need for Y"
5. **Be conservative and honest** — if you're uncertain, say "typically" or "in similar businesses we've seen" — it's more credible than false precision
