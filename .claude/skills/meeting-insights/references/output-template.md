# Meeting Insights — Output Template

Defines the structure, section order, formatting rules, and handling for every output
file generated by the meeting-insights skill. Follow this exactly. Sections with no
content are omitted entirely — no placeholders, no "None".

---

## File Header

```markdown
# [Meeting Name]

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Attendees:** [Name, Name, Name]
**Client / Project:** [Name]
**Duration:** [e.g., 45 min]
```

Omit `Client / Project` line if not applicable (internal-only meeting with no client
context). Omit `Duration` if not stated anywhere in the transcript. Date and attendees
are required — if attendees have no names, list speaker labels (Speaker A, Speaker B)
or "unknown" if completely unlabeled.

---

## Section 1: Client Problem / Pain Points

**Header:** `## Client Problem / Pain Points`

**What goes here:** Problems, frustrations, or pain points the client described,
implied, or confirmed. Operational friction, cost, time loss, things breaking down.
This is why the conversation was happening.

**Format:** Bulleted list. One distinct problem per bullet. Be specific — "their intake
is fully manual and takes 3-4 hours per day" is useful; "operational issues" is not.

**Omit if:** The meeting was entirely internal (no client) or the client described no
problems (e.g., purely a progress update call).

---

## Section 2: Key Points

**Header:** `## Key Points`

**What goes here:** The 4-8 most important things that came out of this conversation.
What would someone need to know if they hadn't been in the room? Leads with the most
impactful items.

**Format:** Bulleted list. One point per bullet. Order by importance, not chronology.
Include numbers, specifics, and named entities when they appeared in the conversation.

**Omit if:** The meeting was so brief or routine that all meaningful content is already
captured in other sections. This is rare — Key Points is almost always present.

---

## Section 3: Workflows and Processes

**Header:** `## Workflows and Processes`

**What goes here:** Existing workflows the client or team described, or new processes
proposed during the meeting. Anything that maps how something currently works or how
something will work.

**Format:** Use a sub-header per workflow if more than two were discussed:
```markdown
### [Workflow Name]
[Description or numbered steps]
```
For a single workflow, a paragraph or bulleted steps is fine.

**Omit if:** No workflows or processes were described or proposed.

---

## Section 4: Action Items

**Header:** `## Action Items`

**What goes here:** Concrete tasks assigned or volunteered during the meeting. Only
include tasks that were explicitly stated — don't invent tasks from general discussion.

**Format:** Checkbox list:
```markdown
- [ ] [Task description] — **Owner:** [Name] | **Due:** [date or "unspecified"]
```

If deadline was stated as a relative reference ("by Friday", "end of next week"),
preserve the exact phrasing rather than converting to a specific date.

**Rules:**
- If owner was named, include them. If not, write `Owner: unspecified`.
- If deadline was given, include it. If not, write `Due: unspecified`.
- Do not combine two separate tasks into one bullet.
- Do not drop a task because one field is missing.

**Omit if:** No tasks were assigned or volunteered during the meeting.

---

## Section 5: Decisions Made

**Header:** `## Decisions Made`

**What goes here:** Things that were explicitly decided, agreed on, or confirmed
during the meeting. A decision is a conclusion reached, not a task.

**Format:** Numbered list. One decision per line. Use a direct quote when the exact
phrasing of a commitment matters:
```markdown
1. [Decision statement]
2. "[Exact quote]" — [Speaker]
```

**Omit if:** No explicit decisions were made. Exploratory meetings with no finalized
conclusions have no Decisions section.

---

## Section 6: Open Questions

**Header:** `## Open Questions`

**What goes here:** Questions that were raised but not answered during the meeting.
Things that need to be resolved before work can proceed.

**Format:** Bulleted list. Phrase each item as a question:
```markdown
- Is the Salesforce integration a hard requirement or a preference?
- What is the client's total budget for phase 1?
```

**Omit if:** All questions raised in the meeting were answered in the same meeting.

---

## Section 7: Next Steps

**Header:** `## Next Steps`

**What goes here:** The agreed trajectory — what happens next in the relationship,
project, or conversation. Higher-level than action items; captures sequencing and
timing when discussed.

**Format:** Ordered list when sequence matters, unordered when steps are parallel:
```markdown
1. Aleem sends proposal by Thursday
2. Client reviews with team over the weekend
3. Follow-up call scheduled for Monday
```

**Omit if:** Next steps are fully covered by the action items section and no additional
trajectory was discussed.

---

## Section 8: General Notes

**Header:** `## General Notes`

**What goes here:** Anything worth capturing that did not fit another section. Meeting
tone, relationship context, background information shared, red flags, observations.
This section is deliberately unstructured.

**Format:** Bulleted list or short paragraphs, whichever fits the content.

**Omit if:** Nothing is left over after the other sections. This section is often absent.

---

## Section Order

Always write sections in this order when present:

1. Client Problem / Pain Points
2. Key Points
3. Workflows and Processes
4. Action Items
5. Decisions Made
6. Open Questions
7. Next Steps
8. General Notes

Present sections keep their position in this order. If section 3 is empty, section 4
still follows section 2. The order never shifts based on what's present.

---

## Global Formatting Rules

- No em dashes anywhere. Use a hyphen or rewrite.
- No emojis.
- Bold for emphasis only where it helps the reader (owner names in action items, key
  decisions). Not decorative.
- One idea per bullet. A bullet needing two sentences is usually two bullets.
- Speaker attribution is optional. Only attribute when it matters who said something
  (e.g., a commitment the client made, a specific number they quoted).
- No invented content. Every bullet traces back to something in the transcript.
