# Grad School Playbook — Master's / PhD (AI/CS)

For "should I do a master's," "MS vs PhD," and "how do I apply." Grounded in `research-synthesis.md`
Q6. Lead with the worth-it decision, then the application mechanics and timeline.

## Is a master's worth it? (the honest answer)

For most people heading into **industry** AI/ML, a master's is **optional** — skills + portfolio +
experience usually win. It's **worth it** when it meaningfully changes your trajectory:

- You're **transitioning into AI/ML** from another field and need structured training you can't
  self-source.
- You need **credential signaling** for competitive or visa-gated roles, or **relocation** (a degree
  abroad is also a study-visa/PR route — see `scholarships-studyabroad.md`).
- You want a **route into research** or a future PhD.

It's usually **not worth** ~2 years + ~$100K if you already have meaningful ML experience and a
portfolio. Decide on trajectory change, not the title.

## MS vs PhD

| Choose a **Master's** if… | Choose a **PhD** if… |
|---|---|
| You want industry roles (ML Eng, Data Scientist, Applied Scientist) within ~2 years | You want research roles at top labs (DeepMind, OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) or academia |
| You want flexibility or employer sponsorship | You have a research question you're obsessed with |
| You're using it to pivot or relocate | You've tried research and genuinely liked it |

PhDs run ~5-6 years (US; ~4 in the UK, often requiring a prior master's). The opportunity cost is high
— don't start one without having tried research first (CMU/80,000 Hours).

**Program types (Cornell):** Professional Master's (~1 yr, coursework + team project, mostly self-
funded, industry-oriented); MA/MS (~2 yr, half coursework half research, some funding possible); PhD
(funded, research).

## Application components

- **Transcripts / GPA** — the baseline screen.
- **Statement of Purpose (SOP)** — 500-1000 words; the most important component after GPA.
  - *Master's SOP:* emphasize preparation (courses, skills, **projects**), career direction, and why
    *this specific program* (faculty, courses, fit).
  - *PhD SOP:* emphasize research interests, technical depth, and **alignment with specific faculty
    advisors**; show you can become an independent researcher.
- **3+ letters of recommendation** — from people who can speak to your ability; lock writers in early.
- **CV**, sometimes a personal/diversity statement, and **GRE** (many CS programs have dropped or made
  it optional — check each program).
- English proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL) for international applicants, unless waived (see the MOI tip in
  `scholarships-studyabroad.md`).

## Timeline (work backwards from the deadline)

- **~18 months out:** start thinking, shortlist programs, identify the gaps to close.
- **~6 months before the deadline:** finalize school list, **ask recommenders** (give them a month+),
  draft the SOP, book any required tests early.
- Most deadlines fall **~7-9 months before** the program start (winter deadlines for autumn start).
- Tailor the SOP per program; reuse a strong core, swap the "why this program" section.

## Funding

Master's programs are often self-funded; **fully funded routes exist** via scholarships (Fulbright,
Chevening, DAAD, Erasmus Mundus, Commonwealth, MEXT) and PhD assistantships — see
`scholarships-studyabroad.md`. Always factor funding into the worth-it math.
