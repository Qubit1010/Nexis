# Scholarships & Study-Abroad Playbook

For "what scholarships should I target," "how do I win one," and "best country to study in." Grounded
in `research-synthesis.md` Q7-Q8. **Always read `student-context.md` first** — eligibility, deadlines,
and best destinations depend heavily on the student's country. Default examples below foreground
options open to Pakistan / South Asia. Verify deadlines/amounts via the live notebook before quoting
as current — they change yearly.

## The major fully funded scholarships

| Program | Country | Covers | Key conditions | Window |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fulbright** (Foreign Student) | USA | Tuition, living, airfare, insurance | ~4,000 grants/yr, 160+ countries; via binational Commission/Embassy | Varies by country |
| **Chevening** | UK | Tuition, living, travel (1-yr master's) | ~**2,800 hrs (~2 yrs) work experience**; commit to return home 2 yrs | Opens ~Aug, closes ~early Nov |
| **DAAD** | Germany | ~**€934/mo** stipend + more (study); ~€1,200 doctoral | World's largest funder; many German public unis are **€0 tuition** anyway | Most deadlines ~**Oct 15** |
| **Erasmus Mundus** Joint Masters | EU (2-4 countries) | Tuition + ~**€1,400/mo** + travel + insurance | Apply directly to each programme | EACEA deadline ~**12 Feb** |
| **Commonwealth Master's** | UK | Full funding | Developing-Commonwealth countries | Annual cycle |
| **MEXT** | Japan | Tuition + stipend + travel | Research/undergrad tracks | Via embassy/university |

## How to actually win one

- **Read eligibility twice and confirm every requirement before investing time.** The #1 cause of
  rejection is applying when ineligible. If unclear, email the official contact.
- **Use a Medium-of-Instruction (MOI) certificate** from your registrar **in place of IELTS** where
  accepted (DAAD, CSC China, GKS Korea, others) — saves money and time.
- **Apply to 3-5 in parallel** rather than betting on one.
- **Write a specific return/impact plan**, not a generic one — what you'll do with the degree back
  home. Tailor every essay to the program's mission.
- **Contact professors before applying** for research-track awards (DAAD PhD, CSC China).
- **Start months ahead.** Book IELTS/TOEFL early (results take days-weeks); order and attest
  transcripts; give recommenders a month+.
- **Typical document set:** transcripts (attested), English test (IELTS ~6.5+ / TOEFL ~90+, or MOI),
  SOP/personal statement, CV, 2-3 recommendation letters, sometimes essays/video. For Pakistan, most
  deadlines cluster **Oct-Feb**.

## Best countries to study abroad (choose by the visa, not the ranking)

The move that turns "study abroad" into "settle abroad" is the **post-study work visa + PR pathway**.
All major destinations changed rules recently — verify currency.

| Country | Post-study work | Tuition (intl, approx) | PR signal | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Germany** | 18-mo job-seeker → EU Blue Card | **~€0** public unis | Blue Card → PR | Cheapest; needs blocked-account funds; Chancenkarte |
| **Canada** | PGWP **1-3 yrs** (auto) | ~CA$20-35K | Express Entry (clear) | PGWP rules changed Nov 1, 2024 (field/language) |
| **UK** | Graduate Route **2 yrs** (3 PhD) | ~£15-38K | No direct PR from Graduate visa | Being cut to 18 mo from Jan 2027 (reported) |
| **Australia** | Temp Graduate (485) **2-4 yrs** | ~AU$30-45K | Skilled pathways | High minimum wage |
| **Netherlands** | Zoekjaar **12 mo**, full work rights | mid | Skilled routes | No separate visa; many English programs |
| **USA** | OPT 12 mo (+24 STEM) | **~$40-65K** | H-1B **lottery (~25%)** | Top-ranked unis; highest cost + visa risk |

**Cost framing:** compare **all-in** cost (tuition + living + insurance + visa + proof-of-funds/blocked
account), not tuition alone — "free" Germany still needs a sizable funds reserve.

**2026 momentum:** Germany rising (€0 tuition + opportunity card), UK slipping on cost, Netherlands
climbing on English programmes; Canada/Australia stable but tightening.
