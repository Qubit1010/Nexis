# Creative Feedback Log

Append-only log of editorial feedback from the client's creative team on delivered

When research and this log conflict, this log wins (the paying client's editor is the tiebreaker).

## 2026-06-29 — Red Cajucom (Belle & Perry) — review of `05-hybrid-pod-ep-26`

~25 Google Doc comments on the Deal Falling Apart candidate set. Six patterns:

### 1. Engagement CTAs on every long-form post (most repeated comment)
Every long-form post must END with a specific, experience-inviting question that drives
comments. Generic invites don't count.

Verbatim examples he added:
- "What were the hard times you faced as an agent?"
- "So, how does stability and steady-thinking look like to you?"
- "So, in situations like these, how do you often respond? And what did you learn from your experience?"

### 2. Second-sentence test / quote-first openers (4 comments)
- "Consider dropping the 1st sentence. 2nd sentence appeals more as a hook." (kept: "Nobody covers what happens between 'this deal is dead' and 'we closed.'")
- "Consider switching the 2 sentences. 2nd sentence appeals as more of a hook." (quote first: "Most deals are not dead. They're unstable..." THEN "This is what Brenda Thompson taught me...")
- "Switch these 2 sentences. 2nd sentence appeals more as a hook." (put the quoted line before its setup)

Rule: if the second sentence is the stronger hook, cut or demote the first. Quotes lead, attribution follows.

### 3. Read-aloud flow polish
- Softeners: "REVISE: They're just unstable." · "REVISE: It's usually just stuck." (flagged 3x)
- Possessive reframe: "The deal isn't the problem. Your response to the deal is."
- Parallel structure: "REVISE: When you're irritated. When you're trying to prove a point. Breathe first."
- Simplify clunk: "too clunky. simplify: And it starts with something that is surprisingly simple: Before anything else, put the facts in place first."
- Trim filler tag-ons in hooks: "Can remove 'Here is the difference.' Or replace: 'Here's how.'"
- Colon-merge choppy two-liners: "One habit that kills fixable deals: walking into a conversation with only one option."
- Word-level flow: "remove 'to' for better flow" · "Revise: When it's still early." · "Revise: Their voices shift." · "The hard deals build the agent you will become."

### 4. Formatting
- "Add numbers if we're enumerating." (steps listed inline must be numbered)
- "all caps all Ws: WHO WHAT WHERE WHEN WHY" (named framework words get ALL CAPS)

### 5. Personality beats
- "tweak to add personality: Spoiler alert: You're not."
Light, human asides are welcome where the voice file's candor supports them.

### 6. Recommended-hook markers
He manually tagged hooks "reco hook" / "Reco" / "Can be 2nd option" / "2nd option" /
"Reco with minor tweak" / "Reco with revision" on nearly every segment. Output should
pre-mark our top pick `(recommended)` and runner-up `(2nd option)` per segment so his
review starts from a shortlist.

## 2026-07-04 — Min Rosales (Belle & Perry) — review of `05-hybrid-v2` (Deal Falling Apart)

One comment, but it's a hard rule: **"It should be in the first POV as this is her channel."**

The captions and posts were written in third person about Brenda ("Brenda Thompson has
taken the 9:15pm call... she asks them one question"). The content publishes to **Brenda's
own channels**, so Brenda is the speaker. It must be **first person**.

**Rule — match POV to the channel owner:**
- Content published on the **client's own channel** → first person. The client is the
  speaker ("I've taken the 9:15pm call", "I call them the W's"). Never refer to the client
  in third person, and never write in a fan/curator "I heard [client] say..." voice.
- Only use third-person attribution if the content ships to a **different** channel (an
  agency page, a curator, a team member's feed) — set that per client in the voice file.
- This supersedes the 2026-06-29 entry-2 example that showed "This is what Brenda Thompson
  taught me" — that phrasing is third-person-about-the-client and is now wrong for her channel.
- Editorial scaffolding stays third person: file headers, "Why this segment", transcript
  excerpts, and metadata address the creative team, not the audience.

## 2026-07-10 — Red Cajucom (Belle & Perry) — review of `05-hybrid` (Options Health Scare to Yes Year, Corporate to Real Estate)

Feedback delivered in Slack, not Doc comments — referencing "another thread": **"the way most
of our posts were written follow this one short sentence cadence per line... Even when
multiple lines could perfectly be written as one full paragraph with several sentences, the
transcript separates them into one-liner paragraphs. The more we do it, the more the tone
sounds robotic, cold, and not very personal... It will sound more Brenda."**

Every long-form post was defaulting to one sentence per line for its entire length, including
the middle, where several lines could have read as one flowing paragraph. Hooks and CTAs are
exempt — Red confirmed those should stay short: **"for beginning hooks and ending lines or
call-to-actions, the shorter/sharper it is, the better."**

**Rule — vary cadence by section, don't default to one-sentence-per-line everywhere:**
- Hooks, openers, and closing CTAs stay short and punchy — one idea per line, unchanged.
- The body of long-form posts (the substance between opener and CTA) writes in short
  paragraphs of 1-3 sentences, blending a shorter line with a slightly longer one —
  storytelling rhythm, not a list. Matches `social-writing-research-2026.json`'s
  `social_copy_formatting` sources ("1-3 sentences per paragraph"), which the prior "one
  sentence per line" instruction had oversimplified.
- Supersedes the "One sentence per line" wording in `templates/05-hybrid.md` Step 4 and
  `references/output-spec.md`'s shared LinkedIn/Facebook shape — both updated same day.
- Applied retroactively to the live candidate docs this feedback covers (Options Health
  Scare to Yes Year, Corporate to Real Estate, and Deal Falling Apart / 5 Steps) — see the
  Google Docs for the rewritten long-form posts. On Deal Falling Apart specifically, several
  short standalone lines were left untouched on purpose: they're Red's own hand-flagged
  edits from the 2026-06-29 review (softeners, parallel structure, personality beats) — this
  cadence fix only targets mechanical one-sentence-per-line stacking, not deliberate craft.
