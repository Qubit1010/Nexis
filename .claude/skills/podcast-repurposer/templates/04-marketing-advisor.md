# Template 04 — marketing-advisor (2026 research-backed)

**Angle:** Select and write against the **2026 short-form algorithm reality**. Where the other
templates lean on craft, intent, or platform fit, this one optimizes for the metrics that
actually drive distribution now: completion, saves, and sends. Segments are chosen for
saveability and shareability; hooks target a >25% hook-rate; scripts hit a measured word
density; posts are grounded in LinkedIn dwell-time benchmarks.

**Source methods:** `.claude/skills/marketing-advisor/references/` —
`instagram-reels-playbook.md`, `content-strategy-playbook.md`, `channel-benchmarks.md`.

Read `references/output-spec.md` for the shape and the client's main voice file for limits and
brand. This template defines the *research-driven filling*.

---

## Method (with the numbers it's built on)

### 1. Segment selection by saveability + shareability + completion
Ranking signals that matter in 2026: **watch-time/completion (highest), sends-per-reach
(3-5x a like), saves (~3x a like)**. Likes are vanity. So pick segments that are:
- **Saveable** — a specific, useful truth an agent wants to come back to (e.g. "6 months of
  runway before you quit").
- **Sendable** — something an agent would DM to another agent ("this is us").
- **Completable** — a tight arc that pays off fast.

**Length note:** the 2026 sweet spot is **15-30s** (a 15s reel at 80% completion beats a 3-min
reel at 20%). The client spec allows up to 2 min. So: select the 30s-2min segment per the
voice file, **and flag a tighter 15-45s in-point** where the punch really lands. Surfacing that
trade-off is this template's job.

### 2. Hooks: win the first 3 seconds (target >25% hook-rate)
Up to 50% scroll past in 3 seconds. Use the proven formulas:
- **Mystery** — "Did you know most people quit corporate for the wrong reason?"
- **Mistake** — "The mistake almost every new agent makes in year one."
- **Exclusivity** — "What top producers know that nobody tells new agents."
- **Transformation** — "How she went from glass ceiling to brokerage owner."

Rules: **motion/high-impact word in the first frame**, no slow intro, high-impact words
("you," "secret," "the truth"). 5 hooks, varied across these formulas.

### 3. Script density + kinetic captions
~**75 words per 30 seconds** of clip — note the target words for each segment's duration so the
editor knows the pacing. 60-85% watch on mute, so add a one-line **"kinetic captions
mandatory"** production note per segment.

### 4. Long-form posts grounded in platform benchmarks
- **LinkedIn:** optimize for **dwell time** (61s+ dwell = ~13x the engagement of a 3s skim).
  150-300 words, POV/"how I" framing, no link in body, end on a principle or question.
- **Facebook:** community framing; conversational.
- Captions self-contained (no "watch the full episode" as the only CTA) — saves and sends come
  from value delivered in-feed, not from a teaser.

**In your file header,** note the target word-count per segment and the suggested tighter
in-point. That's this template's signature and what the comparison judges it on.
