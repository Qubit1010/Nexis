# Template 03 — client-content-creator (full-funnel, platform-native package)

**Angle:** Treat each segment as a mini "hero asset" and cascade it into **platform-native**
pieces written differently for Instagram, LinkedIn, and Facebook. This template's whole reason
to exist is to fix the current gap: one caption format pasted across every channel. It also
ideates each segment from two angles (teaching vs emotional) and ties everything back to
Brenda's archetypes with brand-alignment notes.

**Source methods:** `.claude/skills/client-content-creator/SKILL.md` — brand-voice extraction,
two-angle ideation, platform-specific framing, brand-alignment notes, the "no AI tells" /
"So What" / "specificity over vagueness" quality rules.

Read `references/output-spec.md` for the shape and the client's main voice file for limits and
brand. This template defines the *full-funnel, platform-native filling*.

---

## Method

### 1. Two-angle ideation per segment
For each of the 3-5 segments, find both:
- a **teaching angle** (the practical takeaway an agent can use), and
- an **emotional angle** (the feeling — being seen, being backed, the identity shift).

Choose the stronger angle to lead each asset, and use the other for one of the A/B captions.
This guarantees the 3 captions are real alternatives, not rewordings.

### 2. Platform-native by design (the core fix)
Write the captions and posts **differently per platform**. Do not reuse one body:
- **Instagram:** punchy hook line, 3-5 short paragraphs (one idea each, line breaks), soft CTA.
  Scannable on a phone. Self-contained.
- **LinkedIn:** thought-leadership framing. Open with a bold claim or specific moment, not a
  scene-setter. Short paragraphs, white space, end on an extractable principle or a question.
- **Facebook:** warmer, community-first. Conversational, a little more narrative; speaks to the
  agent like a peer, invites a reply.

Label every caption and post with its platform so the platform difference is visible.

### 3. Mini hero-asset cascade
Each segment is a hero moment that fans out: the on-video hooks (5) carry the clip; the captions
(3) adapt it per platform/angle; the long-form posts (3-5) extend it into LinkedIn/FB
thought-leadership. Same insight, genuinely re-shaped per surface.

### 4. Brand-alignment note per segment
After each segment's assets, add 1-2 sentences: which archetype it leans on (Hero / Mama-Bear /
Sage), which pillar, and why the framing fits Brenda. This is the client-content-creator
signature — it makes the package feel deliberately on-brand, not generic.

### 5. Quality rules (apply to every line)
From client-content-creator: no AI tells (no em dashes, no "it's not X it's Y", no filler
openers, no buzzwords), specificity over vagueness (name the segment, the number, the real
pain), the **So What test** (every paragraph must matter to the reader right now), varied
cadence. And the client's absence signals on top.
