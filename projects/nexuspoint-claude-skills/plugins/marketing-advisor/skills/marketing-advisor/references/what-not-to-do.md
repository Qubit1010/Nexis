# What NOT To Do 2026 (Sourced Kill List)

**Source basis:** Every ban below comes from `references/research-synthesis.md` (234-source 2026 NotebookLM synthesis). Citations map to `_research/sources.json`. These are tactics that are penalized, filtered, or simply dead in 2026 - many were "best practice" two years ago. Run advice through this filter before delivering it.

---

## Universal (every channel)

- **Generic AI-generated copy.** LinkedIn's 360Brew throttles it; Gmail's Gemini filters it (up to 40% of delivered email quietly hidden). Homogeneous "AI speak" loses. Write with specifics and a point of view [s186, s88].
- **Vanity metrics as goals** - opens, impressions, raw traffic, follower counts predict nothing. Track SQL rate, reply rate, CAC, POAS instead [s206].
- **Spray-and-pray volume.** Unpersonalized blasts get ~2.1% reply and torch reputation. Narrow + personalize beats wide + generic everywhere now [s41].
- **Engagement bait** ("Comment YES," "Tag someone," "Like for Part 2") triggers spam filters on LinkedIn and Instagram [s186, s119].

## Email

- Tracking **open rate as a KPI** (Apple MPP inflates to 42-44%; true ~27.7%) [s39].
- **DMARC at `p=none`** - now treated as no DMARC; triggers rejection [s92].
- **"Just bumping this" follow-ups** - Gemini's thread summary exposes them as spam [s95].
- **Purchased lists** (18.5% bounce, permanent domain damage) [s41].
- **Multiple links / heavy HTML** - trip filters. Mostly plain text, max one link [s67].
- Exceeding **50 emails/inbox/day** or skipping 3-6 week domain warmup [s56].
- Spam-trigger words: "free money," "act immediately," "Re: your invoice" (fake threads), "lose weight," etc. [s180].

## LinkedIn

- **Engagement pods** - 97% detection accuracy, permanent reach cut [s186].
- **Links in the post body** (-60-68% reach) AND **links in the first comment** (now penalized too) [s185].
- **Hashtag stuffing** (3-5 tags = -29% reach; use 1-2 or none) [s186].
- **Mass tagging** (5+ people) and **post-and-ghost** (must engage 5-10 niche posts before, reply in first 60 min) [s186].
- **Editing a post in the first 10-15 min** - resets the algorithm's evaluation [s186].
- **Pitching in the connection note** (reply rate down to 2.2-3%) - warm first [s156].
- Company-page-led content (personal profile = 8x engagement) [s85].

## Instagram / Reels

- **Reposting watermarked TikToks** - 10+ in 30 days = excluded from Explore; original gets +40-60% [s119].
- **30 hashtags** (spam penalty; use 3-5 niche) [s119].
- **Slow intros** ("Hey guys, today...") waste the 3-second window - cold-open instead [s100].
- **Chasing likes** (weakest signal) instead of sends/saves/completion [s119].
- **Polished cinematic** over raw native-style footage (native wins) [s57].
- **Cold DM blasting** - gets accounts banned; only automate DMs triggered by the user's own comment [s100].

## Content / Short-form

- **High-frequency mediocre posting** - actively damages algorithmic standing. Consistent quality > volume [s201].
- **Generic thought leadership** ("Future of the Industry") and **static infographics** - near-zero conversion [s201].
- **Viral shorts with no offer** - convert <0.1%. Pair reach with a direct-response next step [s201].
- **"How to" lecturing** over **"How I" experience** (timeless, and still true) - but now it must also be substantive enough to earn dwell time.

## Paid Ads

- Letting Google push you to **Performance Max for B2B leads** (Search wins 553% vs 436% ROAS) [s43].
- **Underfunding tests** so ad sets never exit the learning phase (fund the min viable budget or don't start) [s168].
- Optimizing to **ROAS/impressions** instead of POAS / cost-per-qualified-lead [s206].

## Stale framework claims to retire (from the old skill)

- **"Open rate 50%+ is the benchmark"** - false in 2026. True cold open ~27.7%; opens are not a KPI [s39].
- **"Rule of 100 / pure volume wins"** - volume without personalization gets 2.1% reply and burns domains. Narrow + relevant beats raw volume [s41].
- **"Higher price always increases perceived value"** (unqualified) - in 2026, premium pricing works when packaged as outcomes/tiers/value-capture, not as a blanket psychological law [s27].
- **"7-38-55 rule" as settled science** - treat as a heuristic, not a cited fact.

When you catch yourself about to repeat any of these, stop and quote the 2026 number from `channel-benchmarks.md` instead.
