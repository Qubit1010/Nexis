## Pricing models: the 2026 data is decisive

High-earning operators ($150k+ revenue) price this way [s87, s163]:
- **Value-based: 62%** of the time
- **Monthly retainer: 28%**
- **Hourly: only 8%** (and only for discovery/audits/un-scoped troubleshooting)

Why it matters: value-based pricers report a **median income of $96k vs $58k** for hourly billers - a **66% gap** [s87]. For an AI agency specifically, hourly billing is self-sabotage: automation compresses delivery from days to seconds, so billing time **penalizes your own efficiency** [s163].

**Rule:** price on business impact, not hours. Use hourly only to scope a paid audit.

## Price ranges by scope (AI automation, 2026)

| Tier | Project fee | Monthly retainer | Fits |
|------|-------------|------------------|------|
| **Entry (single workflow)** | $1,500-$10,000 | $500-$1,500 | lead routing, doc flows, single-system sync |
| **Core (departmental)** | $10,000-$35,000 | $1,500-$4,000 | multi-system rollouts, sales/support ops, basic LLM logic |
| **Premium (enterprise agentic)** | $35,000-$100,000+ | $4,000-$10,000+ | compliance-heavy, multi-agent orchestration, private DB/RAG |

Source [s32]. Reference points: AI SEO retainers avg **$3,200/mo** ($2k-20k+); chatbots $10-35k (FAQ) to $35-75k (support); custom ML $50-150k+; RAG $35-250k+ [s28].

Start at Entry/Core and grow into retainers. Map web projects as the entry wedge, AI automation as the premium upsell.

## How to package for premium pricing

1. **The AI Audit Gateway** - never pitch a high-ticket build cold. Sell a **$1,997-$5,000 "AI Readiness Audit"** as foot-in-the-door: identify bottlenecks, deliver a roadmap. **40-60% of audit clients convert** into a core build within 30 days [s28]. This is one of the best packaging moves for an automation agency (it also doubles as the discovery mechanism).
2. **Three tiers, always** - presenting three options yields a **75% close rate** vs 55% (two) vs 35% (single yes/no). The middle tier looks most logical (compromise effect) [s27].
3. **Autonomy-based naming** - ditch "Basic/Premium." Name by how the AI mimics an employee: **Assist** (responds to prompts), **Orchestrate** (coordinates workflows, adapts), **Automize** (plans multi-step work, decides independently) [s176].
4. **Hybrid retainer / agent license** - heavy setup fee (e.g. $20k) + ongoing **Agent License** (e.g. $2k/mo) for IP access, monitoring, model upgrades. Pitched right after a successful launch, this converts **50-70%** into recurring revenue [s27]. Turns one-off builds into MRR.

## Margin protection (the numbers that keep you profitable)

- **Value capture rate:** price at **10-25% of Year-1 value created**. If a workflow saves a client $300k/yr, a 20% capture = a **$60k** price. Target client payback in **3-6 months** [s32].
- **API cost markup:** bundle estimated LLM/API costs with a **20-30% buffer**, or pass through at a flat **15-25% markup** [s28].
- **Risk premium:** embed **15-20%** into flat-fee builds for model drift/iteration [s27].
- **Billable utilization:** early agencies lose ~38% of time to non-billable setup; profitability kicks in at **60-70%** utilization [s163].
- **Net Revenue Retention:** target **110-130%**. Below 100% means packages underdeliver or lack upsell paths [s163].

## Quick application
- "How should I price this?" -> pick the scope tier, set a value-capture price (10-25% of Year-1 impact), present three tiers, attach a risk premium + API markup.
- "They want hourly." -> offer it only for a paid audit; quote the audit gateway, then convert to a value-priced build.
- "How do I make this recurring?" -> hybrid retainer / agent license pitched right after launch (50-70% convert).
