# Paid Ads Playbook 2026 (B2B, lighter coverage)

**Source basis:** Distilled from `references/research-synthesis.md` Q7 (234-source NotebookLM synthesis). Citations map to `_research/sources.json`. Paid is usually a lower priority for a young agency (organic + outbound come first), but this gives a research-backed answer when the topic comes up. Treat it as an unvalidated channel until the offer converts cheaply through outbound/organic.

---

## Should you run ads yet? (the honest take)

Not as a first move. Paid ads are the **fastest** path to pipeline (leads in weeks vs months for organic) but they only pay off once your offer already converts through cheaper channels [s59]. Sequence: prove the message via outbound/organic, *then* pour fuel on the winner with paid. Paid ads validate and scale a proven message; they don't fix a weak one.

When paid earns its place:
- Your cold email/LinkedIn angles are converting and you've hit volume caps (LinkedIn = 80-100 safe requests/week) and need to scale the proven message [s148].
- You want a cheap **retargeting layer** to stay in front of warm prospects who saw your organic content (they convert at much lower CPL).

---

## Platform split & where each wins

For B2B, recommended starting split is **70% Google / 30% Meta** [s59]:
- **Google Search = demand capture.** Catches bottom-funnel intent ("best CRM," "n8n automation agency"). For B2B leads, **Search beats Performance Max (553% vs 436% ROAS)** - don't let Google auto-push you to PMax [s43].
- **Meta = demand creation + retargeting.** CPMs are 3-5x cheaper than LinkedIn ($8-15 vs $40-60), making it a cost-effective retargeting/awareness layer [s59].
- **LinkedIn Ads** cost more per click ($5.26 CPC) but yield a **28% lower cost-per-qualified-lead than Google ($47 vs $65)** thanks to targeting - worth testing for high-ACV B2B [s148].

## Minimum viable test budgets (don't starve the algorithm)

The #1 cause of ad failure is spreading budget too thin to exit the learning phase [s168]:
- **Meta:** needs ~50 conversions/ad set/week. Formula: **`(target CPA x 50) / 7 = min daily budget per ad set`**. E.g. $40 CPA -> ~$300/day. Manual test floor $300-600/mo; Advantage+ needs $1,000/mo+.
- **Google Search:** $500-1,000/mo over 2-4 weeks. PMax: $1,500/mo over 4-6 weeks.

If you can't fund a clean test, don't start - you'll burn cash in perpetual learning phase.

## Rules
- Match the landing page to the ad exactly (message, promise, design) - continuous experience from click to close.
- Track **POAS (profit on ad spend)**, not just ROAS, and cost per *qualified* lead - not impressions/clicks (see `martech-stack.md`).
- Best organic content makes the best ad creative - reuse winners.
- For a young agency: recommend a small Meta retargeting test against warm traffic first, fund it properly, and keep outbound/organic as the primary engine until the offer is proven.
