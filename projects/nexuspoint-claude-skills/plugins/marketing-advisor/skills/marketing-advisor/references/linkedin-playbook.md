## The 2026 reality: Interest Graph, not Social Graph

LinkedIn now runs on "360Brew," a 150B-parameter AI that reads the **semantic meaning** of your posts and matches them to people by interest, not by who you're connected to [s185, s186]. Consequences:
- A good post from a small account can reach widely (niche authority beats follower count).
- Generic, GPT-flavored, "bro-etry" copy gets throttled - the AI detects it.
- Overall organic reach fell hard after the shift (impressions -63-66%, views -50%, engagement -25%). The bar is higher; substance wins.

**The 3-stage distribution you're playing against** [s186]:
1. **Golden Hour (0-60 min):** post is shown to **2-5% of your network**. It needs **5-10% engagement** in that window to advance. Below 2% and distribution dies (kills ~87% of posts). Only 5% of weak starters ever recover.
2. **Expanded (1-6 hrs):** survivors go to 10-20% of network + 2nd-degree.
3. **Dwell time is the #1 signal:** 0-3s dwell = 1.2% ER; **61s+ dwell = 15.6% ER (13x)**. Write posts people *stop and read*, not skim-and-like.

## Content formats, ranked by 2026 performance

| Format | Engagement rate | Play |
|--------|----------------|------|
| **Document/PDF carousel** | **6.6-7.0%** (highest) | 5-15 slides; teach one idea visually. +278% vs video [s146] |
| **Native vertical video** | 4.7-5.6% | 30-90s, hook in first 1-2s, 9:16 (=+71% impressions) [s185] |
| **Text post** | 2.0-4.5% (expert 6-8%) | 150-300 words / 800-1000 chars to earn the "see more" click [s186] |
| **Newsletter** | **40-60% open** | Bypasses the feed (email+push). Start one for compounding reach [s154] |

**Rules that move reach:**
- **No external links in the post body** (-60-68% reach). Don't put the link in the first comment either - that's penalized now in 2026. Put the link in the post only when the post IS a resource directory (3+ links can lift +236%) [s185]. Otherwise: "comment X and I'll send it" or link from profile.
- **Personal profile >> company page** (8x engagement, 5.6x reach). Lead with the founder's face/voice, not the company page [s85].
- **1-2 hashtags max, or none** - the AI reads context; 3-5 hashtags actually cut reach 29% [s186].
- **Comments are gold:** a thoughtful 10+ word comment carries 5-7x the weight of a like; threaded back-and-forth = 5.2x amplification. Reply to every comment in the first 60 min.

## Posting cadence & timing

- **3-5 posts/week.** More isn't better; daily posting with weak engagement throttles you [s146].
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday.
- **Windows (local time):** 7-9am, 8-10am, 12-1pm, 2-4pm. Avoid Monday AM, Friday PM, weekends (-30-60%) [s222].
- **Engage before you post:** comment on 5-10 niche posts in the 15 min before publishing to prime distribution.

## A weekly LinkedIn rhythm (founder-led)

The founder posts personally. No agency-name-led or academia content (personal brand rule). Weekly mix:
- 1 document carousel (teach an automation/AI-for-ops idea visually)
- 1-2 text posts (a "how I" build story or a contrarian take)
- 1 native video or behind-the-build clip
- Daily: 10-15 min of substantive comments on ICP posts (this is half the algorithm game)
- Consider launching a LinkedIn newsletter for compounding, feed-independent reach.

Hook/retention patterns and the personal-brand content engine itself live in `content-strategy-playbook.md`. Idea sourcing and finished drafts: the **content-engine** skill.

## Outreach benchmarks (strategy here, copy in sales-playbook)

Use these to set expectations and pace volume. The actual connection note + DM sequence = **sales-playbook**.

- **Connection acceptance:** platform avg 28.5-30%. Generic mass: 12-18%. **Personalized + pre-warmed by engaging their content first: 55-70%** [s156, s149]. Engagement-before-request is the single biggest lever.
- **Safe volume:** 80-100 requests/week manually (20-25/wk if using cloud automation). Drop below 20% acceptance and LinkedIn caps you to 20-50/wk [s156]. Protect the account.
- **Reply rates:** connection-note reply is down to 2.2-3.0% (don't pitch in the note). Post-acceptance DM averages 10.4%; good is 10-25%, elite multi-touch 30-50% [s156, s157].
- **InMail (paid):** 57.5% open, 10-25% reply [s148].
- **Omnichannel:** pairing LinkedIn with email + phone lifts purchase rate +287% vs email alone [s150]. Coordinate with the cold-outreach and email pipelines.

**Sequencing principle:** warm before you ask. Engage their content -> personalized connection (no pitch) -> after accept, open with a genuine question or value, not a pitch. Full archetypes and the 4-touch sequence: sales-playbook.
