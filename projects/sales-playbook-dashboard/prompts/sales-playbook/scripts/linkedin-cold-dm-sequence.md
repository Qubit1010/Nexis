# LinkedIn Cold DM Sequence — Source-Backed Playbook

> **Source basis:** Cited from `references/research-synthesis.md` (Q1, Q2). Real data: SalesBread connection-acceptance rates, Expandi 13.2M data point study, Outbound Squad / GetReplies signal-led sequences, Justin Welsh's documented 3-touch frame.

## The hard truth from the data (2026)

- LinkedIn connection acceptance is **declining**: connection-note reply rates dropped from **3.5% (May 2025) → 2.2% (April 2026)**. (Expandi)
- The platform-wide connection acceptance average is **28.5%** with note vs **55-68%** without note. (SalesBread)
- BUT — with a personalized note, the **positive reply rate is ~39%** once accepted. So: notes lower acceptance, raise downstream conversion.
- C-level senders see **29.4% acceptance**, junior senders see **26.3%**. (Expandi)

**Implication for Aleem:** Send WITHOUT note for max acceptance, OR send WITH a no-pitch note for higher downstream conversion. Test both. Don't send pitchy notes — those underperform both.

## The two documented sequences

### Sequence A — Multi-Channel 14-Day (highest-replying outbound motion)

**Sourced from research synthesis Q2.** This is the documented highest-converting LinkedIn-centric outbound sequence in 2026.

| Day | Channel | Move |
|---|---|---|
| **Day 1** | LinkedIn | Connection request triggered by a SIGNAL (post, hire, funding, launch). |
| **Day 3** | Cold email | Reference the same trigger. (If you have their email.) |
| **Day 5** | LinkedIn DM | Follow up after they've accepted — Archetype 5 (Post-Connection Question). |
| **Day 8** | Email | Value-add (resource, insight, framework — no pitch). |
| **Day 14** | LinkedIn or Email | Soft close. No-oriented call ask. |

**Aleem version of each step:**

#### Day 1 — Connection request (no-pitch note)
*Archetype: 3 — No-Pitch Connection Request (see `frameworks/opener-archetypes.md`)*

Template (under 280 chars):
> Hi [Name], saw your post on [specific topic from their feed]. The bit about [specific detail they wrote] matches exactly what I'm seeing with [their industry] teams I work with. Useful to be connected. No pitch.

#### Day 3 — Cold email referencing the trigger
*Archetype: 2 — Specific Signal + Named Peers*

Template (under 120 words):
> Subject: [their company] + [specific trigger]
>
> [Name] - saw the [trigger] yesterday. Congrats.
>
> Teams hitting that point in [their niche] usually start drowning in [specific manual ops] inside 60 days. Built AI workflows for [Peer 1] and [Peer 2] that took 12+ hrs/week off founder plates.
>
> Worth a 12-min call Tuesday or Thursday to see if it'd port to your stack?
>
> - Aleem
> NexusPoint | AI automation

#### Day 5 — LinkedIn DM after acceptance
*Archetype: 5 — Post-Connection Genuine Question*

Template (under 500 chars):
> Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Genuine question - how's your team handling [specific manual workflow they likely run] right now? I keep running into [their role] in [their niche] who've automated the [obvious part] but [the hard part] is still in spreadsheets. Curious where you've landed.

**No CTA. Just a question.** Wait for their answer. If they engage, transition to the live-conversation playbook (`scripts/live-conversation-playbook.md`).

#### Day 8 — Value-add email (no pitch)
*The non-Aleem move: send a resource, not an ask.*

Options:
- A short Loom (60-90 sec) walking through one specific automation idea for their stack
- A 1-page PDF "What we'd automate first if we were running [their company]"
- A framework / cheatsheet relevant to their world

Template:
> Subject: One thing I'd automate first at [their company]
>
> [Name] - was thinking about your [specific situation] after the post.
>
> [Either: brief 3-bullet observation about what you'd build] OR [Loom link / PDF attachment].
>
> No ask. Just thought you might find it useful.
>
> - Aleem

#### Day 14 — Soft close (no-oriented)
*If still no response after Day 8.*

Template:
> Subject: Should I stop?
>
> [Name] - haven't heard back, so two options:
>
> Either now is genuinely the wrong time and I should move on, OR there's interest but it's gotten buried.
>
> Would it be a bad idea to grab 15 minutes Tuesday 2pm or Thursday 11am so I can show you the actual [specific workflow] we built for [Peer]?
>
> If timing's just off, no hard feelings - I'll quietly disappear.
>
> - Aleem

(Uses Voss no-oriented question + explicit "permission to disappear" — sourced as the highest-converting exit-ramp move.)

### Sequence B — Justin Welsh's 3-Touch Zero-Ask (long game, founder targeting)

**Sourced from research synthesis Q2.** This is documented as a highly effective relationship-driven pipeline for FOUNDERS/CEOs specifically, but operates on a longer timeline (months, not days).

| Touch | Timing | Move |
|---|---|---|
| **Touch 1** | Day 1 | High-Value, Zero Ask. Reference a specific recent action. |
| **Touch 2** | Months later (when relevant trigger occurs) | Continued value — send a custom resource tied to a new mention of theirs. |
| **Touch 3** | Months later | Strategic education — answer their questions in detail without pitching. |

**Example Touch 1 (Welsh's verbatim style):**
> Loved the tweet about the simple landing page. Writing to suggest you improve the contrast of the buttons - the CTA gets lost on mobile. Cheers, friend.

**Example Touch 2 (months later, when they tweet about an internal project):**
> Saw you're building out the onboarding flow. Made you a quick planning tool for ops handoffs - might be useful: [link to Notion template]. No ask.

**Example Touch 3:**
> When you asked about [topic], here's the strategic breakdown I usually give clients: [detailed 200-word strategic answer]. Happy to nerd out more if useful.

**When to use Sequence B:**
- Targeting 5-10 specific dream-client founders/CEOs (not a 100-prospect blast)
- Playing a 3-6 month relationship game, not a 14-day conversion play
- Building authority + recognition before any pitch happens

This is a high-leverage parallel motion to Sequence A. Run both — Sequence A for volume, Sequence B for whales.

## CTA / call-ask language (sourced)

The data: best LinkedIn CTAs actually **avoid asking for a meeting**. Highest converters are:

| Style | Example (sourced) |
|---|---|
| **No-CTA peer question** | "Thanks for connecting, [first name]. Genuine question: how is your team handling [specific problem] right now?" |
| **Soft ask (resource offer)** | "We've helped [comparable company] solve [problem] in about [timeframe]. Happy to share the playbook. No demo needed. Want me to send it over?" |
| **Passive zero-ask** | "Following you all from way over here in [your city]." |
| **Anchored call-ask** (use sparingly, only Day 14) | "Would it be a bad idea to grab 15 minutes Tuesday 2pm so I can show you the actual [workflow] we built for [Peer]?" |

**Never use:** "Worth a quick chat?" / "Open to a call?" / "Hop on a 15-min call?" — these are pattern-matched as cadence in <1 sec.

## Frequency, pacing, and platform limits

Documented from synthesis (Q3 covers IG limits; LinkedIn limits below come from Expandi/GetReplies):

- **Connection requests:** Max ~20-30/day per account (LinkedIn enforced)
- **DMs:** Max ~50/day per account (no hard limit, but volume + low reply rate triggers throttling)
- **Pacing:** Space sends out across the day — don't fire 30 in 30 minutes
- **Personalization minimum:** First 2 sentences MUST be specific to the prospect (Outbound Squad data: drops 50% without)

## Worst-performing LinkedIn DM patterns (sourced kills)

From Expandi / GetReplies as the most underperforming:

1. **Pitching in Touch 1** — almost never works
2. **Self-centered aggression** — "I can help you grow! Hire me!" — documented worst performer
3. **Generic templates pitching from line 1** — lowest acceptance + reply
4. **Targeting outside ICP** — reply rate craters
5. **Long pitchy paragraphs** — get skimmed past
6. **Sales-pitch tone vs peer tone** — peer always wins

See `references/what-not-to-do.md` for the full kill list.

## Daily workflow (Aleem's operational version)

1. **Morning (30 min):** Generate 10-15 connection requests using `linkedin-outreach` skill (with new opener rotation from `frameworks/opener-archetypes.md`). Send.
2. **After acceptance notifications:** Within 24-48 hours of acceptance, send Day 5 DM (Archetype 5).
3. **Mid-week:** For prospects who replied → handoff to `scripts/live-conversation-playbook.md`.
4. **Day 14 batch:** Friday afternoon — run the Day 14 close on all silent connections.
5. **Track:** Update Google Sheets CRM (existing `linkedin-outreach` infrastructure).
