## Why this file exists

Aleem's existing scripts open with the same skeleton every time ("Hey [Name], it looks like [X], curious how you handle Y?"), which now reads as cadence to founders who get 50+ DMs/week. The fix is **rotating across 5 documented opener archetypes** — each drawn from a different practitioner — so a batch of 30 prospects looks like 30 different humans, not one bot.

## The 5 Archetypes (rotation discipline)

Never send two prospects the same archetype in the same batch. The generator picks one per prospect based on available signal.

### Archetype 1 — Trigger-Aware Zero-Ask (Justin Welsh)

**Source:** Justin Welsh's documented "Passive DM" / Google Alerts Inbound Loop (sourced verbatim in research synthesis).
**Why it works:** Zero ask = zero defensive reaction. Establishes you exist in the prospect's world without demanding anything. Compounds over weeks as you're "the founder who reached out, didn't pitch."
**When to use:** Prospect has a public trigger event (funding round, hire, launch, podcast appearance, tweet thread, product release).
**Length:** Under 60 words.

**Source-cited template (Justin Welsh, verbatim):**

> "Congrats on the recent round, {{FirstName}}. I'm a [your one-line expertise] (specific credentials). Love what you're doing. Following you all from way over here in [your city]."

**Aleem-adapted variants:**

> Congrats on the launch, [Name]. Run an AI automation studio out of Islamabad — building the kind of internal AI workflows your team's probably going to want by quarter end. Love what you're shipping. Following from over here.

> Saw the funding announcement, [Name]. Founder of NexusPoint (AI automation + web). Pakistan side of the world. The "use of funds" line about ops scaling is the exact problem we solve. No pitch — just wanted to say nice round.

> [Name] — caught your thread on [specific topic from their post]. Building AI workflows for founders going through that exact stretch. Wanted to be in your DMs without an ask. Genuinely impressive build.

**Hard rule:** Must reference a REAL trigger. If no trigger exists, use Archetype 2 or 4.

### Archetype 2 — Specific Signal with Named Peers (Becc Holland / Outbound Squad)

**Source:** Becc Holland's Trigger-Aware Day 0 framework and the GetReplies / Outbound Squad signal-led sequence (sourced verbatim).
**Why it works:** Ties a specific external event → a problem you solve → named social proof → a concrete time ask (not "got 15 min?"). Specificity = credibility.
**When to use:** Prospect has a trigger AND you have ≥1 named peer with a matching win.
**Length:** 50–80 words.

**Source-cited template (Becc Holland, verbatim):**

> "[Name] — saw [specific trigger] yesterday. Companies hitting that milestone in [segment] typically face [specific pain] inside 60 days. [Peer 1] and [Peer 2] solved it with [one-sentence outcome]. Worth comparing notes?"

**Outbound Squad / GetReplies adaptation:**

> "Hi [first name], Saw [specific trigger — new hire, funding round, product launch, podcast appearance]. Congrats on that. [One sentence connecting the trigger to a problem you solve]. We've helped [2 named similar companies] cut [specific metric] by [specific number]. Worth a 12-minute call next Tuesday to see if it lines up?"

**Aleem-adapted variants (use real peer names from `offer/proof-bank.md`):**

> [Name] — saw the [hire/launch/round] yesterday. Teams hitting that point in [niche] usually start drowning in [specific manual ops]. Built AI workflows for [Peer 1] and [Peer 2] that took 12+ hrs/week off founder plates. Worth a 12-min call Tuesday or Thursday?

> [Name] — caught the [trigger]. Congrats. The reason I'm reaching out: scaling that fast usually means [specific bottleneck] becomes a fire inside 60 days. Wired exactly this fix for [Peer]. Want me to send the playbook? No demo needed.

**Hard rule:** Use real peer names. If you don't have 2 matching wins, drop to 1 + a generic descriptor ("an agency at similar stage").

### Archetype 3 — No-Pitch Connection Request (LinkedIn-specific)

**Source:** Documented as the highest-converting LinkedIn connection note format in 2026 (GetReplies / 14-day multi-channel sequence research).
**Why it works:** Explicitly stating "no pitch" inverts the prospect's prediction. They opened it expecting a pitch and got a peer. Documented to outperform pitch-style notes despite the lower 2.2% reply baseline (Apr 2026).
**When to use:** LinkedIn connection request. Use when prospect has a recent post or thread you can reference.
**Length:** Under 280 characters (LinkedIn note cap is 300).

**Source-cited template (verbatim):**

> "Hi [first name], came across your post on [topic], the point about [specific detail] matched what I'm seeing with [similar company type] teams. Would be useful to be connected. No pitch."

**Aleem-adapted variants:**

> Hi [Name], read your post on [specific topic]. The bit about [specific line] matches exactly what I'm seeing with [niche] teams I work with. Useful to be connected. No pitch.

> [Name] — your take on [specific thing they wrote] was sharp. I work in [adjacent space] and run into the same pattern with founders weekly. Worth connecting. Not pitching.

**Hard rule:** "No pitch" only works if you ACTUALLY don't pitch in the connection note. Save the value drop for the post-connection DM (Archetype 5).

### Archetype 4 — Laid-Back Anti-Pitch (Josh Braun)

**Source:** Josh Braun's documented anti-pitch DM language (sourced verbatim).
**Why it works:** Removes pressure ("not sure if it's a fit") + drops a specific value claim. Psychological reactance: telling someone they're "probably not a fit" makes them want to evaluate it.
**When to use:** Prospect is high-status / receives lots of outbound. Limited public signal.
**Length:** 40–60 words. Conversational.

**Source-cited template (Josh Braun, verbatim, adapted from his info-product example):**

> "We show [target description] like yourself a lesser-known approach to [specific outcome], ultimately [end benefit]. Not sure if it's a fit, but I thought you might be interested."

**Aleem-adapted variants:**

> Show founders running [niche] businesses a lesser-known way to claw back 10+ hrs/week through one specific AI workflow most agencies haven't touched yet. Not sure if it's a fit for [company], but figured I'd flag it.

> Probably not a fit, but had to ask — is the manual [specific task] step still on your plate, or did you guys already wire it? Asking because most [role]s at your stage haven't and I'm trying to figure out if there's a pattern.

> No pitch, won't waste your time. Just curious — is [specific pain] something you're actively solving for, or is it on the "deal with later" pile? Either answer is fine, just mapping who's looking for what.

**Hard rule:** The disqualifier ("not sure if it's a fit", "probably not a fit") is what makes it work. Don't soften it. Don't sound desperate.

### Archetype 5 — Post-Connection Genuine Question DM

**Source:** Documented as the highest-reply-rate LinkedIn DM format after connection acceptance (GetReplies / Outbound Squad).
**Why it works:** No CTA, no pitch — just a peer-level operational question. Founders answer peer questions, they ignore vendor questions. Bypasses the "what does he want?" filter entirely.
**When to use:** Immediately after a LinkedIn connection is accepted (or after Touch 1 on Instagram if prospect replies).
**Length:** Under 60 words.

**Source-cited template (verbatim):**

> "Thanks for connecting, [first name]. Genuine question: how is your team handling [specific operational problem] right now? I keep running into [similar role] who are doing X but say Y is still broken. Curious where you've landed."

**Aleem-adapted variants:**

> Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Genuine question — how's your team handling [specific manual workflow] right now? I keep running into [role]s in [niche] who've automated the [easy thing] but [the hard thing] is still in spreadsheets. Curious where you've landed.

> Appreciate the connect, [Name]. Operational question — when you onboard [new clients / new hires / new deals], is the handoff documented somewhere or is it still tribal? I'm seeing this break a lot of [stage] teams and trying to understand the pattern.

> Thanks for accepting. Quick one — are you actually using [tool/stack visible from their content] end-to-end or did you bolt on [adjacent tool]? Asking because the handoff between those two is where I keep seeing 5+ hrs/week leak out for similar teams.

**Hard rule:** This is a QUESTION, not a setup for a pitch. Don't add "happy to share what we built" at the end. Wait for them to ask.

## Rotation rule (for the generator)

When generating a batch of 10 LinkedIn messages:

| Archetype | Count | Required signal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Trigger-Aware Zero-Ask | 2 | Public trigger event exists |
| 2 — Specific Signal + Named Peers | 3 | Trigger + matching peer win |
| 3 — No-Pitch Connection (note) | 2 | LinkedIn connection note context, recent post exists |
| 4 — Laid-Back Anti-Pitch | 2 | High-status prospect or no public signal |
| 5 — Post-Connection Question | (separate) | Use for DMs after connection accepted |

**Fallback chain:** If signal missing for archetype 1 or 2 → use 4 (anti-pitch always works with minimal signal). For LinkedIn DM after connection → always Archetype 5.

For Instagram, rotation differs (see `scripts/instagram-cold-dm-sequence.md`).

## Shared rules (apply to ALL archetypes)

These are pulled directly from documented banned-phrase lists (Sopro, Outbound Squad, Black Swan). Full list: `references/what-not-to-do.md`.

1. **No "Hey [Name]," followed by Voss-template** — "It looks like..." is fine mid-message but the cadence of `Hey [Name], it looks like X, curious how you handle Y` is the burned pattern.
2. **No formal sign-offs** — never "Best regards," / "Cheers," / "Looking forward to hearing from you." End on the question or the statement.
3. **No em-dashes** in cold messages, ever. Use a hyphen with spaces (` - `) or a comma.
4. **No emojis** in LinkedIn cold messages. **1-3 emojis OK in Instagram DMs only** (CreatorFlow data; 10+ flags unprofessional).
5. **Character limits:**
   - LinkedIn connection note: under 280 chars (LinkedIn caps at 300)
   - LinkedIn DM: keep under ~500 chars
   - Instagram DM: under 100 words (strict — 30% reply drop past 200 words, per CreatorFlow)
   - Cold email: under 120 words / 3-5 sentences
6. **One ask max** — never combine a question AND a CTA in the same message.
7. **"I" count** — count your "I"s. More than 2 in a cold message = rewrite.
8. **No pitch in opener** — never mention NexusPoint, services, or sell in archetypes 1, 3, 4, 5. Only Archetype 2 names what you do (and only as social proof, not pitch).

## What "good" actually looks like (the sourced gold standard)

These four examples are pulled directly from the research synthesis as proven, real-practitioner templates:

**Justin Welsh — zero-ask:**
> "Congrats on the recent round, {{FirstName}}. I'm a healthcare technology veteran (ZocDoc/PatientPop) and love what you're doing. Following you all from way over here in Los Angeles."

**Jason Bay (Outbound Squad) — targeted email:**
> "Hey Jason, Took a look at your LinkedIn post on cold calling. Loved your tip on going out to socialize at a coffee shop before making your cold calls. I'm an introvert, so found that helpful! Not sure if you run into this, but a challenge I've heard sales trainers talk about is finding reliable data. Especially if your clients are prospecting within LinkedIn. One way our ABC tool helps is by getting data from LinkedIn into your CRM in half the time. Would that be worth a few minutes of your time to chat about? John"

**Becc Holland — trigger-aware Day 0:**
> "[Name] — saw [specific trigger] yesterday. Companies hitting that milestone in [segment] typically face [specific pain] inside 60 days. [Peer 1] and [Peer 2] solved it with [one-sentence outcome]. Worth comparing notes?"

**Josh Braun — anti-pitch:**
> "We show info product creators like yourself a lesser-known approach to reach salespeople who miss your posts on LinkedIn, ultimately driving more sales of your guide. Not sure if it's a fit, but I thought you might be interested."

## What "bad" looks like (sourced kill list)

These were specifically flagged in our research sources as cadence-smell triggers:

- "Hey [Name], hope this finds you well..."
- "I came across your profile and was really impressed..."
- "Love your content" / "Love the shit you're doing" (CreatorFlow flagged as the #1 "tells me you copy-pasted" line)
- "Bro" (specifically called out as a kill-on-sight word for cold DMs to women)
- "Did I catch you at a bad time?" (Martal Group 2026: lowest converter in their data)
- "How are you today?" (same)
- "I'm just calling to check in" / "I wanted to follow up" / "Just want to circle back"
- "Are you the decision-maker?" / "Do you have budget for this?"
- "I know you're busy, but..."
- "Let me know..." / "I'd be happy to..."
- "Does that make sense?" / "Do you know what we do?"
- "We are the #1 provider..."
- Generic "your work is truly inspiring"

Full banned list with citations: `references/what-not-to-do.md`.

## Note on archetypes we considered but couldn't source

**Loom video DM stats:** Anecdotally referenced widely in the outbound community, but **specific reply-rate data is not in our 77-source corpus**. Loom is still likely a high-leverage move for top 20 prospects/week (intuition: video shows effort + can't be ignored), but I won't quote a "3-5x lift" claim because it isn't sourced. Treat as experimental, measure your own results.

**Permission-based openers ("Open to a stupid idea?"):** Documented at 11.18% success rate for COLD CALLS (Gong's 300M+ call analysis, via Martal Group), but **NOT documented for cold DMs** in our sources. Likely transfers — but I'm not citing a DM stat that doesn't exist. Use cautiously.

**"Anti-pitch" reply rates:** Sources note these "yield high conversion rates with skeptical senior buyers" but specific numbers for DM-channel are not provided. The template is documented (Josh Braun, verbatim above); the stat isn't.
