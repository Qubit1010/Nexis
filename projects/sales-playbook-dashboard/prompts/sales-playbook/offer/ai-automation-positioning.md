# AI Automation Positioning — The Premium Wedge

> **Not directly sourced from NotebookLM research** — this file is positioning specific to NexusPoint. It applies the Hormozi Value Equation (`frameworks/hormozi-value-equation.md`) to Aleem's actual services from `context/work.md`.

## The strategic shift

**From:** "We build websites and do AI automation on the side." (Current revenue mix — 70% web, 10% AI.)

**To:** "We build AI workflows that take manual ops off founder plates. Web is the upsell."

This isn't a rebrand — it's an outreach positioning shift. The web work still happens. But every cold message, every DM, every call leads with AI automation because:

1. **Higher margin** — automation work prices to outcome (hours saved, $ unlocked), not to feature count
2. **Less commoditized** — every freelancer sells websites; few sell credible AI workflows
3. **Defensible** — AI builds compound (workflow audits → improvements → new workflows), websites are one-and-done
4. **Better cold positioning** — "we take 10 hrs/week off your plate" outperforms "we build websites" as a cold opener
5. **Door-opener for the rest** — clients who buy AI automation later buy web, not the reverse

## The one-liner

Use this verbatim in every cold opener that needs a one-line description:

> **"We build AI workflows that take the manual ops work off founders' plates — 10+ hours per week back, usually inside 14 days."**

This line passes the Hormozi check:
- **Dream outcome:** "10+ hours per week back" (specific, quantified)
- **Perceived likelihood:** "usually inside 14 days" (concrete timeline)
- **Time delay:** 14 days (short)
- **Effort & sacrifice:** "off founders' plates" (implies DFY)

## The 3 sub-offers (productized, named)

Rather than custom-quoting every prospect, lead with 3 productized offers. Pick whichever matches the prospect's pain.

### Offer 1: The Ops Teardown (free → wedge)

**What it is:** A 20-minute Zoom screen-share where you:
- Look at the prospect's tool stack
- Identify the #1 highest-leverage workflow to automate first
- Give them the build plan, free, even if they don't hire you

**Why it works:**
- Frees you from "free consult" framing — it's a teardown (deliverable, not a pitch)
- Sourced as the highest-converting call-ask anchor (`scripts/discovery-call-script.md`)
- Gives every prospect VALUE on the first interaction → builds reputation even from "no" prospects

**Price:** Free.

**Position as:** "I'll spend 20 min looking at your stack and tell you the first thing I'd automate. You decide if it's worth building."

### Offer 2: The Workflow Sprint (entry product — $1,500-3,000)

**What it is:** Build ONE specific AI workflow in 14 days. Scoped to a single manual pain.

**Examples:**
- Auto-route inbound leads from Instagram DMs → CRM → Slack channel + first-response template
- Daily content brief generated from 5 RSS feeds → summarized + scored → posted to Notion
- Client-onboarding workflow: contract signed → Drive folder created → onboarding doc generated → welcome email queued
- Sales-call recordings → transcript → action items → follow-up email draft

**Deliverable:**
- The built workflow (in n8n / Make / Python — wherever fits)
- 60-min handoff Loom walkthrough
- 30-day monitoring + fixes

**Price:** $1,500-3,000 depending on stack complexity.

**Position as:** "We pick one specific workflow that's eating your week, build it in 14 days, and hand you a system that runs forever."

### Offer 3: The AI Ops Retainer (recurring — $3,000-8,000/month)

**What it is:** Ongoing AI workflow buildout + monitoring. The "we're your AI ops team" play.

**Includes:**
- Monthly workflow build (one new automation per month, prioritized by Aleem + client together)
- Monitoring of all existing workflows
- Fixes / iteration on existing workflows
- Quarterly "Ops Teardown" reviews to spot new automation opportunities
- Slack channel for ongoing requests

**Price:** $3,000-8,000/month based on workflow count + complexity.

**Position as:** "We build a new high-leverage automation every month, monitor everything we've built, and act as your AI ops team without the headcount."

## The two-yes price drop (sourced from Hormozi)

On a discovery call, never offer one option. Always two:

| Tier | Price | Frame |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 — Done-With-You (Workflow Sprint) | $1,500-3,000 | "We build the workflow, you handle ongoing tweaks." |
| Tier 2 — Done-For-You (AI Ops Retainer first month) | $3,000-8,000 | "We build and monitor — you don't touch it." |

Most prospects pick Tier 2 once they hear Tier 1 — anchoring effect (Cialdini). And the prospects who pick Tier 1 often upgrade to Tier 2 within 60 days as soon as they see the first workflow working.

## How web fits (the upsell, not the lead)

Web doesn't disappear — it just stops being the cold opener.

**The flow:**
1. Cold reach → "We build AI workflows" → Discovery call → Sell Tier 1 or 2
2. Once retained, during a Quarterly Teardown: "While we've been automating your ops, I've been looking at your site — there's a conversion gap on the [specific page] that's costing you ~$X/month. Want us to fix it as a one-off?"
3. Web becomes a $3-10k add-on to an existing client relationship, not a standalone deal.

Net effect: same web revenue, but at higher margin (warm upsell vs cold quote) and with retainer revenue layered underneath.

## What NOT to lead with

Don't open with:
- "We build websites" → commoditized, low signal
- "We do digital marketing" → vague, low credibility
- "We're a digital agency" → matches 50,000 cold DMs/day
- "We do data analysis" → narrow audience
- "We help businesses grow" → unfalsifiable, immediate delete
- "We integrate Shopify with X" → too tactical, doesn't anchor on dream outcome

**Test your opener:** Read it back. Could it match 100 other agencies? If yes → kill it.

Two — I'm seeing teams that move first on this clawing back 10+ hrs/week, and the ones that wait are watching competitors lap them. Wanted to see where you sit."

(This is a credibility builder + urgency anchor in one move.)
