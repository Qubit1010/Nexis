# Worked Example — Instagram Cold DM to Closed Client

> A full end-to-end walkthrough of one Instagram prospect: pre-warm → cold DM sequence → live conversation → booked call → 30-min Ops Teardown discovery call → signed contract. Uses the IG cold sequence (`scripts/instagram-cold-dm-sequence.md`), the live conversation flow (`scripts/live-conversation-playbook.md`), the discovery script (`scripts/discovery-call-script.md`), and real proof-bank entries (`offer/proof-bank.md` — Steve/Andrey/Mikey).
>
> Companion to `worked-example-linkedin.md`. The cold layer differs by channel; the discovery call and close are identical.

---

## The Prospect

**Marco Reyes** — runs **@marcobuildsbrands**, a personal-brand + Shopify DTC consultancy (solo + 2 contractors). Recent reel: a rant about spending "every Sunday night manually pulling sales numbers from Shopify into a spreadsheet to send client reports." Stack from his content: Shopify + Klaviyo + Google Sheets + ChatGPT. Maps to Andrey's proof (multi-tool ops + client reporting).

---

## STAGE 0 — Pre-Warm (Days 1–4, the missing step)

Instagram punishes cold DMs. Before any message, warm the account (improves delivery 40–50%):

- **Day 1:** Follow him. Like his last 2 posts.
- **Day 2:** Leave one substantive comment on the Sunday-reporting reel, not "great post." Something like: *"the sunday-night spreadsheet ritual is so real. the recon step is the worst part of it."*
- **Day 4:** He's now seen your profile 3x. You're a familiar face, not a cold account.

Mark him **"Warming – Day 1"** in the CRM, then **"Warm – ready"** on Day 4.

---

## STAGE 1 — The Cold DM Sequence (IG-native)

**Touch 1 (Day 4) — The Observation.** Archetype 1/4 blend, lowercase, contractions, under 100 words, no ask, no sign-off.

> yo marco, that reel on the sunday-night shopify recon hit home
>
> that exact thing is what we automate for DTC founders. just killed it for another guy on shopify + klaviyo, got his whole sunday back
>
> wanted to flag it's solvable. no ask

No reply yet. **Touch 2 (Day 7) — Value Add, still no ask:**

> marco - quick value drop, no catch
>
> here's a 90-sec loom of how we wired shopify → auto-generated client report → no spreadsheet at all: [loom link]
>
> figured it'd save you the research even if we never talk

He replies an hour later:

> ok this is sick. how much does something like this run?

*Price question early — don't quote. This is buying behavior, route to the live playbook.*

## STAGE 2 — The Middle Conversation (live playbook + speed-to-reply)

**Reply within 1–5 min** (IG: +391% conversion vs delayed). Price-curious early = use the deflect.

**Phase 1 — Qualify / deflect price:**

> depends what you'd actually need, that's why i don't quote blind
>
> quick one first - the sunday recon, is that the only manual reporting bit or is the whole client-update process by hand right now?

> honestly the whole thing. pulling numbers, formatting, writing the summary, sending to like 6 clients

**Phase 2 — Label, then silence:**

> sounds like client reporting has quietly turned into a part-time job you didn't sign up for

*(send, wait)*

> lol yeah. it's why i can't take on more than 6 clients tbh

*(Same floodgate as the LinkedIn example — the real pain is capped growth, not the hours.)*

**Phase 3 — Deepen:**

> if you put a number on it - how many hours a week does the full reporting cycle eat across you + the contractors?

> me alone probably 8-10. sundays mostly

> and you said 6 clients is the ceiling. what's a 7th retainer worth if you could take it?

> like $2-3k/month

**Phase 4 — Proof (he already pulled at Touch 2, reinforce with the matching peer):**

> yeah this is the exact wedge. built the multi-tool reporting + ops automation for andrey (agency operator, similar scattered-tools setup, shopify-adjacent). took 10+ hrs/week off his plate. the loom i sent is basically a slice of that same build

**Phase 6 — Warm Ask → Call** (anchored deliverable, two times):

> easier to show than type. want a 20-min ops teardown? i screen-share your shopify + klaviyo + sheets flow and tell you the exact first thing i'd automate. you decide if it's worth building
>
> tuesday 2pm or thursday 11am your time?

> thursday works

Booked. **Send the 5-question pre-call form** in the DM as a simple link, low-friction.

## STAGE 3 — The Discovery Call (same 30-min Ops Teardown)

The call script doesn't change by channel — it's the same 4 Acts. Compressed here since the structure mirrors the LinkedIn example; the *content* differs because Marco's pain is reporting/recon, not content rewriting.

**Act I — UFC:** Same frame. Price anchored at "$2k to $6k depending on scope." Two no-oriented questions ("is now a bad time?" / "does that seem like a bad idea?").

**Act II — Pain Funnel (the 8 questions):** Surfaces that the Sunday recon has run for ~18 months, he tried a Zapier setup that broke, and when pushed on cost he lands on it himself:

> between my sundays and the 7th client I keep turning down, probably $4-5k a month I'm leaving on the table

*Label:* "so every month this runs, it's basically one extra retainer you can't say yes to."

**Act III — Need-Payoff + Mistake ABC:**

> if reporting ran itself, what changes? → "I take the 7th and 8th client. easy."

Mistake ABC, peer-matched to Andrey:

> the mistake i see DTC operators make is they bolt a zapier zap onto a broken process, so it breaks the second a client changes their format. we map every step first, kill the ones that shouldn't exist, then automate what's left. that's why andrey's reporting build has held instead of breaking like your zap did.

*(Pre-kills "I tried Zapier and it broke" before he raises it.)*

**Act IV — Close:**

- 3-step integration plan (Week 1 map, Weeks 2-3 build, Week 4 live + 30-day monitor)
- Double tie-down: *"how do you feel about the process, feel like it's what you need?"*
- Price tied to his number, two tiers:

> against the $4-5k/month you're leaving on the table - Tier 1 Done-With-You is $2k, Tier 2 Done-For-You we build and run it, live in 14 days, $4,500. pays for itself in about 3 weeks.

*(Silence.)*

> let's do the done-for-you one

- Risk reversal: live in 14 days or you don't pay till it is; 5+ hrs/week back in 30 days or full refund, keep the build.
- No-oriented close: *"would it be a bad idea to kick off Monday?"* → "no, let's do it."

## LinkedIn vs Instagram — what actually changed

| Dimension | LinkedIn (Sarah) | Instagram (Marco) |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-work | Connection request | **3-5 day pre-warm** (follow + engage) before any DM |
| Voice | Professional-peer, standard caps | Lowercase, contractions, "yo", no sign-off |
| Opener | Archetype 2 in a 280-char note | Archetype 1/4 blend, broken into short lines |
| Cadence | DM 2/3/4 over ~2 weeks | Tighter: Touch 1 Day 4, Touch 2 Day 7 |
| Speed-to-reply | Important | **Critical** (+391% if under 1 min) |
| Price-early handling | Deflect to free Teardown | Same deflect, but he asked at Touch 2 (faster tempo) |
| Proof match | Steve (content/social agency) | Andrey (multi-tool ops + reporting) |
| **Discovery call** | **Identical 4-Act script** | **Identical 4-Act script** |

The cold layer changes by channel. The conversation and the close don't — once they're on the call, it's the same Ops Teardown either way.
