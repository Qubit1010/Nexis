# which US cities and states have the highest concentration of real estate agencies and brokerages 2026 hottest real estate markets by number of agents

*mode: general | depth: medium | 2026-07-14*

---

## Answer

In 2026, Northeast states like New York and New Jersey, and coastal cities like San Francisco, have the highest concentration of real estate agencies and brokerages. These areas remain competitive with high demand.

## Summary
The highest concentration of real estate agencies and brokerages in the U.S. is found primarily in states like Florida and cities such as Los Angeles Metro Area. The 2026 hottest real estate markets by number of agents and activity remain concentrated in high-demand, fast-growing regions including coastal metros and Sun Belt states that continue to attract real estate professionals.

## Key Findings
1. Florida has the most Realtors of any state, with 216,182 agents, and Vermont has the fewest [1].
2. The Los Angeles Metropolitan Area holds the highest count of employed real estate sales agents among U.S. cities [1].
3. Sun Belt states like South Carolina, Oklahoma, Florida, and Georgia have some of the highest per capita concentrations of real estate agents, buoyed by consistent population growth and market activity [3].
4. The 2026 hottest real estate markets are mostly in high-cost coastal metros such as San Francisco, and in Northeast states, characterized by strong competition, low inventory, and active bidding [2][6][7].
5. Dallas/Fort Worth leads all markets as a Top Primary Market in real estate activity for 2026, maintaining its leading status with robust agent presence and market development [8].
6. Overall, the national total comprises about 1,489,932 Realtors and 102,116 real estate brokerages, meaning about 14.6 Realtors per brokerage on average [1].

## Detail
Analysis of Realtor concentrations shows Florida dominating the market with the highest number of agents (216,182), attributed to its rapid population growth and attractive housing markets [1]. At the city level, the Los Angeles Metropolitan Area has been identified as the single largest hub of real estate agents in the U.S., indicating not just a large market but also a high density of real estate professionals [1].

Beyond just raw numbers, examining per capita concentrations shows the strength of real estate industries in Sun Belt states like South Carolina, Oklahoma, Florida, and Georgia. These markets exhibit sustained population and economic growth, encouraging brokerage establishments and agent employment [3]. This regional trend aligns with overall demographic shifts favoring warmer climates and more affordable housing markets.

Regarding the hottest real estate markets of 2026, sources identify high-demand coastal metros like San Francisco as well as Northeast markets with high bidding activity and low inventory, signaling competitive landscapes for agents [2][6][7]. Dallas/Fort Worth emerges as a standout Primary Market for 2026, not only in terms of sales metrics but also in maintaining the largest concentration of real estate activity among emerging and established markets [8]. This metro’s consistent growth and affordability continue to attract real estate professionals.

The nationwide scale includes nearly 1.5 million Realtors across more than 100,000 brokerages, yielding an average of roughly 15 agents per brokerage, suggesting a moderately concentrated yet fragmented agent structure [1].
