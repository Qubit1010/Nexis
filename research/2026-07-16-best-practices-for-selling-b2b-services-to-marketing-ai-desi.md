  - Frame your pitch around organizational alignment, not just executional capacity, since misalignment is a structural blocker in B2B advertising/marketing adoption. This is a reliable lever for both initial conversion and ongoing retention. [8]

  - Agencies evaluate white‑label options on pricing, margins, and vendor red flags; 2026 guidance explicitly compares solutions and outlines a playbook for launching B2B outreach under a white label. - Positioning and evaluation criteria
  - Buyers emphasize what actually matters when selecting B2B marketing partners. Map your positioning to those criteria—credibility, specialization, and outcomes—so agencies can justify the choice internally on logical, financial grounds. [5][4]  
  - Anchor claims to strategy and KPIs (as recommended in 2026 guides) to meet agencies’ expectations for measurable performance. [1]

- Pricing approach
  - For white‑label and A2A deals, price in a way that supports agency margin while aligning to their financial logic and stability concerns. While no numeric benchmarks are provided, the need to compare pricing/margins and avoid vendor red flags is explicit. [7][4]  
  - Tie pricing and packaging to KPIs and outcomes that agencies track (per the strategy/KPI focus), reinforcing value realization. [1]

- Common objections and how to preempt them
  - Organizational misalignment is a central issue in B2B advertising; objections often stem from structural fit rather than pure execution—address alignment in discovery and proposals. [8]  
  - A robust sales strategy (process, messaging, qualification) is necessary to surface and handle objections effectively, rather than relying on product/service merit alone. [6]  
  - Because buyers focus on financial logic and stability, anticipate pushback on ROI clarity, risk, and continuity; counter with specialized content and data‑driven targeting that demonstrate long‑term value. [4]

- Execution enablers
  - Combine persona‑by‑role messaging and timing (signal intelligence) with a KPI‑anchored strategy and modern SEO/content approaches to reach and convert agencies that evaluate partners rigorously. [2][1][3]  
  - Maintain fit-for-purpose proof (case outcomes, metrics) and clear pricing/margins for white‑label to satisfy what buyers say actually matters. [5][7]
